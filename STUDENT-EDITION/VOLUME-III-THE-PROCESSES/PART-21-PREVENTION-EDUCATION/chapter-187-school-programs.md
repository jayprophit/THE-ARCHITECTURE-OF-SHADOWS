# Chapter 187: School-Based Prevention

## Drug Education in Schools

School-based programs are a primary prevention strategy:

**Program Types:**
- **DARE**: Drug Abuse Resistance Education
- **Life Skills Training**: Life skills training programs
- **Social Norms Programs**: Programs correcting misperceptions about drug use
- **Interactive Programs**: Interactive prevention programs
- **Peer-Led Programs**: Peer-led prevention programs
- **Comprehensive Programs**: Comprehensive school-based programs

**Program Components:**
- **Drug Information**: Accurate information about drugs
- **Decision Making**: Decision-making skills
- **Resistance Skills**: Skills to resist peer pressure
- **Social Skills**: Social skills development
- **Self-Esteem**: Building self-esteem
- **Media Literacy**: Media literacy skills

**Evidence-Based Programs:**
- **Life Skills Training (LST)**: Evidence-based life skills program
- **Project STAR**: Evidence-based comprehensive program
- **Positive Action**: Evidence-based positive action program
- **Keepin' it REAL**: Evidence-based multicultural program
- **Other Evidence-Based Programs**: Various other evidence-based programs
- **Program Effectiveness**: Evidence-based programs show effectiveness

**Challenges:**
- **Implementation Fidelity**: Maintaining program fidelity
- **Teacher Training**: Teacher training for program delivery
- **Program Duration**: Sufficient program duration
- **Booster Sessions**: Booster sessions to reinforce learning
- **Cultural Adaptation**: Cultural adaptation of programs
- **Resource Constraints**: Resource constraints for implementation

**The School Prevention Reality:**
- **Widely Implemented**: Widely implemented in schools
- **Mixed Results**: Mixed results from programs
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based programs most effective
- **Interactive**: Interactive approaches most effective
- **Multi-Component**: Multi-component programs most effective
- **Long-Term**: Long-term commitment required
