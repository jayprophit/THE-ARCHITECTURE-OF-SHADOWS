# Chapter 188: Family-Based Prevention

## Family Interventions for Drug Prevention

Family-based prevention programs strengthen family protective factors:

**Program Types:**
- **Family Skills Training**: Training for parenting skills
- **Family Therapy**: Family therapy approaches
- **Home Visitation**: Home visitation programs
- **Family Support**: Family support programs
- **Multisystemic Therapy**: Multisystemic family therapy
- **Strengthening Families**: Strengthening families programs

**Protective Factors:**
- **Parental Monitoring**: Parental monitoring of children
- **Family Bonding**: Strong family bonds
- **Clear Rules**: Clear family rules and expectations
- **Parental Involvement**: Parental involvement in children's lives
- **Positive Reinforcement**: Positive reinforcement for good behaviour
- **Family Communication**: Open family communication

**Risk Factors Addressed:**
- **Family Conflict**: Reducing family conflict
- **Parental Substance Use**: Addressing parental substance use
- **Poor Parenting**: Improving parenting skills
- **Family Trauma**: Addressing family trauma
- **Economic Stress**: Addressing economic stress on families
- **Mental Health**: Addressing family mental health issues

**Evidence-Based Programs:**
- **Strengthening Families Program**: Evidence-based family program
- **Family Check-Up**: Evidence-based family intervention
- **Multisystemic Therapy**: Evidence-based therapy for at-risk youth
- **Nurse-Family Partnership**: Evidence-based home visitation program
- **Other Programs**: Various other evidence-based programs
- **Program Effectiveness**: Evidence-based programs show effectiveness

**The Family Prevention Reality:**
- **Important**: Family is important protective factor
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based programs effective
- **Early Intervention**: Early family intervention most effective
- **Multi-Component**: Multi-component approaches most effective
- **Cultural Adaptation**: Cultural adaptation important
- **Long-Term**: Long-term family support needed
