# Chapter 117: The Metric System

## Standardised Measurement

The metric system provides standardised drug measurements:

**Metric Units:**
- **Gram (g)**: Base unit for drug measurement
- **Kilogram (kg)**: 1,000 grams
- **Milligram (mg)**: 0.001 grams
- **Microgram (μg)**: 0.000001 grams

**Common Drug Quantities:**
- **Gram**: Common unit for street-level sales
- **Eighth**: 3.5 grams (eighth of an ounce)
- **Quarter**: 7 grams (quarter of an ounce)
- **Half Ounce**: 14 grams
- **Ounce**: 28 grams
- **Kilogram**: 1,000 grams (wholesale unit)

**Metric Advantages:**
- **Standardisation**: International standard
- **Precision**: Precise measurements
- **Scalability**: Easily scalable
- **Universal**: Universally understood
- **Legal**: Legal system uses metric
- **Scientific**: Scientific standard

**Metric in Drug Trade:**
- **International Trade**: International drug trade uses metric
- **Wholesale**: Wholesale transactions in kilograms
- **Retail**: Retail transactions in grams
- **Precise Dosing**: Precise dosing for potent substances
- **Quality Control**: Quality control uses metric
- **Legal Proceedings**: Legal proceedings use metric

**The Metric Reality:**
- **Global Standard**: Global standard for drug measurement
- **Precision**: Enables precise measurement
- **Universal**: Universally understood
- **Legal Basis**: Legal basis for drug charges
- **Scientific**: Scientific approach to measurement
- **Essential**: Essential for accurate measurement
