# Chapter 147: Communication Methods

## How Drug Trade Participants Communicate

Drug trade participants use various communication methods:

**Traditional Methods:**
- **Face-to-Face**: Face-to-face meetings
- **Pay Phones**: Disposable pay phones
- **Burner Phones**: Disposable mobile phones
- **Pagers**: Pager communication
- **Written Notes**: Written notes and codes
- **Dead Drops**: Dead drops for messages

**Digital Methods:**
- **Encrypted Messaging**: Encrypted messaging apps
- **Social Media**: Social media platforms
- **Email**: Encrypted email services
- **VoIP**: Voice over IP services
- **Video Calls**: Encrypted video calls
- **Dark Web Forums**: Dark web forum communication

**Encrypted Communication:**
- **Signal**: Encrypted messaging app
- **WhatsApp**: Encrypted messaging (with concerns)
- **Telegram**: Encrypted messaging
- **PGP Encryption**: PGP encryption for email
- **End-to-End Encryption**: End-to-end encrypted communication
- **Self-Destructing Messages**: Self-destructing message features

**Code and Slang:**
- **Drug Slang**: Slang for drugs and transactions
- **Code Words**: Code words for activities
- **Numeric Codes**: Numeric codes for quantities
- **Symbolic Communication**: Symbolic communication methods
- **Evolving Codes**: Codes constantly evolving
- **Regional Variations**: Regional variations in codes

**The Communication Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential for drug trade operations
- **Adaptive**: Constantly adapting to surveillance
- **Encrypted**: Increasingly encrypted communication
- **Sophisticated**: Sophisticated communication methods
- **Risk**: Risk of interception and monitoring
- **Cat and Mouse**: Constant cat and mouse with law enforcement
