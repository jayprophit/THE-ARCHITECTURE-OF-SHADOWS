# Chapter 154: Operational Security

## Protecting Operations from Detection

Operational security (OPSEC) protects drug trade operations from detection:

**OPSEC Principles:**
- **Need to Know**: Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- **Compartmentalisation**: Compartmentalisation of information
- **Minimisation**: Minimisation of digital footprint
- **Diversification**: Diversification of methods and locations
- **Redundancy**: Redundancy in communication methods
- **Contingency Planning**: Contingency planning for compromise

**Digital OPSEC:**
- **Device Separation**: Separation of personal and operational devices
- **Secure Operating Systems**: Use of secure operating systems (Tails, etc.)
- **Encryption**: Encryption of all communications and data
- **VPN Use**: VPN use for internet access
- **No Personal Info**: No personal information on operational devices
- **Regular Wiping**: Regular wiping of devices

**Physical OPSEC:**
- **Location Variation**: Variation of meeting locations
- **Time Variation**: Variation of meeting times
- **Surveillance Awareness**: Awareness of potential surveillance
- **Route Variation**: Variation of travel routes
- **Counter-Surveillance**: Counter-surveillance techniques
- **Secure Locations**: Use of secure locations for sensitive activities

**Communication OPSEC:**
- **Encrypted Communication**: All communication encrypted
- **Code Language**: Use of codes and slang
- **Minimal Communication**: Minimal communication to reduce exposure
- **Multiple Methods**: Multiple communication methods for redundancy
- **Regular Rotation**: Regular rotation of communication methods
- **No Sensitive Info**: No sensitive information in unencrypted channels

**The OPSEC Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential for avoiding detection
- **Complex**: Complex set of practices
- **Constant**: Constant vigilance required
- **Vulnerable**: Vulnerable to mistakes
- **Professional**: Professional OPSEC requires expertise
- **Cat and Mouse**: Constant cat and mouse with law enforcement
