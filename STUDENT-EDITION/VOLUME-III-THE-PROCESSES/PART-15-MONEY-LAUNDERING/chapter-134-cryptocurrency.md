# Chapter 134: Cryptocurrency Money Laundering

## Digital Assets for Money Laundering

Cryptocurrency provides new methods for money laundering:

**Cryptocurrency Methods:**
- **Conversion**: Converting drug money to cryptocurrency
- **Mixing Services**: Mixing services to obscure transaction trails
- **Privacy Coins**: Privacy-focused cryptocurrencies
- **Decentralised Exchanges**: Decentralised exchanges without KYC
- **Peer-to-Peer Transactions**: Direct peer-to-peer transactions
- **Cross-Border Transfers**: Rapid cross-border transfers

**Mixing Services:**
- **Tumblers**: Services that mix cryptocurrency transactions
- **Mixers**: Services that mix coins from multiple users
- **Privacy Enhancement**: Enhanced privacy through mixing
- **Fee-Based**: Fee-based mixing services
- **Anonymity**: Increased anonymity through mixing
- **Detection Challenge**: Challenging to trace mixed transactions

**Privacy Coins:**
- **Monero**: Privacy-focused cryptocurrency
- **Zcash**: Privacy-focused cryptocurrency
- **Dash**: Privacy features in cryptocurrency
- **Stealth Addresses**: Stealth addresses hiding transaction details
- **Ring Signatures**: Ring signatures hiding transaction sources
- **Zero-Knowledge Proofs**: Zero-knowledge proofs hiding transaction details

**Decentralised Exchanges:**
- **No KYC**: No know-your-customer requirements
- **Peer-to-Peer**: Direct peer-to-peer trading
- **Anonymous Trading**: Anonymous trading of cryptocurrencies
- **Global Access**: Global access to trading
- **Multiple Cryptocurrencies**: Trading between multiple cryptocurrencies
- **Regulatory Challenge**: Regulatory challenge for enforcement

**The Cryptocurrency Reality:**
- **Emerging Threat**: Emerging threat for money laundering
- **Innovative**: Innovative money laundering methods
- **Challenging**: Challenging for law enforcement
- **Global**: Global scope complicates regulation
- **Adaptive**: Constantly adapting to regulation
- **Regulatory Response**: Increasing regulatory response
