# Chapter 135: Real Estate Money Laundering

## Property-Based Money Laundering

Real estate is a major vehicle for money laundering:

**Methods:**
- **Cash Purchases**: Cash purchases of property
- **Shell Company Purchases**: Shell companies purchasing property
- **Offshore Buyers**: Offshore entities purchasing property
- **Overvaluation**: Paying above market value to move money
- **Rapid Flipping**: Buying and quickly selling property
- **Rental Income**: Using rental income to legitimise drug money

**Cash Purchases:**
- **All-Cash Transactions**: All-cash property purchases
- **No Mortgage**: No mortgage financing
- **Quick Transactions**: Quick closing to avoid scrutiny
- **Multiple Properties**: Purchasing multiple properties
- **Geographic Spread**: Properties in multiple locations
- **Luxury Properties**: Focus on luxury properties

**Shell Company Purchases:**
- **Corporate Ownership**: Property owned by shell companies
- **Hidden Ownership**: True ownership hidden behind corporate structures
- **Offshore Companies**: Offshore companies purchasing property
- **Nominee Directors**: Nominee directors as apparent owners
- **Complex Structures**: Complex corporate structures
- **International Properties**: International property holdings

**Overvaluation:**
- **Above Market Value**: Paying above market value
- **Justification**: Various justifications for overvaluation
- **Seller Cooperation**: Seller cooperation in overvaluation
- **Commission**: Higher commissions for agents
- **Appraisal Fraud**: Fraudulent appraisals
- **Money Movement**: Moving money through overvaluation

**The Real Estate Reality:**
- **Major Vehicle**: Major vehicle for money laundering
- **High Value**: High-value transactions move large amounts
- **Global**: Global real estate market
- **Challenging**: Challenging to detect
- **Professional**: Professional expertise required
- **Regulatory Response**: Increasing regulatory response
