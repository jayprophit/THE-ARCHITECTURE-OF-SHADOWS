# Chapter 180: Medication-Assisted Treatment

## Pharmacological Treatment of Addiction

Medication-assisted treatment (MAT) uses medications to treat addiction:

**MAT for Opioid Addiction:**
- **Methadone**: Methadone maintenance treatment
- **Buprenorphine**: Buprenorphine treatment
- **Naltrexone**: Naltrexone for opioid addiction
- **Naloxone**: Naloxone for overdose reversal
- **Extended-Release Naltrexone**: Long-acting naltrexone
- **Combination Therapies**: Combination of medications

**MAT for Alcohol Addiction:**
- **Naltrexone**: Naltrexone for alcohol dependence
- **Acamprosate**: Acamprosate for alcohol dependence
- **Disulfiram**: Disulfiram for alcohol dependence
- **Baclofen**: Baclofen for alcohol dependence
- **Topiramate**: Topiramate for alcohol dependence
- **Combination Therapies**: Combination of medications

**MAT for Other Addictions:**
- **Nicotine Replacement**: Nicotine replacement therapy
- **Varenicline**: Varenicline for nicotine addiction
- **Bupropion**: Bupropion for nicotine addiction
- **No MAT for Stimulants**: No approved MAT for stimulant addiction
- **Research**: Ongoing research for other MAT options
- **Off-Label Use**: Off-label use of medications

**Benefits of MAT:**
- **Reduced Mortality**: Reduced mortality from overdose
- **Retention**: Improved treatment retention
- **Reduced Illicit Use**: Reduced illicit drug use
- **Improved Social Functioning**: Improved social functioning
- **Cost-Effective**: Cost-effective treatment approach
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base for effectiveness

**Challenges:**
- **Stigma**: Stigma against MAT
- **Access Barriers**: Limited access to MAT
- **Regulatory Barriers**: Regulatory barriers to MAT
- **Misconceptions**: Misconceptions about MAT
- **Cost**: Cost of medications
- **Duration**: Duration of treatment varies

**The MAT Reality:**
- **Effective**: Highly effective treatment approach
- **Underutilised**: Underutilised due to stigma and barriers
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving for opioid addiction
- **Controversial**: Controversial despite evidence
- **Expanding**: Expanding access to MAT
