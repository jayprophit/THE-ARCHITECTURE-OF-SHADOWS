# Chapter 172: Crop Eradication

## Destroying Drug Crops

Crop eradication targets drug-producing plants:

**Eradication Methods:**
- **Manual Eradication**: Manual removal of crops
- **Aerial Spraying**: Aerial spraying of herbicides
- **Mechanical Eradication**: Mechanical destruction of crops
- **Forced Substitution**: Forced substitution with other crops
- **Alternative Livelihoods**: Programs to provide alternative livelihoods
- **Community-Based**: Community-based eradication

**Aerial Spraying:**
- **Glyphosate**: Glyphosate herbicide spraying
- **Colombia**: Extensive aerial spraying in Colombia
- **Environmental Concerns**: Environmental and health concerns
- **Effectiveness**: Questions about effectiveness
- **Controversy**: Significant controversy over aerial spraying
- **Legal Challenges**: Legal challenges to aerial spraying

**Manual Eradication:**
- **Manual Removal**: Manual removal of crops by workers
- **Labor Intensive**: Labor intensive process
- **Dangerous**: Dangerous work due to armed groups
- **Slow**: Slow process compared to aerial spraying
- **Targeted**: More targeted than aerial spraying
- **Community Engagement**: Requires community engagement

**Alternative Livelihoods:**
- **Crop Substitution**: Substitution with legal crops
- **Economic Development**: Economic development programs
- **Infrastructure**: Infrastructure development
- **Market Access**: Market access for alternative crops
- **Education**: Education programs
- **Long-Term Commitment**: Requires long-term commitment

**The Eradication Reality:**
- **Limited Effectiveness**: Limited effectiveness in reducing drug supply
- **Displacement**: Displacement rather than elimination
- **Environmental Impact**: Significant environmental impact
- **Human Rights**: Human rights concerns
- **Economic Impact**: Economic impact on farmers
- **Need for Alternatives**: Need for viable alternatives
