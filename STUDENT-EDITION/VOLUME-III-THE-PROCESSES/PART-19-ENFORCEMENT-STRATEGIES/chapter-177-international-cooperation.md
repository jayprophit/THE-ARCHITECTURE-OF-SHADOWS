# Chapter 177: International Cooperation

International cooperation is essential for effective drug enforcement:

**International Organisations:**
- **UNODC**: United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime
- **INTERPOL**: International Criminal Police Organization
- **Europol**: European Union Agency for Law Enforcement Cooperation
- **DEA International**: DEA international operations
- **FBI International**: FBI international operations
- **Regional Organisations**: Regional drug enforcement organisations

**Cooperation Mechanisms:**
- **MLATs**: Mutual Legal Assistance Treaties
- **Extradition Treaties**: Extradition treaties for suspects
- **Joint Operations**: Joint law enforcement operations
- **Intelligence Sharing**: Intelligence sharing between agencies
- **Training Programs**: Training programs for international partners
- **Capacity Building**: Capacity building in affected countries

**Challenges:**
- **Jurisdictional Issues**: Jurisdictional challenges
- **Sovereignty Concerns**: Sovereignty concerns
- **Resource Disparities**: Resource disparities between countries
- **Corruption**: Corruption in partner agencies
- **Political Differences**: Political differences affecting cooperation
- **Trust Issues**: Trust issues between agencies

**Successes:**
- **Major Takedowns**: Successful international takedowns
- **Arrests**: International arrests and extraditions
- **Seizures**: Major international seizures
- **Intelligence**: Successful intelligence sharing
- **Capacity Building**: Successful capacity building
- **Network Disruption**: Disruption of international networks

**The International Cooperation Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential for effective enforcement
- **Complex**: Complex diplomatic and operational challenges
- **Resource Intensive**: Resource intensive operations
- **Political**: Political considerations affect cooperation
- **Variable**: Variable effectiveness across partnerships
