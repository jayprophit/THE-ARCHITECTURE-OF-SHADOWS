# Chapter 196: Cannabis Regulation

## Evolving Approaches to Cannabis Control

Cannabis regulation is rapidly evolving globally:

**Prohibition Model:**
- **Criminalisation**: Criminal penalties for cannabis
- **Zero Tolerance**: Zero tolerance approach
- **Enforcement Focus**: Focus on law enforcement
- **Limited Medical Use**: Limited or no medical cannabis
- **High Arrest Rates**: High arrest rates for cannabis offenses
- **Racial Disparities**: Racial disparities in enforcement

**Medical Cannabis:**
- **Medical Programs**: Medical cannabis programs
- **Patient Access**: Patient access to medical cannabis
- **Regulated Markets**: Regulated medical cannabis markets
- **Physician Recommendation**: Physician recommendation required
- **Quality Control**: Quality control of medical cannabis
- **Insurance Coverage**: Limited insurance coverage

**Adult-Use Legalisation:**
- **Uruguay**: First country to legalise cannabis
- **Canada**: Legal cannabis market
- **US States**: State-level legalisation
- **Thailand**: Cannabis legalisation
- **Other Countries**: Emerging legalisation efforts
- **Regulated Markets**: Regulated adult-use markets

**Decriminalisation:**
- **Portugal**: Decriminalisation model
- **Other Countries**: Various decriminalisation approaches
- **Civil Penalties**: Civil instead of criminal penalties
- **Focus on Treatment**: Focus on treatment over punishment
- **Reduced Arrests**: Reduced cannabis arrests
- **Social Justice**: Social justice considerations

**Regulatory Challenges:**
- **Age Restrictions**: Age restrictions for purchase
- **Potency Limits**: Potency limits on products
- **Advertising Restrictions**: Restrictions on advertising
- **Taxation**: Cannabis taxation
- **Public Consumption**: Regulation of public consumption
- **Driving Under Influence**: Driving under cannabis influence

**The Cannabis Regulation Reality:**
- **Rapidly Evolving**: Rapidly evolving globally

- **Economic Impact**: Significant economic impact
- **Public Health**: Public health considerations
- **Social Justice**: Social justice implications
