# Chapter 193: Drug Scheduling

## Classification of Controlled Substances

Drug scheduling systems classify substances by their potential for abuse and medical use:

**US Scheduling:**
- **Schedule I**: No accepted medical use, high abuse potential
- **Schedule II**: Accepted medical use, high abuse potential
- **Schedule III**: Accepted medical use, moderate abuse potential
- **Schedule IV**: Accepted medical use, low abuse potential
- **Schedule V**: Accepted medical use, very low abuse potential
- **Controlled Substances Act**: Controlled Substances Act framework

**UK Classification:**
- **Class A**: Most harmful drugs (heroin, cocaine, MDMA)
- **Class B**: Less harmful drugs (cannabis, amphetamines)
- **Class C**: Least harmful drugs (some prescription drugs)
- **Misuse of Drugs Act**: Misuse of Drugs Act framework
- **Penalties**: Penalties based on classification

**UN Scheduling:**
- **Schedule I**: Strictest control
- **Schedule II**: Strict control with medical use
- **Schedule III**: Less strict control
- **Schedule IV**: Minimal control
- **Psychotropic Convention**: Psychotropic substances scheduling
- **International Control**: International control framework

**Scheduling Criteria:**
- **Abuse Potential**: Potential for abuse
- **Medical Use**: Accepted medical use
- **Safety**: Safety under medical supervision
- **Dependence Potential**: Potential for physical or psychological dependence
- **International Considerations**: International scheduling considerations
- **Scientific Evidence**: Scientific evidence for scheduling decisions
