# Chapter 158: Violence Overview

## The Role of Violence in the Drug Trade

Violence is endemic to the illicit drug trade:

**Types of Violence:**
- **Territorial Violence**: Violence over control of territories
- **Market Violence**: Violence over market share
- **Disciplinary Violence**: Violence to enforce discipline
- **Retaliatory Violence**: Violence in retaliation for perceived wrongs
- **State Violence**: Violence by state actors
- **Collateral Violence**: Violence affecting bystanders

**Causes of Violence:**
- **Profit Motive**: High profits drive violent competition
- **Lack of Legal Recourse**: No legal recourse for disputes
- **Territorial Control**: Competition over territories
- **Market Control**: Competition over market share
- **Reputation**: Violence as reputation building
- **State Enforcement**: Response to state enforcement

**Scale of Violence:**
- **Homicides**: Drug-related homicides
- **Assaults**: Drug-related assaults
- **Torture**: Torture in drug trade
- **Disappearances**: Forced disappearances
- **Massacres**: Massacres in drug-related conflicts
- **Civilian Casualties**: Civilian casualties of drug violence

**Geographic Patterns:**
- **Producer Countries**: High violence in drug-producing countries
- **Transit Countries**: High violence in transit countries
- **Consumer Countries**: Variable violence in consumer countries
- **Border Regions**: High violence in border regions
- **Urban Areas**: Concentrated violence in urban areas
- **Rural Areas**: Violence in rural production areas

**The Violence Reality:**
- **Endemic**: Endemic to illicit drug trade
- **Prohibition-Related**: Exacerbated by prohibition
- **Profit-Driven**: Driven by profit motives
- **Systemic**: Systemic problem of prohibition
- **Devastating**: Devastating impact on communities
- **Preventable**: Preventable through policy change
