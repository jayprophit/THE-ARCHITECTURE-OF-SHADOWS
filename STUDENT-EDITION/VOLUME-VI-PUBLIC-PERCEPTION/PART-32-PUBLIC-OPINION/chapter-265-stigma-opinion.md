# Chapter 265: Public Opinion and Stigma

## Stigma in Public Attitudes

Stigma toward drug users is reflected in public attitudes:

**Nature of Stigma:**
- **Moral Judgment**: Moral judgment of drug users
- **Blaming**: Blaming individuals for addiction
- **Dangerousness**: Perception of drug users as dangerous
- **Unreliability**: Perception of drug users as unreliable
- **Social Distance**: Desire for social distance from drug users
- **Dehumanisation**: Dehumanisation of drug users

**Sources of Stigma:**
- **Media Portrayals**: Media contributing to stigma
- **Political Rhetoric**: Political rhetoric contributing to stigma
- **Personal Experience**: Personal experience shaping stigma
- **Cultural Norms**: Cultural norms contributing to stigma
- **Religious Beliefs**: Religious beliefs contributing to stigma
- **Lack of Understanding**: Lack of understanding contributing to stigma

**Consequences of Stigma:**
- **Treatment Avoidance**: Avoidance of treatment due to stigma
- **Social Isolation**: Social isolation of drug users
- **Employment Discrimination**: Employment discrimination
- **Housing Discrimination**: Housing discrimination
- **Healthcare Barriers**: Barriers to healthcare access
- **Family Rejection**: Family rejection of drug users

**Stigma Reduction:**
- **Education**: Education to reduce stigma
- **Contact**: Contact with people in recovery
- **Language**: Language changes to reduce stigma
- **Media Campaigns**: Media campaigns to reduce stigma
- **Policy Reform**: Policy reform to reduce structural stigma
- **Advocacy**: Advocacy by people in recovery

**The Stigma Opinion Reality:**
- **Significant**: Significant stigma in public attitudes
- **Harmful**: Harmful consequences of stigma
- **Reducing**: Efforts to reduce stigma
- **Complex**: Complex sources of stigma
- **Changeable**: Stigma can be changed
- **Essential**: Stigma reduction essential for effective policy
