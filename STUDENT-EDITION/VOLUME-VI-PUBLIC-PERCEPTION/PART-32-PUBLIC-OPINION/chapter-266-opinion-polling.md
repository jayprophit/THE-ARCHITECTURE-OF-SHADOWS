# Chapter 266: Opinion Polling

## Measuring Public Opinion on Drugs

Opinion polling provides data on public attitudes toward drugs:

**Polling Methods:**
- **Telephone Polling**: Telephone surveys
- **Online Polling**: Online surveys
- **In-Person Polling**: In-person interviews
- **Focus Groups**: Focus group discussions
- **Panel Studies**: Longitudinal panel studies
- **Exit Polling**: Exit polling on policy votes

**Polling Challenges:**
- **Social Desirability Bias**: Bias due to social desirability
- **Question Wording**: Impact of question wording on responses
- **Sampling Issues**: Sampling challenges
- **Non-Response Bias**: Non-response bias
- **Timing**: Timing of polls affecting results
- **Interpretation**: Interpretation of polling data

**Key Polling Findings:**
- **Cannabis Support**: Majority support for cannabis legalisation
- **Treatment Support**: Strong support for treatment approaches
- **Enforcement Support**: Mixed support for enforcement
- **Harm Reduction Support**: Growing support for harm reduction
- **Stigma**: Evidence of stigma in polling responses
- **Demographic Variation**: Variation across demographic groups

**Polling Organizations:**
- **Gallup**: Gallup polling on drug issues
- **Pew Research**: Pew Research Center polling
- **Other Pollsters**: Other polling organizations
- **Academic Polling**: Academic polling on drug issues
- **Government Surveys**: Government surveys on drug attitudes
- **International Polling**: International polling organizations

**Using Polling Data:**
- **Policy Development**: Using polling in policy development
- **Campaign Strategy**: Using polling in campaign strategy
- **Public Education**: Using polling to identify education needs
- **Trend Analysis**: Analyzing trends in public opinion
- **Media Reporting**: Media reporting of polling data
- **Academic Research**: Academic research using polling data

**The Polling Reality:**
- **Valuable**: Valuable tool for understanding public opinion
- **Complex**: Complex methodology and interpretation
- **Influential**: Influential in policy debates
- **Variable**: Variable results based on methodology
- **Essential**: Essential for democratic policymaking
- **Evolving**: Evolving polling methods
