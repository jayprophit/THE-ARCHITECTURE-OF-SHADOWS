# Chapter 263: Public Opinion on Other Drugs

## Attitudes Toward Other Substances

Public opinion varies significantly across different substances:

**Opioids:**
- **Prescription Opioids**: Concerns about prescription opioid misuse
- **Heroin**: Strong opposition to heroin use
- **Opioid Crisis**: Public awareness of opioid crisis
- **Treatment Support**: Strong support for opioid treatment
- **Harm Reduction**: Growing support for harm reduction
- **Stigma**: Significant stigma toward opioid users

**Stimulants:**
- **Cocaine**: Strong opposition to cocaine use
- **Methamphetamine**: Strong opposition to methamphetamine use
- **ADHD Medications**: Acceptance of medical stimulant use
- **Prescription Stimulants**: Concerns about prescription stimulant misuse
- **Stigma**: Significant stigma toward stimulant users
- **Treatment Support**: Support for treatment

**Psychedelics:**
- **Historical Opposition**: Historical opposition to psychedelics
- **Therapeutic Interest**: Growing interest in therapeutic use
- **Medical Research**: Support for medical research
- **Legalisation**: Limited support for legalisation
- **Stigma**: Stigma toward psychedelic use
- **Changing Attitudes**: Changing attitudes toward psychedelics

**Other Substances:**
- **MDMA/Ecstasy**: Mixed attitudes toward MDMA
- **Ketamine**: Growing awareness of therapeutic potential
- **Novel Psychoactive Substances**: Limited public awareness
- **Inhalants**: Strong opposition to inhalant use
- **Steroids**: Concerns about steroid use
- **Substance-Specific**: Attitudes vary by substance

**The Other Drugs Opinion Reality:**
- **Variable**: Variable attitudes across substances
- **Medical Acceptance**: Acceptance of medical use
- **Recreational Opposition**: Opposition to recreational use
- **Stigma**: Significant stigma for many substances
- **Evolving**: Evolving attitudes for some substances
- **Context-Dependent**: Attitudes depend on context
