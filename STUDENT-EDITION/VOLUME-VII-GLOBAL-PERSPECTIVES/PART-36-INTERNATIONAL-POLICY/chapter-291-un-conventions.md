# Chapter 291: UN Drug Conventions

## United Nations Drug Control Treaties

The UN drug conventions form the foundation of international drug control:

**1961 Single Convention:**
- **Foundation**: Foundation of international drug control
- **Cannabis and Coca**: Scheduling of cannabis and coca
- **Opium**: Regulation of opium
- **Medical Use**: Medical use provisions
- **Cultivation Control**: Control of cultivation
- **Compliance**: Compliance monitoring

**1971 Convention:**
- **Psychotropic Substances**: Control of psychotropic substances
- **LSD, MDMA**: Scheduling of psychedelics
- **Amphetamines**: Control of amphetamines
- **Barbiturates**: Control of barbiturates
- **Medical Use**: Medical use provisions
- **Research**: Research provisions

**1988 Convention:**
- **Illicit Traffic**: Convention against illicit traffic
- **Money Laundering**: Money laundering provisions
- **Precursor Chemicals**: Control of precursor chemicals
- **Trafficking Offenses**: Criminalization of trafficking
- **Extradition**: Extradition provisions
- **Law Enforcement**: Law enforcement cooperation

**Treaty Obligations:**
- **Criminalization**: Obligation to criminalize drug offenses
- **Scheduling**: Compliance with international scheduling
- **Control**: Control measures for scheduled substances
- **Reporting**: Reporting requirements
- **Cooperation**: International cooperation obligations
- **Penalties**: Appropriate penalties for offenses

**Compliance Mechanisms:**
- **INCB Monitoring**: INCB monitoring of compliance
- **Country Reports**: Country reports to INCB
- **Review**: Review of compliance
- **Technical Assistance**: Technical assistance for compliance
- **Peer Review**: Peer review of compliance
- **Sanctions**: Limited sanctions for non-compliance

**The UN Conventions Reality:**
- **Foundational**: Foundation of international drug control
- **Prohibition-Based**: Based on prohibition model
- **Controversial**: Controversial effectiveness
- **Reform Pressure**: Pressure for treaty reform
- **Tension**: Tension with national reforms
- **Rigid**: Rigid framework limiting policy innovation
