# Chapter 295: Human Rights and Drug Policy

## Human Rights Implications of Drug Policy

Drug policy has significant human rights implications:

**Human Rights Concerns:**
- **Right to Health**: Right to health and access to treatment
- **Right to Life**: Right to life in enforcement contexts
- **Freedom from Cruel Treatment**: Freedom from cruel and degrading treatment
- **Due Process**: Due process rights in drug prosecutions
- **Discrimination**: Discrimination in drug enforcement
- **Indigenous Rights**: Indigenous rights in drug policy

**Enforcement and Human Rights:**
- **Excessive Force**: Excessive force in drug enforcement
- **Extrajudicial Killings**: Extrajudicial killings in drug war
- **Torture**: Torture in drug enforcement
- **Arbitrary Detention**: Arbitrary detention of drug users
- **Fair Trial**: Fair trial rights in drug cases
- **Death Penalty**: Death penalty for drug offenses

**Access to Treatment:**
- **Right to Health**: Right to health includes access to treatment
- **Treatment Access**: Barriers to treatment access
- **Medication Access**: Access to essential medications
- **Pain Management**: Access to pain medication
- **Harm Reduction**: Right to harm reduction services
- **Discrimination**: Discrimination in treatment access

**Human Rights Framework:**
- **UN Human Rights Bodies**: UN human rights bodies addressing drug policy
- **Special Rapporteurs**: Special rapporteurs on drug policy
- **Human Rights Impact Assessment**: Human rights impact assessment of drug policy
- **Rights-Based Approach**: Rights-based approach to drug policy
- **Accountability**: Accountability for human rights violations
- **Remedies**: Remedies for human rights violations

**The Human Rights Reality:**
- **Violations**: Significant human rights violations in drug policy
- **Death Penalty**: Death penalty for drug offenses
- **Enforcement Abuses**: Enforcement abuses
- **Treatment Barriers**: Barriers to treatment access
- **Growing Attention**: Growing attention to human rights
- **Reform Needed**: Human rights-based reform needed
