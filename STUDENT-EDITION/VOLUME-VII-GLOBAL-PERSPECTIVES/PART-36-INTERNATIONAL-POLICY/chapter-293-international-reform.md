# Chapter 293: International Reform

## Movement for International Drug Policy Reform

There is a growing movement for reform of international drug policy:

**Reform Arguments:**
- **Ineffectiveness**: Ineffectiveness of current approach
- **Human Rights**: Human rights violations
- **Public Health**: Public health focus needed
- **Development**: Development impacts of drug policy
- **Racial Justice**: Racial justice concerns
- **Evidence-Based**: Need for evidence-based policy

**Reform Movements:**
- **NGO Advocacy**: NGO advocacy for reform
- **Academic Advocacy**: Academic advocacy for reform
- **Political Advocacy**: Political advocacy for reform
- **Civil Society**: Civil society movements
- **International Networks**: International reform networks
- **Regional Reform**: Regional reform efforts

**Treaty Reform:**
- **Treaty Reform Debate**: Debate over treaty reform
- **Flexibility**: Flexibility interpretations of treaties
- **Modernization**: Modernization of treaties
- **Withdrawal**: Possibility of treaty withdrawal
- **New Treaties**: Possibility of new treaties
- **Compliance**: Compliance challenges for reforming countries

**Alternative Frameworks:**
- **Health-Based Framework**: Health-based international framework
- **Human Rights Framework**: Human rights-based framework
- **Development Framework**: Development-focused framework
- **Regulation Framework**: Regulation-focused framework
- **Harm Reduction Framework**: Harm reduction framework
- **Hybrid Approaches**: Hybrid approaches to international policy

**Challenges to Reform:**
- **Conservative Countries**: Conservative countries opposing reform
- **Bureaucratic Inertia**: Bureaucratic inertia in international organizations
- **Political Will**: Lack of political will for reform
- **US Influence**: US influence on international policy
- **Treaty Constraints**: Treaty constraints on national reform
- **Coordination**: Coordination challenges for reform

**The International Reform Reality:**
- **Growing**: Growing reform movement
- **Challenging**: Challenging to achieve international reform
- **Influential**: Influential on national debates
- **Slow**: Slow progress at international level
- **National First**: National reform preceding international reform
- **Future**: Future of international drug policy
