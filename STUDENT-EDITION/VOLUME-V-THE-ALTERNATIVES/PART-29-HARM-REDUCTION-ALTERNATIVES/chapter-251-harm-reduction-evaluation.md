# Chapter 251: Evaluating Harm Reduction

## Assessing Harm Reduction Outcomes

Evaluating harm reduction programs provides evidence for policy:

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- **Overdose Deaths**: Changes in overdose mortality
- **Infectious Disease**: Changes in infectious disease rates
- **Drug Use**: Changes in drug use patterns
- **Treatment Entry**: Changes in treatment entry
- **Public Order**: Changes in public drug use and disorder
- **Cost Savings**: Economic impacts and cost savings

**Syringe Exchange Evaluation:**
- **HIV Prevention**: Dramatic reduction in HIV transmission
- **Hepatitis Prevention**: Reduction in hepatitis transmission
- **No Increased Use**: No increase in drug use
- **Cost Savings**: Significant healthcare cost savings
- **Treatment Entry**: Increased treatment entry
- **Public Safety**: No increase in crime or public disorder

**Supervised Consumption Site Evaluation:**
- **Overdose Prevention**: Dramatic reduction in overdose deaths
- **Infectious Disease**: Reduction in infectious disease transmission
- **Increased Treatment**: Increased treatment entry
- **Public Order**: Reduced public drug use
- **Cost Savings**: Significant cost savings
- **Community Benefits**: Benefits to surrounding communities

**Naloxone Access Evaluation:**
- **Overdose Reversals**: Thousands of overdose reversals
- **Overdose Deaths**: Reduction in overdose deaths
- **Community Impact**: Community impact of naloxone distribution
- **Cost-Effectiveness**: Highly cost-effective intervention
- **Training Impact**: Impact of training programs
- **Survival**: Improved survival from overdose

**Drug Checking Evaluation:**
- **Overdose Prevention**: Prevention of overdose from adulterated substances
- **Harm Reduction**: Reduced harm from dangerous substances
- **Market Influence**: Influence on drug market quality
- **User Behavior**: Changes in user behavior
- **Data Collection**: Collection of valuable drug market data
- **Public Health**: Public health benefits

**The Evaluation Reality:**
- **Strong Evidence**: Strong evidence base for harm reduction
- **Cost-Effective**: Highly cost-effective interventions
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving programs
- **Controversial**: Controversial despite evidence
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based approach
- **Essential**: Essential component of drug policy
