# Chapter 246: Supervised Consumption Sites

## Safe Spaces for Drug Consumption

Supervised consumption sites provide safe spaces for drug use:

**Site Characteristics:**
- **Safe Environment**: Safe and hygienic environment for consumption
- **Medical Supervision**: Medical supervision during consumption
- **Overdose Prevention**: Overdose prevention and response
- **Equipment Provision**: Provision of sterile equipment
- **Health Services**: Access to health services
- **Referral Services**: Referral to treatment and other services

**Service Components:**
- **Consumption Rooms**: Supervised consumption rooms
- **Injection Equipment**: Sterile injection equipment
- **Smoking Rooms**: Rooms for smoking drugs
- **Naloxone**: Immediate naloxone access
- **Medical Staff**: Medical staff on-site
- **Counselling**: Counselling and support services

**Benefits:**
- **Reduced Overdose**: Dramatic reduction in overdose deaths
- **Reduced Infectious Disease**: Reduced infectious disease transmission
- **Increased Treatment**: Increased entry into treatment
- **Public Order**: Reduced public drug use and discarded paraphernalia
- **Cost Savings**: Cost savings from reduced healthcare and criminal justice costs
- **Community Safety**: No increase in crime or public disorder

**Implementation Examples:**
- **Canada**: Supervised consumption sites in Canada
- **Europe**: Long history of sites in Europe
- **Australia**: Supervised injection facilities in Australia
- **United States**: Limited implementation in US
- **Other Countries**: Implementation in other countries
- **Model Variation**: Variation in site models

**Challenges:**
- **Political Opposition**: Political opposition to sites
- **Community Opposition**: Community opposition (NIMBY)
- **Legal Barriers**: Legal barriers to implementation
- **Funding**: Funding challenges for sites
- **Location**: Challenges in site location
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting program acceptance

**The Supervised Consumption Site Reality:**
- **Effective**: Highly effective at reducing harm
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base
- **Controversial**: Controversial despite evidence
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving intervention
- **Expanding**: Expanding implementation globally
- **Essential**: Essential harm reduction service
