# Chapter 249: Syringe Exchange Programs

## Needle Exchange for Harm Reduction

Syringe exchange programs provide sterile injection equipment:

**Program Overview:**
- **Sterile Equipment**: Provision of sterile syringes and needles
- **Safe Disposal**: Safe disposal of used equipment
- **One-for-One Exchange**: Exchange of used for sterile equipment
- **No Cost**: Free or low-cost access
- **Multiple Access Points**: Various access points for services
- **Additional Services**: Additional health and social services

**Service Components:**
- **Syringe Distribution**: Distribution of sterile syringes
- **Needle Disposal**: Safe disposal containers
- **Condom Distribution**: Condom distribution for sexual health
- **HIV Testing**: HIV testing and counselling
- **Hepatitis Testing**: Hepatitis C testing and vaccination
- **Referral Services**: Referral to treatment and other services

**Benefits:**
- **HIV Prevention**: Dramatic reduction in HIV transmission
- **Hepatitis Prevention**: Reduction in hepatitis transmission
- **Overdose Prevention**: Overdose prevention education
- **Treatment Entry**: Increased entry into treatment
- **Cost Savings**: Cost savings from reduced healthcare costs
- **Public Safety**: No increase in drug use or crime

**Implementation Models:**
- **Fixed Sites**: Fixed location syringe exchange programs
- **Mobile Programs**: Mobile van-based programs
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacy-based syringe access
- **Vending Machines**: Syringe vending machines
- **Peer Distribution**: Peer distribution programs
- **Outreach Programs**: Outreach to injection drug users

**Challenges:**
- **Political Opposition**: Political opposition to programs
- **Community Opposition**: Community opposition (NIMBY)
- **Legal Barriers**: Legal barriers in some jurisdictions
- **Funding**: Funding challenges for programs
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting program use
- **Police Harassment**: Police harassment of program users

**The Syringe Exchange Reality:**
- **Effective**: Highly effective at preventing disease
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base
- **Controversial**: Controversial despite evidence
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving intervention
- **Widely Implemented**: Widely implemented globally
- **Essential**: Essential harm reduction service
