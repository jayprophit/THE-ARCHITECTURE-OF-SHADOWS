# Chapter 42: The "Lawyer"

## Legal Professionals Who Enable the Trade

Corrupt lawyers play crucial roles in the drug trade:

**Services Provided:**
- **Legal Advice**: Advise drug organisations on legal matters
- **Defence**: Represent drug traffickers in court
- **Corporate Structures**: Create shell companies and complex legal structures
- **Asset Protection**: Help hide and protect assets
- **Negotiation**: Negotiate with authorities and other criminal organisations
- **Compliance**: Advise on how to appear legitimate while breaking laws

**Types of Involvement:**
- **Defence Attorneys**: Legitimate defence of criminal clients
- **Corrupt Lawyers**: Actively facilitate criminal activities
- **Grey Area Lawyers**: Push legal boundaries without clearly breaking laws
- **Specialist Lawyers**: Specialise in specific areas (international law, tax law)
- **In-House Counsel**: Employed directly by criminal organisations

## The Role of Corruption

**Corrupt Practices:**
- **Bribery**: Bribing judges, prosecutors, and officials
- **Evidence Tampering**: Tampering with or destroying evidence
- **Witness Intimidation**: Intimidating witnesses on behalf of clients
- **Information Leaks**: Leaking confidential information to clients
- **Legal Loopholes**: Exploiting legal loopholes to protect criminal activities
- **Professional Privilege Abuse**: Using attorney-client privilege to hide criminal activities

**Ethical Violations:**
- **Conflict of Interest**: Representing conflicting interests
- **Fraud**: Engaging in fraudulent legal practices
- **Money Laundering**: Using legal practices to launder money
- **Obstruction of Justice**: Interfering with legal proceedings
- **Professional Misconduct**: Violating professional ethics codes

**Legal Consequences:**
- **Disbarment**: Loss of license to practice law
- **Criminal Charges**: Prosecution for criminal activities
- **Prison Sentences**: Incarceration for serious offenses
- **Asset Forfeiture**: Loss of assets gained through corruption
- **Reputation Damage**: Permanent damage to professional reputation

**The Lawyer's Motivation:**
- **High Fees**: Substantial fees for specialised services
- **Power**: Influence over legal processes
- **Thrill**: Excitement of working with criminal organisations
- **Coercion**: Some lawyers coerced by criminal organisations
- **Ideology**: Some believe in challenging drug laws
