# Chapter 35: The Street Dealer

## Profile and Motivations

Street dealers are the face of the drug trade to most users:

**Demographics:**
- **Age**: Often young adults (18-35)
- **Background**: Often from disadvantaged backgrounds
- **Education**: Often limited educational opportunities
- **Employment**: Limited legal employment options
- **Family**: Often come from families with drug involvement

**Motivations:**
- **Economic Necessity**: Primary motivation for most
- **Lack of Alternatives**: Few legitimate economic opportunities
- **Peer Influence**: Growing up in drug-dealing environments
- **Status**: Dealing can provide status in some communities
- **Addiction**: Some deal to support their own use

**Types of Street Dealers:**
- **User-Dealers**: Deal primarily to support their own use
- **Career Dealers**: Dealing as primary occupation
- **Part-Time Dealers**: Supplement other income
- **Youth Dealers**: Young people recruited by older dealers
- **Territorial Dealers**: Control specific territories

## The Economics of Street Dealing

Street-level economics:

**Profit Margins:**
- **Markups**: Significant markups from wholesale to street level
- **Volume**: High volume, low margin per transaction
- **Risk Premium**: Prices reflect risk of arrest and violence
- **Competition**: Competition affects prices and territories

**Costs of Doing Business:**
- **Supply Costs**: Purchasing from wholesalers
- **Losses**: Seizures, theft, robbery
- **Violence**: Protection payments, violence costs
- **Legal Costs**: Fines, legal fees, incarceration
- **Lifestyle**: Maintaining street credibility

**Income:**
- **Variable**: Income fluctuates based on sales
- **Unstable**: No guaranteed income
- **Risk**: High risk of arrest and violence
- **Tax-Free**: No taxes paid (but also no legal protections)
- **Cash Business**: All transactions in cash

## Risks and Consequences

**Legal Risks:**
- **Arrest**: Constant risk of arrest and prosecution
- **Incarceration**: Prison sentences for dealing
- **Criminal Record**: Limits future employment
- **Asset Forfeiture**: Loss of money and property
- **Surveillance**: Police monitoring and informants

**Violence Risks:**
- **Robbery**: Target for robbery by other criminals
- **Territorial Disputes**: Violence over territory
- **Debts**: Violence for unpaid debts
- **Retaliation**: Violence for perceived disrespect
- **Gang Violence**: Involvement in gang conflicts

**Health Risks:**
- **Addiction**: Easy access to drugs leads to addiction
- **Violence**: Risk of injury or death from violence
- **Stress**: High stress from constant risk
- **Mental Health**: Anxiety, paranoia, PTSD
- **Overdose**: Risk of overdose from own use

**Social Consequences:**
- **Family Strain**: Dealing strains family relationships
- **Social Isolation**: Limited legitimate social connections
- **Community Impact**: Dealing harms communities
- **Limited Future**: Criminal record limits opportunities
- **Stigma**: Social stigma associated with dealing
