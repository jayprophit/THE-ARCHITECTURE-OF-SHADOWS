# Chapter 39: The Cartel Member

## Structure and Hierarchy

Cartels are highly organised criminal enterprises:

**Hierarchical Structure:**
- **Leadership**: Top leadership (bosses, kingpins)
- **Management**: Regional managers, lieutenants
- **Operational**: Traffickers, enforcers, accountants
- **Street Level**: Dealers, lookouts, runners
- **Support**: Lawyers, corrupt officials, money launderers

**Organisation:**
- **Military Discipline**: Military-style organisation and discipline
- **Territorial Control**: Control of specific territories
- **Diversification**: Often involved in multiple criminal activities
- **Vertical Integration**: Control of entire supply chain
- **Political Influence**: Corruption and political connections

## Violence and Enforcement

**Violent Enforcement:**
- **Territorial Disputes**: Violent conflicts over territory
- **Internal Discipline**: Violent punishment for disloyalty
- **Intimidation**: Violence to intimidate competitors and communities
- **Public Violence**: Public displays of violence to maintain power
- **Assassinations**: Targeted killings of rivals and officials

**Methods of Violence:**
- **Assassinations**: Targeted killings
- **Massacres**: Mass killings of rivals or civilians
- **Torture**: Extreme violence as punishment and intimidation
- **Disappearances**: Forcing people to disappear
- **Bombings**: Explosives attacks on rivals and infrastructure

**Impact on Communities:**
- **Fear**: Communities living in fear
- **Displacement**: People forced to flee violence
- **Economic Impact**: Violence disrupts local economies
- **Social Fabric**: Violence tears apart communities
- **Normalisation**: Violence becomes normalised in affected areas

## Major Cartels

**Historical Cartels:**
- **Medellín Cartel**: Pablo Escobar, dominated 1980s cocaine trade
- **Cali Cartel**: Rival to Medellín, more business-like approach
- **Gulf Cartel**: One of Mexico's oldest cartels

**Current Major Cartels:**
- **Sinaloa Cartel**: Mexico's most powerful cartel
- **Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG)**: Rapidly expanding, extremely violent
- **Los Zetas**: Originally military special forces, extremely violent
- **Beltrán Leyva Cartel**: Split from Sinaloa, involved in fierce conflicts

**Other Organisations:**
- **Taliban**: Major opium producer in Afghanistan
- **Italian Mafia**: Historical involvement in drug trade
- **Chinese Triads**: Involved in synthetic drug production
- **Russian Organised Crime**: Major trafficking networks

**The Cartel Member's Role:**
- **Soldier**: Often function as soldiers in criminal armies
- **Loyalty**: Extreme loyalty demanded, enforced by violence
- **Risk**: Extremely high risk of death or imprisonment
- **Power**: Can wield significant power in territories
- **Psychological Impact**: Often traumatised by violence
