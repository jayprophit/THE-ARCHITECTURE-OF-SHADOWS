# Chapter 90: The Corrupt Judge

## Judicial Corruption

Corrupt judges undermine the justice system:

**Forms of Judicial Corruption:**
- **Bribery**: Accepting bribes for favorable rulings
- **Case Fixing**: Fixing cases for drug traffickers
- **Selective Sentencing**: Selective sentencing based on bribes
- **Case Dismissal**: Dismissing cases in exchange for payment
- **Reduced Sentences**: Reducing sentences for corrupt reasons
- **Early Release**: Facilitating early release for payment

**Mechanisms of Corruption:**
- **Direct Bribery**: Direct cash payments for rulings
- **Indirect Benefits**: Indirect benefits and favors
- **Family Benefits**: Benefits to family members
- **Future Employment**: Promises of future employment
- **Political Pressure**: Political pressure on judges
- **Threats**: Threats to coerce favorable rulings

**Impact on Justice:**
- **Erosion of Trust**: Erosion of public trust in judiciary
- **Injustice**: Injustice for victims and communities
- **Impunity**: Impunity for drug traffickers
- **Systemic Corruption**: Systemic corruption in justice system
- **Violence**: Corruption enables continued violence
- **Social Harm**: Social harm from corrupted justice

**Detection Challenges:**
- **Judicial Independence**: Judicial independence protects corruption
- **Legal Immunity**: Legal immunity for judicial decisions
- **Complex Cases**: Complex cases hide corruption
- **Political Protection**: Political protection from investigation
- **Professional Networks**: Professional networks protect corrupt judges
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for investigation

**The Corrupt Judge's Reality:**
- **Power**: Judicial power enables corruption
- **Rationalisation**: Rationalisation of corrupt behaviour
- **Risk**: Risk of exposure and prosecution
- **Professional Betrayal**: Betrayal of professional ethics
- **Systemic Impact**: Systemic impact on justice
- **Accountability**: Need for judicial accountability
