# Chapter 91: The Corrupt Banker

## Financial System Corruption

Corrupt bankers enable money laundering through the financial system:

**Forms of Banking Corruption:**
- **Willful Blindness**: Ignoring obvious signs of money laundering
- **Active Participation**: Active participation in money laundering
- **Structuring**: Helping clients structure transactions to avoid reporting
- **Shell Companies**: Creating and managing shell companies for clients
- **Offshore Services**: Providing offshore banking services for money laundering
- **Client Protection**: Protecting clients from regulatory scrutiny

**Mechanisms of Corruption:**
- **Fee-Based**: Corruption driven by fee income
- **Performance Pressure**: Pressure to meet performance targets
- **Management Pressure**: Management pressure to ignore suspicious activity
- **Culture of Secrecy**: Banking cultures that prioritise secrecy
- **Complex Structures**: Using complex financial structures to hide money laundering
- **International Networks**: International networks to move money

**Impact on Financial System:**
- **Erosion of Trust**: Erosion of trust in financial system
- **Systemic Risk**: Systemic risk to financial stability
- **Regulatory Failure**: Failure of regulatory oversight
- **Economic Impact**: Economic impact of money laundering
- **International Consequences**: International consequences of banking corruption
- **Criminal Economy**: Enabling the criminal economy

**Detection Challenges:**
- **Complexity**: Complexity of financial transactions
- **International Scope**: International scope complicates detection
- **Legal Complexity**: Legal complexity of financial regulations
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited regulatory resources
- **Professional Expertise**: Professional expertise in avoiding detection
- **Political Influence**: Political influence protecting corrupt banks

**The Corrupt Banker's Reality:**
- **Profit**: Profit motivation drives corruption
- **Rationalisation**: Rationalisation of corrupt behaviour
- **Risk**: Risk of prosecution and fines
- **Professional Betrayal**: Betrayal of professional ethics
- **Systemic Impact**: Systemic impact on financial system
- **Accountability**: Need for banking accountability
