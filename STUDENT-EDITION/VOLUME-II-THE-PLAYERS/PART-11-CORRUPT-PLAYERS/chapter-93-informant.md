# Chapter 93: The Informant

## The Complex Role of Informants

Informants play a complex role in drug enforcement:

**Types of Informants:**
- **Coerced Informants**: Informants coerced by law enforcement
- **Paid Informants**: Informants paid for information
- **Revenge Informants**: Informants motivated by revenge
- **Reduced Sentence**: Informants seeking reduced sentences
- **Professional Informants**: Professional informants working for multiple agencies
- **Double Agents**: Informants working for both law enforcement and criminals

**Motivations:**
- **Self-Preservation**: Fear of prosecution or violence
- **Financial Gain**: Payment for information
- **Revenge**: Revenge against former associates
- **Reduced Sentence**: Seeking reduced prison sentences
- **Protection**: Seeking protection from prosecution
- **Coercion**: Coerced by law enforcement

**Risks:**
- **Violence**: Extreme risk of violence from criminal associates
- **Betrayal**: Betrayal of former friends and associates
- **Mental Health**: Mental health impact of informant work
- **Legal Risk**: Legal risk if informant status is revealed
- **Social Isolation**: Social isolation from former community
- **Lifetime Risk**: Lifetime risk of retaliation

**Ethical Issues:**
- **Coercion**: Ethical issues around coercion
- **Reliability**: Reliability of informant information
- **Entanglement**: Law enforcement entanglement with criminal elements
- **Accountability**: Limited accountability for informant actions
- **Due Process**: Due process concerns with informant testimony
- **Systemic Impact**: Systemic impact on communities

**The Informant's Reality:**
- **Fear**: Constant fear of discovery
- **Guilt**: Guilt about betrayal
- **Isolation**: Social isolation
- **Danger**: Extreme danger
- **Complex Loyalties**: Complex divided loyalties
- **Survival**: Survival as primary motivation
