# Chapter 54: Opium Poppy Cultivation

**Traditional Cultivation:**
- **Small-Scale**: Traditional small-scale cultivation
- **Mountainous Terrain**: Cultivated in mountainous regions
- **Manual Labour**: Intensive manual labour
- **Traditional Methods**: Methods passed down through generations
- **Sustainable**: Traditional methods often more sustainable
- **Cultural Significance**: Cultural significance in some regions

**Cultivation Process:**
- **Sowing**: Seeds sown in prepared fields
- **Growth**: Plants grow for several months
- **Flowering**: Plants produce distinctive flowers
- **Scoring**: Seed pods scored to release latex
- **Harvesting**: Latex collected from seed pods
- **Processing**: Opium refined to morphine, then heroin

**Environmental Factors:**
- **Climate**: Temperate to subtropical, well-drained soil
- **Altitude**: Optimal altitude varies by region
- **Water**: Adequate water supply
- **Soil**: Well-drained soil
- **Sunlight**: Adequate sunlight
- **Pests and Diseases**: Management of pests and diseases

**Geographic Variations:**
- **Afghanistan**: Largest producer, specific cultivation methods
- **Myanmar**: Traditional cultivation methods
- **Mexico**: Different cultivation methods and varieties
- **Golden Triangle**: Historical cultivation region
- **Golden Crescent**: Major current production region

**Opium Processing:**
- **Raw Opium**: Latex collected from seed pods
- **Drying**: Sun-drying raw opium
- **Morphine Extraction**: Chemical extraction of morphine
- **Heroin Synthesis**: Chemical synthesis of heroin from morphine
- **Byproducts**: Various byproducts from processing

**Environmental Impact:**
- **Deforestation**: Cultivation can contribute to deforestation
- **Chemical Use**: Chemicals used in processing
- **Water Use**: Significant water use
- **Soil Degradation**: Potential soil degradation
- **Biodiversity**: Impact on local biodiversity
