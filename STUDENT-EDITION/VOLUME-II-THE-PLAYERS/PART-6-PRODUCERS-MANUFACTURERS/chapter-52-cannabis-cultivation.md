# Chapter 52: Cannabis Cultivation Methods

**Indoor Cultivation:**
- **Controlled Environment**: Complete control over light, temperature, humidity
- **Artificial Lighting**: LED, HPS, MH lights
- **Hydroponics**: Soil-less cultivation using nutrient solutions
- **Multiple Harvests**: Can harvest multiple times per year
- **Higher Quality**: Often produces higher quality cannabis
- **Higher Cost**: Higher energy and equipment costs

**Outdoor Cultivation:**
- **Natural Environment**: Dependent on natural sunlight and climate
- **Seasonal**: Single harvest per year in most climates
- **Lower Cost**: Lower energy costs
- **Larger Scale**: Can cultivate larger plants
- **Climate Dependent**: Success depends on climate
- **Environmental Impact**: Less energy intensive

**Greenhouse Cultivation:**
- **Hybrid Approach**: Combines natural and artificial light
- **Climate Control**: Some control over temperature and humidity
- **Extended Season**: Can extend growing season
- **Moderate Cost**: Moderate costs between indoor and outdoor
- **Quality**: Often high quality
- **Flexibility**: Flexible approach to cultivation

**Hydroponic Systems:**
- **Deep Water Culture**: Roots suspended in nutrient solution
- **Drip Systems**: Nutrient solution dripped onto roots
- **Ebb and Flow**: Periodic flooding and draining
- **Aeroponics**: Roots misted with nutrient solution
- **Nutrient Film Technique**: Thin film of nutrient solution flows over roots

**Organic Cultivation:**
- **Natural Nutrients**: Organic fertilisers and amendments
- **Living Soil**: Soil with active microbiome
- **Sustainable**: More sustainable practices
- **Flavour**: Often enhances flavour profile
- **Slower Growth**: Often slower than synthetic nutrients
- **Complexity**: Requires understanding of soil biology

**Cultivation Techniques:**
- **Cloning**: Asexual reproduction from cuttings
- **Training**: Techniques to shape plant growth (LST, topping, FIM)
- **Pruning**: Removing plant material to improve growth
- **Defoliation**: Removing leaves to improve light penetration
- **Screen of Green (SCROG)**: Training plants through screen
- **Sea of Green (SOG)**: Many small plants grown quickly
