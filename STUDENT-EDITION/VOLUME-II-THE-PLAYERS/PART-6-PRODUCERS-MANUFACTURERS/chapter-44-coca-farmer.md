# Chapter 44: The Coca Farmer

## The Source of Cocaine

Coca farmers are at the beginning of the cocaine supply chain:

**Geographic Location:**
- **Primary Regions**: Colombia, Peru, Bolivia
- **Altitude**: 500-2,000 metres above sea level
- **Climate**: Tropical, consistent rainfall, moderate temperatures
- **Terrain**: Often remote, mountainous areas
- **Economic Context**: Often poor, rural communities

**Cultivation:**
- **Traditional Use**: Coca has been cultivated for thousands of years
- **Traditional Consumption**: Chewing coca leaves for energy and altitude sickness
- **Legal Status**: Traditional use legal in some countries; cocaine production illegal
- **Cultivation Cycle**: 12-18 months to maturity, multiple harvests per year
- **Labour Intensive**: Requires significant manual labour

**Economic Reality:**
- **Poverty**: Often among poorest farmers in their countries
- **Lack of Alternatives**: Few alternative crops or economic opportunities
- **Market Forces**: Subject to market fluctuations and cartel control
- **Debt**: Often in debt to intermediaries
- **Violence**: Subject to violence from cartels and government forces

## Colombia, Peru, Bolivia

**Colombia:**
- **Largest Producer**: World's largest coca producer
- **Conflict Zones**: Cultivation often in conflict zones
- **Government Eradication**: Aerial spraying and manual eradication
- **Alternative Development**: Programs to encourage alternative crops
- **FARC Influence**: Former FARC territories were major production areas

**Peru:**
- **Traditional Cultivation**: Long history of coca cultivation
- **Legal Traditional Use**: Legal traditional use of coca leaves
- **Valley of the Apurímac, Ene and Mantaro Rivers (VRAEM)**: Major production region
- **Government Response**: Eradication and alternative development programs
- **Traditional vs. Illicit**: Tension between traditional and illicit use

**Bolivia:**
- **Traditional Use**: Strong tradition of coca use
- **Legal Cultivation**: Legal cultivation for traditional use
- **Controlled Market**: Government-controlled market for legal coca
- **Chapare Region**: Major production area
- **Evo Morales**: Former president was coca grower union leader

**The Coca Farmer's Reality:**
- **Caught in Middle**: Between cartels, government, and poverty
- **Limited Agency**: Little control over their economic situation
- **Violence**: Subject to violence from multiple sources
- **Economic Necessity**: Often no viable alternatives
- **Cultural Significance**: Coca has deep cultural significance in Andean cultures
