# Chapter 102: The Crime-Terror Connection

## Nexus Between Organised Crime and Terrorism

The nexus between organised crime and terrorism is a significant global concern:

**Types of Connections:**
- **Drug Trafficking for Terror Funding**: Terrorist groups using drug trafficking to fund operations
- **Shared Routes**: Shared trafficking routes for drugs and other contraband
- **Shared Networks**: Shared networks for smuggling and distribution
- **Strategic Alliances**: Strategic alliances between criminal and terrorist groups
- **Competition**: Competition between criminal and terrorist groups
- **Cooperation**: Cooperation in certain contexts

**Documented Examples:**
- **Taliban**: Taliban involvement in Afghan opium trade
- **FARC**: FARC involvement in Colombian cocaine trade
- **ISIS**: ISIS involvement in drug trafficking in certain regions
- **Hezbollah**: Allegations of Hezbollah involvement in drug trade
- **Al-Qaeda**: Al-Qaeda connections to drug trafficking
- **Other Groups**: Various other terrorist groups involved in drug trade

**Mechanisms of Cooperation:**
- **Revenue Sharing**: Revenue sharing arrangements
- **Route Sharing**: Sharing smuggling routes
- **Protection**: Providing protection for drug operations
- **Logistics**: Sharing logistics and infrastructure
- **Money Laundering**: Shared money laundering networks
- **Corruption**: Shared corruption networks

**Impact on Global Security:**
- **Terror Funding**: Drug trade funding terrorist operations
- **Regional Instability**: Drug trade contributing to regional instability
- **State Fragility**: Drug trade contributing to state fragility
- **Violence**: Increased violence from crime-terror nexus
- **Counterterrorism Challenges**: Challenges for counterterrorism efforts
- **Drug Policy Impact**: Drug policy implications of crime-terror nexus

**The Crime-Terror Reality:**
- **Complex Nexus**: Complex relationships between crime and terror
- **Strategic Adaptation**: Both groups adapt to changing conditions
- **Regional Variation**: Significant regional variation in connections
- **Security Threat**: Significant security threat globally
- **Policy Challenge**: Complex policy challenge
- **International Cooperation**: Need for international cooperation
