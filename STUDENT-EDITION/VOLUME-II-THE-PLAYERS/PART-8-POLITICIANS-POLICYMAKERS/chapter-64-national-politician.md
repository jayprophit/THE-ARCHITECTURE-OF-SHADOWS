# Chapter 64: The National Politician

## Domestic Drug Policy

National politicians shape domestic drug policy:

**Roles and Responsibilities:**
- **Legislation**: Creating and passing drug laws
- **Budgeting**: Allocating resources for drug enforcement and treatment
- **Oversight**: Overseeing drug enforcement agencies
- **Policy Direction**: Setting overall drug policy direction
- **Public Statements**: Making public statements on drug issues
- **Constituent Service**: Responding to constituent concerns

**Political Dynamics:**
- **Tough on Crime**: Political pressure to be "tough on crime"
- **Public Opinion**: Responding to public opinion on drugs
- **Interest Groups**: Pressure from various interest groups
- **Party Politics**: Party positions on drug policy
- **Election Cycles**: Drug policy as election issue
- **Media Coverage**: Media coverage of drug policy positions

**Policy Approaches:**
- **Prohibition**: Strict prohibition and enforcement
- **Decriminalisation**: Reducing criminal penalties
- **Legalisation**: Legal and regulated markets
- **Harm Reduction**: Emphasis on reducing harm
- **Treatment**: Emphasis on treatment and prevention
- **Mixed Approaches**: Combination of approaches

**Challenges:**
- **Complexity**: Complexity of drug policy issues
- **Political Pressure**: Political pressure for simple solutions
- **Evidence vs. Politics**: Tension between evidence and politics
- **Resource Allocation**: Limited resources for competing priorities
- **International Obligations**: International drug control obligations
- **Public Perception**: Managing public perception

**The National Politician's Perspective:**
- **Public Safety**: Concern for public safety
- **Political Survival**: Political survival considerations
- **Evidence-Based**: Desire for evidence-based policy
- **Constituent Concerns**: Responding to constituent concerns
- **Legacy**: Desire for positive legacy
