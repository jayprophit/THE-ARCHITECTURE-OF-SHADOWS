# Chapter 57: The Border Control Agent

## Intercepting International Drug Shipments

Border control agents prevent drugs from crossing borders:

**Roles and Responsibilities:**
- **Inspection**: Inspecting passengers, cargo, and vehicles
- **Detection**: Using technology to detect concealed drugs
- **Interdiction**: Intercepting drug shipments
- **Intelligence**: Gathering intelligence on smuggling routes
- **Cooperation**: Working with international agencies
- **Documentation**: Documenting seizures and arrests

**Detection Methods:**
- **X-Ray Scanning**: X-ray scanners for cargo and luggage
- **Sniffer Dogs**: Dogs trained to detect drugs
- **Chemical Testing**: Chemical swabs and tests
- **Profiling**: Behavioural and document analysis
- **Intelligence**: Using intelligence to target high-risk shipments
- **Technology**: Advanced scanning and detection technology

**Challenges:**
- **Volume**: High volume of passengers and cargo
- **Sophistication**: Increasing sophistication of smuggling methods
- **Corruption**: Risk of corruption from drug money
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for comprehensive screening
- **Pressure**: Pressure to facilitate legitimate trade while preventing smuggling
- **International Cooperation**: Need for international cooperation

**The Border Control Agent's Perspective:**
- **National Security**: Motivated by national security concerns
- **Public Safety**: Protecting public from harmful substances
- **Professional Challenges**: Professional challenges of border security
- **Moral Dilemmas**: Moral dilemmas about drug policy
- **International Context**: Working in international context
