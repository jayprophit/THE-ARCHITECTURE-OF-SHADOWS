# Chapter 73: The Cryptocurrency Exchange

## Digital Money Laundering

Cryptocurrency exchanges facilitate modern money laundering:

**Roles in Money Laundering:**
- **Conversion Services**: Converting drug money to cryptocurrency
- **Anonymous Transactions**: Facilitating anonymous transactions
- **Cross-Border Transfers**: Enabling rapid cross-border transfers
- **Mixing Services**: Mixing services to obscure transaction trails
- **Privacy Coins**: Supporting privacy-focused cryptocurrencies
- **KYC Evasion**: Methods to evade KYC requirements

**Cryptocurrency Methods:**
- **Bitcoin**: Primary cryptocurrency for money laundering
- **Privacy Coins**: Monero, Zcash for enhanced privacy
- **Mixing Services**: Services that mix cryptocurrency transactions
- **Tumblers**: Services that obscure transaction trails
- **Decentralised Exchanges**: Exchanges without KYC requirements
- **Peer-to-Peer Transactions**: Direct peer-to-peer transactions

**Regulatory Response:**
- **KYC Requirements**: Increasing KYC requirements for exchanges
- **AML Regulations**: Anti-money laundering regulations for cryptocurrency
- **Blockchain Analysis**: Blockchain analysis to track transactions
- **Exchange Regulation**: Regulation of cryptocurrency exchanges
- **International Cooperation**: International cooperation on cryptocurrency regulation
- **Law Enforcement**: Law enforcement tracking of cryptocurrency transactions

**The Cryptocurrency Exchange's Perspective:**
- **Innovation**: Commitment to financial innovation
- **Compliance**: Compliance with regulations
- **Privacy**: Balancing privacy with regulation
- **Profit**: Profit-driven motivation
- **Technology**: Utilising blockchain technology
