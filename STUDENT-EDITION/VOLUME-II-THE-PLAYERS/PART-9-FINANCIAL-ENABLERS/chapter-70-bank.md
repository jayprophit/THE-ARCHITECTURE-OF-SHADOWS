# Chapter 70: The Bank

## Financial System Complicity

Banks play a critical role in the drug trade through money laundering:

**Roles in Money Laundering:**
- **Deposit Acceptance**: Accepting large cash deposits
- **Wire Transfers**: Facilitating international wire transfers
- **Account Services**: Providing accounts to drug traffickers
- **Investment Services**: Investing drug money in legitimate assets
- **Shell Companies**: Creating and managing shell companies
- **Offshore Services**: Providing offshore banking services

**Complicity Patterns:**
- **Willful Blindness**: Ignoring obvious signs of money laundering
- **Active Participation**: Active involvement in money laundering
- **Negligence**: Failure to implement adequate controls
- **Pressure from Management**: Pressure to ignore suspicious activity
- **Profit Motive**: Profiting from drug money
- **Cultural Issues**: Cultures that prioritise profit over compliance

**Regulatory Response:**
- **AML Regulations**: Anti-money laundering regulations
- **KYC Requirements**: Know Your Customer requirements
- **Suspicious Activity Reports**: Requirements to report suspicious activity
- **Fines**: Multi-billion dollar fines for money laundering failures
- **Prosecutions**: Criminal prosecutions of banks and executives
- **Whistleblowers**: Whistleblower revelations of bank complicity

**Major Cases:**
- **HSBC**: Fined $1.9 billion for money laundering
- **Deutsche Bank**: Multiple fines for money laundering failures
- **Standard Chartered**: Fined for sanctions and money laundering violations
- **Danske Bank**: Massive money laundering scandal
- **Wachovia**: Fined for Mexican drug money laundering

**The Bank's Perspective:**
- **Profit**: Primary focus on profit
- **Compliance**: Compliance as cost of doing business
- **Risk Management**: Managing regulatory and reputational risk
- **Shareholder Value**: Maximising shareholder value
- **Corporate Responsibility**: Limited corporate responsibility considerations
