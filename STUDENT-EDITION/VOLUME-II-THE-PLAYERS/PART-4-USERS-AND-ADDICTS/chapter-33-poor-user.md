# Chapter 33: The Poor User

## The Exploitation of the Vulnerable

Poor drug users face unique vulnerabilities:

**Economic Exploitation:**
- **Cheap Drugs**: Access only to low-quality, adulterated substances
- **Debt Traps**: Credit from dealers creates debt bondage
- **Cuckooing**: Dealers taking over homes for dealing
- **County Lines**: Exploited as drug runners
- **Prostitution**: Trading sex for drugs

**Limited Resources:**
- **Treatment Access**: Limited access to quality treatment
- **Healthcare**: Poor access to healthcare services
- **Housing**: Homelessness or unstable housing
- **Nutrition**: Poor nutrition exacerbates health problems
- **Support Systems**: Limited social support networks

**Legal System Impact:**
- **Policing**: Heavy policing in poor neighbourhoods
- **Arrest Rates**: Higher arrest rates for possession
- **Incarceration**: More likely to receive prison sentences
- **Legal Representation**: Poor quality legal representation
- **Criminal Records**: Criminal records limit employment opportunities

## Intergenerational Addiction

Addiction often runs in families in poverty:

**Cycle of Poverty and Addiction:**
- **Childhood Exposure**: Children exposed to parental drug use
- **Normalisation**: Drug use becomes normalised behaviour
- **Limited Opportunities**: Poverty limits life opportunities
- **Trauma**: Childhood trauma increases addiction risk
- **Lack of Resources**: Limited access to treatment and support

**Breaking the Cycle:**
- **Early Intervention**: Programs for at-risk youth
- **Family Treatment**: Treating entire families
- **Economic Support**: Addressing underlying poverty
- **Education**: Breaking cycles through education
- **Community Support**: Building supportive communities

**The Poverty-Addiction Trap:**
- **Bidirectional Relationship**: Poverty leads to addiction, addiction worsens poverty
- **Limited Options**: Few alternatives to drug use
- **Survival Mechanism**: Drugs as coping mechanism for difficult circumstances
- **Systemic Issues**: Broader social and economic factors contribute
- **Need for Comprehensive Solutions**: Treatment alone insufficient without addressing poverty
