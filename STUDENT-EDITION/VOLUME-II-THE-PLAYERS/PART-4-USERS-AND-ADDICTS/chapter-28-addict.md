# Chapter 28: The Addict

## The Cycle of Addiction

Addiction follows a predictable cycle:

**Initiation Phase:**
- First use, often voluntary
- Positive initial experience
- Social or environmental factors

**Escalation Phase:**
- Increased frequency of use
- Development of tolerance
- Increased quantity to achieve same effect

**Maintenance Phase:**
- Regular, often daily use
- Life organised around obtaining and using drugs
- Negative consequences begin to accumulate

**Crisis Phase:**
- Severe consequences (health, legal, financial, relationships)
- Attempts to quit often fail
- Rock bottom may trigger change or continued decline

**Recovery Phase (if achieved):**
- Abstinence or controlled use
- Addressing underlying issues
- Ongoing maintenance and relapse prevention

## Physical and Psychological Dependence

**Physical Dependence:**
- **Tolerance**: Need higher doses for same effect
- **Withdrawal**: Physical symptoms when drug use stops
- **Neuroadaptation**: Brain adapts to drug presence
- **Medical Detox**: Often required for safe withdrawal

**Psychological Dependence:**
- **Craving**: Intense desire for drug
- **Emotional Dependence**: Using to cope with emotions
- **Behavioural Patterns**: Rituals and habits around use
- **Mental Obsession**: Constant thoughts about drug use
- **Triggers**: People, places, situations associated with use

**The Interaction:**
- Physical and psychological dependence often coexist
- Physical withdrawal can be managed medically
- Psychological dependence often requires longer-term treatment
- Both must be addressed for successful recovery
