# Chapter 27: The Profile of a User

## Demographics (Age, Gender, Socio-Economic Status)

Drug users span all demographic groups:

**Age Distribution:**
- **Adolescents (12-17)**: Experimentation, peer influence
- **Young Adults (18-25)**: Peak use period, highest rates
- **Adults (26-34)**: Continued use, some transition to dependence
- **Middle-Aged (35-54)**: Long-term users, prescription drug issues
- **Older Adults (55+)**: Prescription medications, alcohol

**Gender Patterns:**
- **Historically Male**: Higher rates of illicit drug use
- **Closing Gap**: Women's use increasing, particularly prescription drugs
- **Substance Differences**: Men more likely to use stimulants; women more likely to use prescription drugs
- **Treatment Seeking**: Women more likely to seek treatment for certain substances

**Socio-Economic Status:**
- **All Classes**: Drug use occurs across all socio-economic groups
- **Different Patterns**: Different substances favoured by different classes
- **Poverty**: Higher rates of certain drugs (e.g., crack, heroin)
- **Wealth**: Access to higher-quality substances, prescription drug abuse

## The Spectrum of Use (Recreational to Dependent)

Drug use exists on a continuum:

**Abstinence**: No drug use
**Experimental**: One-time or occasional use
**Recreational**: Regular but controlled use
**Problematic**: Use causing problems but not dependent
**Dependent**: Addiction, compulsive use despite consequences

**Factors Influencing Progression:**
- **Genetics**: Family history of addiction
- **Environment**: Peer groups, availability, stress
- **Mental Health**: Co-occurring disorders
- **Trauma**: Childhood or adult trauma
- **Age of Onset**: Earlier use increases risk of dependence
