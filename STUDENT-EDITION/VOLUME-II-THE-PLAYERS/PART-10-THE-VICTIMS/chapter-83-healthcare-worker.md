# Chapter 83: The Healthcare Worker

## Frontline Care for Drug-Related Harm

Healthcare workers bear the burden of drug-related harm:

**Emergency Department Impact:**
- **Overdose Cases**: Constant stream of overdose cases
- **Trauma**: Drug-related trauma injuries
- **Violence**: Violence from drug-affected patients
- **Resource Strain**: Strain on emergency department resources
- **Burnout**: High rates of burnout among staff
- **Mental Health Impact**: Mental health impact on healthcare workers

**Challenges:**
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for drug-related care
- **Repeat Patients**: Frequent repeat patients
- **Violence Risk**: Risk of violence from drug-affected patients
- **Emotional Toll**: Emotional toll of treating preventable conditions
- **Systemic Issues**: Frustration with systemic issues
- **Moral Injury**: Moral injury from preventable deaths

**Treatment Challenges:**
- **Limited Treatment Options**: Limited treatment options
- **Wait Lists**: Long wait lists for treatment
- **Funding**: Inadequate funding for treatment
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting treatment seeking
- **Complex Cases**: Complex medical and social cases
- **Prevention**: Frustration with lack of prevention

**The Healthcare Worker's Perspective:**
- **Compassion**: Compassion for patients
- **Frustration**: Frustration with preventable harm
- **Burnout**: High rates of burnout
- **Systemic Issues**: Awareness of systemic issues
- **Advocacy**: Advocacy for better policies
- **Hope**: Hope for better approaches
