# Chapter 86: The Community

## Collective Victim of the Drug Trade

Communities are collective victims of the drug trade:

**Social Impact:**
- **Community Disintegration**: Breakdown of community cohesion
- **Social Fragmentation**: Fragmentation of social networks
- **Trust Erosion**: Erosion of trust between community members
- **Fear**: Community fear of violence
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting entire communities
- **Social Exclusion**: Social exclusion of drug-affected areas

**Economic Impact:**
- **Property Values**: Decreased property values
- **Business Impact**: Negative impact on local businesses
- **Employment**: Reduced employment opportunities
- **Investment**: Reduced investment in affected areas
- **Tourism**: Negative impact on tourism
- **Economic Decline**: General economic decline

**Environmental Impact:**
- **Drug Paraphernalia**: Drug paraphernalia in public spaces
- **Waste**: Improper disposal of drug waste
- **Drug Labs**: Environmental damage from drug labs
- **Pollution**: Pollution from drug production
- **Public Space Degradation**: Degradation of public spaces
- **Quality of Life**: Reduced quality of life

**Public Health Impact:**
- **Overdose Deaths**: High rates of overdose deaths
- **Infectious Disease**: Increased infectious disease rates
- **Mental Health**: Increased mental health issues
- **Healthcare Strain**: Strain on local healthcare services
- **Public Safety**: Reduced public safety
- **Health Disparities**: Health disparities in affected communities

**The Community's Reality:**
- **Fear**: Community fear
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting entire community
- **Resilience**: Community resilience
- **Advocacy**: Community advocacy for change
- **Hope**: Hope for community revitalisation
- **Action**: Community action to address issues
