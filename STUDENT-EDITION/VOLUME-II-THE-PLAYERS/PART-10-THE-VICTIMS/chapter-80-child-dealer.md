# Chapter 80: The Child of the Dealer

## Growing Up in the Drug Trade

Children of dealers grow up in the drug trade environment:

**Exposure to Violence:**
- **Witnessing Violence**: Witnessing drug-related violence
- **Trauma**: Trauma from exposure to violence
- **Fear**: Constant fear for parent's safety
- **Normalisation**: Normalisation of violence
- **Desensitisation**: Desensitisation to violence
- **PTSD**: Risk of PTSD from trauma

**Living Conditions:**
- **Wealth**: Sometimes wealth from drug dealing
- **Instability**: Home and family instability
- **Paranoia**: Paranoia about police and rivals
- **Secrecy**: Culture of secrecy and deception
- **Material Goods**: Material goods from drug money
- **Inconsistent Parenting**: Inconsistent parenting due to drug trade involvement

**Moral Confusion:**
- **Mixed Messages**: Mixed messages about right and wrong
- **Normalisation of Crime**: Normalisation of criminal activity
- **Moral Conflict**: Moral conflict about parent's activities
- **Identity Confusion**: Confusion about identity and values
- **Peer Relationships**: Difficulty with peer relationships
- **Secret Keeping**: Burden of keeping family secrets

**Long-Term Outcomes:**
- **Criminal Justice**: Increased risk of criminal justice involvement
- **Drug Trade**: Risk of entering drug trade
- **Mental Health**: Increased risk of mental health issues
- **Relationship Problems**: Difficulty forming healthy relationships
- **Employment**: Employment difficulties
- **Breaking the Cycle**: Some break the cycle successfully

**The Child's Reality:**
- **Secret Life**: Living with family secrets
- **Fear**: Fear of police and violence
- **Confusion**: Confusion about family activities
- **Loyalty**: Loyalty to family vs. moral values
- **Resilience**: Some develop remarkable resilience
- **Choice**: Choice to continue or break cycle
