# Chapter 87: Human Rights Abuses in the Drug War

## Systemic Violations

The War on Drugs has resulted in widespread human rights abuses:

**Extrajudicial Killings:**
- **Philippines**: Thousands killed in Duterte's drug war
- **Other Countries**: Similar killings in other countries
- **Impunity**: Impunity for perpetrators
- **Targeting**: Targeting of poor communities
- **Lack of Due Process**: No due process or legal proceedings
- **International Condemnation**: International human rights condemnation

**Torture and Abuse:**
- **Police Brutality**: Police brutality against drug suspects
- **Torture**: Torture in detention
- **Forced Confessions**: Forced confessions through torture
- **Inhumane Treatment**: Inhumane treatment in detention
- **Prison Conditions**: Inhumane prison conditions
- **Death Penalty**: Death penalty for drug offenses

**Mass Incarceration:**
- **Disproportionate Impact**: Disproportionate impact on minorities
- **Mandatory Sentences**: Harsh mandatory minimum sentences
- **Three-Strike Laws**: Three-strike laws leading to life sentences
- **Private Prisons**: Profit from mass incarceration
- **Family Separation**: Family separation due to incarceration
- **Collateral Consequences**: Collateral consequences of criminal records

**Forced Treatment:**
- **Involuntary Commitment**: Involuntary commitment to treatment
- **Coerced Rehabilitation**: Coerced rehabilitation programs
- **Human Rights Concerns**: Human rights concerns about forced treatment
- **Quality of Care**: Variable quality of coerced treatment
- **Effectiveness**: Questionable effectiveness of forced treatment
- **Alternatives**: More effective voluntary treatment options

**Drug Testing Violations:**
- **Workplace Testing**: Invasive workplace drug testing
- **School Testing**: Drug testing in schools
- **Welfare Testing**: Drug testing for welfare benefits
- **Privacy Violations**: Privacy violations from drug testing
- **Discrimination**: Discrimination based on drug test results
- **Scientific Validity**: Questionable scientific validity of some tests

**International Human Rights Standards:**
- **UN Human Rights**: UN human rights bodies condemn drug war abuses
- **International Law**: Violations of international law
- **Human Rights Treaties**: Violations of human rights treaties
- **International Pressure**: International pressure for reform
- **Accountability**: Calls for accountability for abuses
- **Reform**: Need for human rights-based drug policy

**The Human Rights Reality:**
- **Systemic Abuse**: Systemic human rights abuses
- **Impunity**: Impunity for perpetrators
- **International Condemnation**: International condemnation
- **Need for Reform**: Urgent need for reform
- **Human Rights Approach**: Human rights-based approach needed
- **Accountability**: Need for accountability
