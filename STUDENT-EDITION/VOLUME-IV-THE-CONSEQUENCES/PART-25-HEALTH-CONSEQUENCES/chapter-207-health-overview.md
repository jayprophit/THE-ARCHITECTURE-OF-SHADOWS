# Chapter 207: Health Consequences Overview

## The Health Impact of Drugs

Drug use has significant health consequences for individuals and populations:

**Acute Health Effects:**
- **Overdose**: Drug overdose as immediate health risk
- **Poisoning**: Poisoning from adulterated substances
- **Accidents**: Accidents while intoxicated
- **Violence**: Violence-related injuries
- **Acute Toxicity**: Acute toxicity from substances
- **Emergency Department Visits**: Emergency department visits for drug-related issues

**Chronic Health Effects:**
- **Organ Damage**: Chronic damage to organs
- **Cardiovascular Effects**: Cardiovascular system effects
- **Respiratory Effects**: Respiratory system effects
- **Neurological Effects**: Neurological damage
- **Mental Health**: Mental health consequences
- **Cognitive Impairment**: Cognitive impairment from chronic use

**Infectious Disease:**
- **HIV**: HIV transmission through drug use
- **Hepatitis C**: Hepatitis C transmission
- **Hepatitis B**: Hepatitis B transmission
- **Other Infections**: Other infectious diseases
- **Injection Drug Use**: Injection drug use risks
- **Sexual Transmission**: Sexual transmission risks

**Mental Health:**
- **Depression**: Depression associated with drug use
- **Anxiety**: Anxiety disorders
- **Psychosis**: Drug-induced psychosis
- **Suicide**: Suicide risk
- **Dual Diagnosis**: Co-occurring mental health disorders
- **Trauma**: Trauma associated with drug use

**The Health Consequences Reality:**
- **Significant**: Significant health impact
- **Varied**: Varied health effects by substance
- **Preventable**: Many consequences preventable
- **Treatment**: Treatment can mitigate some effects
- **Public Health**: Major public health concern
- **Policy Impact**: Policy affects health outcomes
