# Chapter 213: Reproductive Health

## Drug Use and Reproductive Health

Drug use has significant impacts on reproductive health:

**Pregnancy and Drug Use:**
- **Fetal Development**: Impact on fetal development
- **Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome**: NAS from opioid use during pregnancy
- **Preterm Birth**: Increased risk of preterm birth
- **Low Birth Weight**: Increased risk of low birth weight
- **Stillbirth**: Increased risk of stillbirth
- **Miscarriage**: Increased risk of miscarriage

**Specific Substances:**
- **Alcohol**: Fetal alcohol spectrum disorders
- **Opioids**: Neonatal abstinence syndrome
- **Cocaine**: Cardiovascular effects on fetus
- **Methamphetamine**: Developmental effects
- **Cannabis**: Potential developmental effects
- **Tobacco**: Low birth weight, developmental issues

**Male Reproductive Health:**
- **Fertility**: Reduced fertility from drug use
- **Sperm Quality**: Reduced sperm quality
- **Testosterone**: Altered testosterone levels
- **Sexual Function**: Sexual dysfunction
- **Birth Defects**: Potential contribution to birth defects
- **Hormonal Effects**: Hormonal disruption

**Female Reproductive Health:**
- **Menstrual Cycle**: Disruption of menstrual cycle
- **Fertility**: Reduced fertility
- **Hormonal Effects**: Hormonal disruption
- **Sexual Function**: Sexual dysfunction
- **Pregnancy Complications**: Increased pregnancy complications
- **Menopause**: Potential effects on menopause

**Treatment Considerations:**
- **Medication-Assisted Treatment**: MAT during pregnancy
- **Prenatal Care**: Importance of prenatal care
- **Treatment Access**: Access to treatment for pregnant women
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting pregnant drug users
- **Child Welfare**: Child welfare considerations
- **Integrated Care**: Need for integrated care

**The Reproductive Health Reality:**
- **Significant Impact**: Significant impact on reproductive health
- **Preventable**: Many effects preventable
- **Treatment**: Treatment can mitigate some effects
- **Stigma**: Significant stigma affecting pregnant drug users
- **Public Health**: Major public health concern
- **Complex**: Complex issues requiring comprehensive care
