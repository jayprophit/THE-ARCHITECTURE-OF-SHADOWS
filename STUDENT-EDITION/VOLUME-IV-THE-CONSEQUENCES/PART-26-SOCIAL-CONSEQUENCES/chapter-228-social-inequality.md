# Chapter 228: Social Inequality

## Drug Policy and Social Inequality

Drug policy and enforcement disproportionately affect marginalized communities:

**Racial Disparities:**
- **Arrest Disparities**: Racial disparities in drug arrests
- **Sentencing Disparities**: Racial disparities in drug sentencing
- **Incarceration Disparities**: Racial disparities in incarceration rates
- **Stop and Frisk**: Racial disparities in stop and frisk
- **Search Disparities**: Racial disparities in vehicle and person searches
- **Historical Context**: Historical context of racialized drug policy

**Economic Disparities:**
- **Poverty**: Concentration of drug enforcement in poor communities
- **Housing**: Drug enforcement in public housing
- **Employment**: Impact of drug convictions on employment
- **Housing Discrimination**: Housing discrimination for drug convictions
- **Legal Representation**: Inadequate legal representation for poor defendants
- **Fines and Fees**: Fines and fees disproportionately affecting poor

**Geographic Disparities:**
- **Urban vs. Rural**: Different enforcement patterns in urban vs. rural areas
- **Neighborhood Targeting**: Targeting of specific neighborhoods
- **Border Communities**: Impact on border communities
- **Producer Countries**: Impact on drug-producing countries
- **Transit Countries**: Impact on drug-transit countries
- **Consumer Countries**: Impact on consumer countries

**Gender Disparities:**
- **Women and Drug Policy**: Gender disparities in drug policy
- **Pregnant Women**: Specific issues for pregnant drug users
- **Caregiving**: Impact on women as caregivers
- **Treatment Access**: Gender disparities in treatment access
- **Incarceration**: Impact of incarceration on women
- **Violence**: Gender-based violence in drug trade

**The Social Inequality Reality:**
- **Disproportionate**: Disproportionate impact on marginalized communities
- **Systemic**: Systemic inequalities in drug policy
- **Historical**: Historical roots of disparities
- **Reform Needed**: Need for policy reform to address inequalities
- **Social Justice**: Drug policy as social justice issue
- **Equity**: Need for equitable drug policy
