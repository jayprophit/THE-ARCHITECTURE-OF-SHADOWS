# Chapter 10: New Psychoactive Substances (NPS)

## "Legal Highs" and Research Chemicals

New Psychoactive Substances (NPS) are designed to circumvent drug laws:
- **Definition**: Substances not controlled by international drug treaties
- **Research Chemicals**: Originally developed for scientific research
- **Legal Highs**: Marketed as "not for human consumption" to avoid regulation
- **Rapid Evolution**: New variants appear faster than laws can be updated

## The Evolution of NPS

NPS have evolved through several generations:
- **First Wave**: Synthetic cannabinoids (Spice, K2)
- **Second Wave**: Cathinones and stimulants (bath salts)
- **Third Wave**: Synthetic opioids (fentanyl analogues, nitazenes)
- **Fourth Wave**: Novel benzodiazepines and other classes
- **Current Trend**: AI-designed substances with unknown properties

## The Cat-and-Mouse Game with the Law

The relationship between NPS and drug regulation:
- **Legislative Lag**: Laws cannot keep pace with new substances
- **Generic Scheduling**: Some countries use blanket bans on chemical classes
- **International Coordination**: UNODC and EMCDDA monitor emerging threats
- **Market Adaptation**: Manufacturers quickly modify molecular structures
- **Public Health Risk**: Users become unwitting test subjects for novel compounds

## WHO ECDD Scheduling Decisions (2025–2026)

The WHO Expert Committee on Drug Dependence (ECDD) has recommended scheduling several NPS:
- **Critical Review**: Scientific assessment of abuse potential and medical utility
- **International Control**: UN Commission on Narcotic Drugs makes final decisions
- **Recent Actions**: Scheduling of nitazenes, synthetic cannabinoids, and novel opioids
- **Impact**: Restrictions on manufacture, trade, and medical use
