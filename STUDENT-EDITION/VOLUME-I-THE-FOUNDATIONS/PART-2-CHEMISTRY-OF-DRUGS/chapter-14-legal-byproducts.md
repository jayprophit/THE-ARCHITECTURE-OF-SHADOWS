# Chapter 14: Legal Byproducts of Drug Plants

## Hemp: Textiles, Paper, Bioplastics, Construction, Food, Biofuels

**Cannabis sativa** (industrial hemp) has numerous legal uses:
- **Textiles**: Durable fibres for clothing, rope, canvas
- **Paper**: High-quality paper production, historically significant
- **Bioplastics**: Biodegradable plastics from hemp cellulose
- **Construction**: Hempcrete (building material), insulation
- **Food**: Hemp seeds, hemp oil, hemp protein powder
- **Biofuels**: Biodiesel from hemp oil
- **Economic Value**: Multi-billion dollar global industry
- **Legal Status**: Legal in many countries when THC content is below 0.3%

## Coca: Textile Dye, Food, Nutraceuticals, Pharmaceuticals

**Erythroxylum coca** has legitimate uses:
- **Textile Dye**: Red dye from coca leaves
- **Food**: Coca leaf products in Bolivia and Peru (tea, flour)
- **Nutraceuticals**: Coca extracts for energy and nutrition
- **Pharmaceuticals**: Source of cocaine for medical use (local anaesthetic)
- **Traditional Use**: Cultural and religious significance in Andean cultures
- **Legal Status**: Traditional use legal in some countries; cocaine strictly controlled internationally

## Opium Poppy: Seeds, Seed Oil, Traditional Medicinal Uses

**Papaver somniferum** byproducts:
- **Seeds**: Food ingredient (poppy seeds on bagels, pastries)
- **Seed Oil**: Cooking oil, paint, soap production
- **Traditional Medicine**: Historical use in various cultures
- **Legal Status**: Seeds and oil legal; opium strictly controlled
- **Distinction**: Seeds contain negligible opiates; legal to buy and sell

## Kava: Dietary Supplements, Anti-Anxiety

**Piper methysticum** products:
- **Dietary Supplements**: Kava extracts for relaxation and anxiety
- **Traditional Preparation**: Ceremonial drink in Pacific cultures
- **Medical Use**: Treatment for anxiety in some countries
- **Legal Status**: Legal in many countries; restricted in some (EU, Germany)
- **Safety Concerns**: Liver toxicity with heavy use (controversial)

## Khat: Cellulose Fibres, Bio-charcoal

**Catha edulis** byproducts:
- **Cellulose Fibres**: Potential for textile production
- **Bio-charcoal**: Charcoal production from plant material
- **Limited Commercial Use**: Most khat consumed fresh for stimulant effect
- **Legal Status**: Controlled in many countries; legal in some (UK, Israel)

## Iboga: Addiction Treatment Research

**Tabernanthe iboga** potential:
- **Ibogaine**: Psychoactive alkaloid studied for addiction treatment
- **Research**: Investigated for opioid, alcohol, and stimulant addiction
- **Mechanism**: May reset brain receptors and interrupt addiction patterns
- **Legal Status**: Controlled in many countries; research ongoing
- **Traditional Use**: Bwiti religious ceremonies in Central Africa
- **Safety Concerns**: Cardiac risks, requires medical supervision
