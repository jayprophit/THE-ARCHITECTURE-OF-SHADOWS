# Chapter 17: Opioids (Painkillers)

## Effects, Risks, and Addiction Potential

Opioids are powerful pain-relieving drugs that act on opioid receptors in the brain and body.

**Common Opioids:**
- **Heroin**: Illicit semi-synthetic opioid
- **Fentanyl**: Synthetic opioid, extremely potent
- **Nitazenes**: Emerging synthetic opioids
- **Orphines**: New generation synthetic opioids
- **Oxycodone**: Prescription opioid
- **Morphine**: Natural opioid, gold standard for pain
- **Codeine**: Less potent prescription opioid

**Effects:**
- **Physical**: Pain relief, euphoria, respiratory depression, constipation, pinpoint pupils
- **Psychological**: Sedation, contentment, reduced anxiety
- **Cognitive**: Clouded thinking, drowsiness

**Risks:**
- **Respiratory Depression**: Primary cause of opioid overdose death
- **Addiction**: Extremely high addiction potential
- **Overdose**: Easily fatal, especially with potent synthetics
- **Withdrawal**: Severe flu-like symptoms, rarely fatal but extremely uncomfortable
- **Infectious Disease**: Injection drug use increases HIV, hepatitis risk

**The Global Opioid Crisis:**
- **United States**: Over 100,000 overdose deaths annually (2022-2024)
- **United Kingdom**: Rising deaths linked to synthetic opioids
- **Supply Changes**: Shift from heroin to fentanyl and analogues
- **Public Health Emergency**: Declared in multiple countries
- **Policy Responses**: Increased naloxone distribution, treatment expansion, harm reduction
