# Chapter 5: The Drug Trade in Popular Culture

## Films, Books, Music, Art

The drug trade has been a persistent theme in popular culture:

**Films:**
- *Scarface* (1983): Cocaine trade and violence
- *Traffic* (2000): Multi-perspective look at the drug war
- *Blow* (2001): True story of cocaine trafficker George Jung
- *Breaking Bad* (TV Series): Methamphetamine production and distribution
- *Narcos* (TV Series): Pablo Escobar and the Medellín Cartel

**Books:**
- *Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas* by Hunter S. Thompson
- *Junky* by William S. Burroughs
- *Trainspotting* by Irvine Welsh

**Music:**
- References in hip-hop, rock, and electronic music
- Drug culture as inspiration for artistic expression
- The relationship between music scenes and drug use

## How Media Shapes Public Perception

Media representations influence public understanding of drug issues:
- **Glamorisation**: Some portrayals romanticise drug use
- **Dramatisation**: Focus on extreme cases rather than typical experiences
- **Stereotyping**: Reinforcement of racial and class stereotypes
- **Simplification**: Complex issues reduced to good vs. evil narratives

## The Glamorisation vs. The Reality

Popular culture often presents an unrealistic view of the drug trade:
- **Glamorised Version**: Wealth, power, excitement, rebellion
- **Reality**: Violence, addiction, exploitation, imprisonment, death
- The gap between media portrayal and actual consequences contributes to misunderstanding and poor policy decisions
