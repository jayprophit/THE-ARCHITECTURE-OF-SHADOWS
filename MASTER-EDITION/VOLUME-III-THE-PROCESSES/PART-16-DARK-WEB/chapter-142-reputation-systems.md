# Chapter 142: Reputation Systems

## Trust in Anonymous Markets

Reputation systems create trust in anonymous dark web markets:

**Rating Systems:**
- **Vendor Ratings**: Customer ratings of vendors
- **Product Ratings**: Customer ratings of products
- **Five-Star Ratings**: Five-star rating systems
- **Written Reviews**: Written reviews from customers
- **Feedback Scores**: Aggregate feedback scores
- **Transaction History**: Transaction history for vendors

**Trust Mechanisms:**
- **Escrow**: Escrow services protect buyers
- **Vendor Bond**: Vendor bonds ensure reliability
- **Verification**: Vendor verification systems
- **Dispute Resolution**: Dispute resolution for failed transactions
- **Forum Discussions**: Forum discussions about vendors
- **Community Policing**: Community policing of bad actors

**Reputation Importance:**
- **Sales**: Higher reputation leads to more sales
- **Premium Pricing**: High-reputation vendors can charge premium prices
- **Customer Loyalty**: Customers return to trusted vendors
- **Market Position**: Reputation determines market position
- **Trust**: Reputation is currency in anonymous markets
- **Competition**: Competitive marketplace based on reputation

**Reputation Manipulation:**
- **Fake Reviews**: Some vendors create fake reviews
- **Review Buying**: Buying positive reviews
- **Competitor Sabotage**: Sabotaging competitors with negative reviews
- **Exit Scams**: High-reputation vendors sometimes exit scam
- **Account Takeover**: Taking over high-reputation accounts
- **Market Manipulation**: Manipulation of reputation systems

**The Reputation Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential for market function
- **Trust-Based**: Trust-based system in anonymous environment
- **Manipulable**: Vulnerable to manipulation
- **Valuable**: Reputation is valuable asset
- **Community-Driven**: Community-driven system
- **Imperfect**: Imperfect but functional system
