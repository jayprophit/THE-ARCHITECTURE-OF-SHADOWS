# Chapter 169: Violence Prevention

## Strategies to Reduce Drug-Related Violence

Violence prevention requires comprehensive strategies:

**Policy Approaches:**
- **Drug Legalisation**: Legalisation to eliminate illicit market
- **Decriminalisation**: Decriminalisation to reduce enforcement violence
- **Regulation**: Regulation of drug markets
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction approaches
- **Treatment Expansion**: Expansion of treatment services
- **Social Investment**: Investment in affected communities

**Community Approaches:**
- **Community Policing**: Community-based policing
- **Social Programs**: Social programs for at-risk youth
- **Education**: Education programs
- **Economic Development**: Economic development in affected areas
- **Community Organising**: Community organising against violence
- **Mediation**: Mediation of conflicts

**Law Enforcement Approaches:**
- **Intelligence-Led Policing**: Intelligence-led policing targeting violence
- **Violence Reduction Strategies**: Violence reduction strategies
- **Gang Intervention**: Gang intervention programs
- **Hot Spot Policing**: Targeted policing of violence hot spots
- **Community Engagement**: Engagement with communities
- **Accountability**: Accountability for police violence

**International Approaches:**
- **International Cooperation**: International cooperation against cartels
- **Arms Control**: Arms control to reduce weapons availability
- **Anti-Corruption**: Anti-corruption efforts
- **Development Assistance**: Development assistance to producer countries
- **Alternative Livelihoods**: Alternative livelihood programs
- **Regional Security**: Regional security cooperation

**The Prevention Reality:**
- **Complex**: Complex problem requiring multi-faceted approach
- **Policy-Dependent**: Dependent on policy choices
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based approaches most effective
- **Long-Term**: Long-term commitment required
- **Comprehensive**: Requires comprehensive approach
- **Prohibition-Related**: Much violence is prohibition-related
