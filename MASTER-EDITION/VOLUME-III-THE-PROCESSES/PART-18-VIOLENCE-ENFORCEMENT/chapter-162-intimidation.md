# Chapter 162: Intimidation and Terror

## Psychological Violence in the Drug Trade

Intimidation and terror are used to control populations:

**Types of Intimidation:**
- **Public Displays**: Public displays of violence
- **Threats**: Direct threats to individuals and communities
- **Extortion**: Extortion of businesses and individuals
- **Forced Displacement**: Forced displacement of populations
- **Recruitment**: Forced recruitment into criminal organisations
- **Social Control**: Social control through fear

**Methods of Intimidation:**
- **Public Executions**: Public executions as intimidation
- **Body Displays**: Display of bodies as intimidation
- **Graffiti**: Graffiti marking territory
- **Threats**: Direct threats to individuals
- **Family Targeting**: Targeting family members
- **Community Targeting**: Targeting entire communities

**Psychological Impact:**
- **Fear**: Widespread fear in affected communities
- **Trauma**: Psychological trauma from intimidation
- **Displacement**: Displacement due to fear
- **Silence**: Silence due to fear of retaliation
- **Compliance**: Compliance through fear
- **Mental Health**: Mental health impacts

**Community Impact:**
- **Social Fragmentation**: Fragmentation of social fabric
- **Economic Impact**: Economic impact through extortion
- **Education Disruption**: Disruption of education
- **Healthcare Disruption**: Disruption of healthcare
- **Migration**: Migration due to fear
- **Community Breakdown**: Breakdown of community structures

**The Intimidation Reality:**
- **Effective**: Effective method of control
- **Psychological**: Psychological warfare
- **Widespread**: Widespread impact on communities
- **Devastating**: Devastating psychological impact
- **Control**: Control through fear
- **Long-Term**: Long-term psychological consequences
