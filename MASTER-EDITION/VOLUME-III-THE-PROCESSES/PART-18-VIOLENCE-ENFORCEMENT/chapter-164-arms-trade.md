# Chapter 164: The Arms Trade

## Weapons in the Drug Trade

The drug trade drives an illicit arms trade:

**Types of Weapons:**
- **Firearms**: Handguns, rifles, shotguns
- **Automatic Weapons**: Automatic rifles and machine guns
- **Explosives**: Explosives and bombs
- **Military Weapons**: Military-grade weapons
- **Ammunition**: Ammunition for all weapon types
- **Accessories**: Weapon accessories and equipment

**Sources of Weapons:**
- **Theft**: Theft from military and police stockpiles
- **Corruption**: Corrupt officials selling weapons
- **Straw Purchases**: Legal purchases for illegal purposes
- **International Trafficking**: International arms trafficking
- **Domestic Production**: Domestic production of illicit weapons
- **Capture**: Capture of weapons from rival groups

**Arms Trafficking Routes:**
- **International Routes**: International arms trafficking routes
- **Regional Routes**: Regional arms distribution
- **Domestic Routes**: Domestic arms distribution
- **Border Crossings**: Border crossings for arms
- **Maritime Routes**: Maritime arms trafficking
- **Air Routes**: Air transport of weapons

**Impact on Violence:**
- **Escalation**: Escalation of violence through better weapons
- **Military-Grade Violence**: Military-grade violence in drug conflicts
- **Increased Lethality**: Increased lethality of conflicts
- **Collateral Damage**: Increased collateral damage
- **State Response**: State military response to armed cartels
- **Arms Race**: Arms race between cartels and state

**The Arms Trade Reality:**
- **Symbiotic**: Symbiotic relationship between drug and arms trade
- **Escalating**: Escalating arms race
- **International**: International arms trafficking
- **Devastating**: Devastating impact on violence levels
- **Profit-Driven**: Profit-driven arms trade
- **Systemic**: Systemic issue requiring systemic solutions
