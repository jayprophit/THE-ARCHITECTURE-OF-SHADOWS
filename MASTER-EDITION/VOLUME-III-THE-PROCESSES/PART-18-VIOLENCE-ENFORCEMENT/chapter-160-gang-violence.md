# Chapter 160: Gang Violence

## Street Gang Violence in Drug Markets

Street gangs use violence to control local drug markets:

**Types of Gang Violence:**
- **Territorial Violence**: Violence over control of territories
- **Market Violence**: Violence over drug market control
- **Retaliatory Violence**: Violence in retaliation for perceived wrongs
- **Internal Violence**: Violence within gang structures
- **Cross-Gang Violence**: Violence between rival gangs
- **Collateral Violence**: Violence affecting bystanders

**Gang Characteristics:**
- **Territorial Control**: Control of specific territories
- **Hierarchical Structure**: Hierarchical gang structures
- **Drug Sales**: Primary income from drug sales
- **Violent Enforcement**: Violent enforcement of market control
- **Recruitment**: Recruitment of young members
- **Community Presence**: Presence in local communities

**Violence Methods:**
- **Shootings**: Shootings of rival gang members
- **Assaults**: Physical assaults
- **Intimidation**: Intimidation of community members
- **Drive-By Shootings**: Drive-by shootings
- **Torture**: Torture for information and discipline
- **Murder**: Murder of rivals and informants

**Geographic Patterns:**
- **Urban Areas**: Concentrated in urban areas
- **Inner Cities**: Inner city neighborhoods
- **Public Housing**: Public housing developments
- **Border Cities**: Border cities with drug trafficking
- **Suburban Areas**: Spreading to suburban areas
- **Rural Areas**: Some rural gang presence

**The Gang Violence Reality:**
- **Local**: Local control of drug markets
- **Territorial**: Territorial violence over markets
- **Community Impact**: Significant impact on communities
- **Youth Involvement**: Heavy youth involvement
- **Cycle of Violence**: Self-perpetuating cycle of violence
- **Systemic**: Systemic issue requiring systemic solutions
