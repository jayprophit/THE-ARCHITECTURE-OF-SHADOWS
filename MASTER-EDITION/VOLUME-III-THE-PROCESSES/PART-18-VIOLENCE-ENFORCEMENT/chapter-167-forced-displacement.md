# Chapter 167: Forced Displacement

## Displacement Due to Drug Violence

Drug violence forces mass displacement of populations:

**Causes of Displacement:**
- **Violence**: Direct violence forcing people to flee
- **Intimidation**: Intimidation forcing displacement
- **Extortion**: Extortion making life unsustainable
- **Territorial Control**: Territorial control forcing displacement
- **State Operations**: State operations causing displacement
- **Economic Collapse**: Economic collapse due to violence

**Types of Displacement:**
- **Internal Displacement**: Internal displacement within countries
- **Cross-Border Displacement**: Cross-border displacement
- **Urban Displacement**: Displacement within urban areas
- **Rural Displacement**: Displacement from rural areas
- **Temporary Displacement**: Temporary displacement
- **Permanent Displacement**: Permanent displacement

**Impact on Displaced Populations:**
- **Trauma**: Psychological trauma from displacement
- **Poverty**: Slide into poverty after displacement
- **Loss of Home**: Loss of homes and property
- **Family Separation**: Family separation during displacement
- **Limited Resources**: Limited resources for displaced populations
- **Vulnerability**: Increased vulnerability to exploitation

**Geographic Patterns:**
- **Mexico**: Displacement due to cartel violence
- **Colombia**: Historic displacement due to drug conflict
- **Central America**: Displacement due to gang violence
- **Other Regions**: Displacement in other affected regions
- **Urban Areas**: Displacement within urban areas
- **Rural Areas**: Displacement from rural production areas

**The Displacement Reality:**
- **Massive Scale**: Massive scale of displacement
- **Humanitarian Crisis**: Humanitarian crisis
- **Traumatic**: Traumatic experience for displaced
- **Long-Term**: Long-term consequences
- **Prohibition-Related**: Exacerbated by prohibition
- **International Response**: Limited international response
