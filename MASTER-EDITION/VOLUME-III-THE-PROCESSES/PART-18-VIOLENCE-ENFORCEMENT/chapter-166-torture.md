# Chapter 166: Torture

## Torture in the Drug Trade

Torture is used for information, discipline, and intimidation:

**Purposes of Torture:**
- **Information Extraction**: Extracting information from rivals
- **Discipline**: Disciplining members of organisations
- **Intimidation**: Intimidating communities and rivals
- **Punishment**: Punishment for perceived wrongs
- **Public Display**: Public display as intimidation
- **Recruitment Enforcement**: Enforcing recruitment compliance

**Torture Methods:**
- **Physical Torture**: Physical pain and injury
- **Psychological Torture**: Psychological torture and trauma
- **Sexual Violence**: Sexual violence as torture
- **Forced Drug Administration**: Forced drug administration
- **Deprivation**: Deprivation of needs
- **Public Torture**: Public torture as display

**Documented Cases:**
- **Cartel Torture**: Extreme torture by cartels
- **Gang Torture**: Torture by street gangs
- **State Torture**: Torture by state actors
- **Historical Cases**: Historical cases of torture
- **Ongoing Cases**: Ongoing torture in drug conflicts
- **International Condemnation**: International condemnation of torture

**Impact on Victims:**
- **Physical Trauma**: Severe physical trauma
- **Psychological Trauma**: Severe psychological trauma
- **PTSD**: Post-traumatic stress disorder
- **Disability**: Permanent disability from torture
- **Death**: Death from torture
- **Long-Term Suffering**: Long-term suffering

**The Torture Reality:**
- **Systematic**: Systematic use of torture
- **Extreme**: Extreme torture methods
- **Widespread**: Widespread use in drug trade
- **Devastating**: Devastating impact on victims
- **Prohibition-Related**: Exacerbated by prohibition
- **Human Rights**: Severe human rights violations
