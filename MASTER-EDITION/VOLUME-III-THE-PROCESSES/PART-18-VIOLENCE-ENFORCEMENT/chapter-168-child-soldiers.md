# Chapter 168: Child Soldiers

## Children in Drug-Related Violence

Children are recruited into drug-related violence:

**Recruitment Methods:**
- **Forced Recruitment**: Forced recruitment by armed groups
- **Economic Recruitment**: Economic necessity driving recruitment
- **Social Recruitment**: Social pressure to join groups
- **Family Recruitment**: Family members in groups
- **School Recruitment**: Recruitment from schools
- **Street Recruitment**: Recruitment of street children

**Roles of Child Soldiers:**
- **Combatants**: Children used as combatants
- **Lookouts**: Children used as lookouts
- **Couriers**: Children used as couriers
- **Intelligence**: Children used for intelligence gathering
- **Support Roles**: Support roles in organisations
- **Sexual Exploitation**: Sexual exploitation of children

**Impact on Children:**
- **Trauma**: Severe psychological trauma
- **Physical Harm**: Physical harm and injury
- **Education Disruption**: Disruption of education
- **Normal Development**: Disruption of normal development
- **Desensitisation**: Desensitisation to violence
- **Long-Term Consequences**: Long-term psychological consequences

**Geographic Patterns:**
- **Mexico**: Child soldiers in Mexican cartels
- **Colombia**: Historic use of child soldiers
- **Central America**: Child soldiers in gangs
- **Other Regions**: Child soldiers in other conflicts
- **Urban Areas**: Urban recruitment of children
- **Rural Areas**: Rural recruitment of children

**The Child Soldier Reality:**
- **Tragic**: Tragic exploitation of children
- **Systemic**: Systemic recruitment of children
- **Devastating**: Devastating impact on children
- **International Crime**: International crime
- **Prohibition-Related**: Exacerbated by prohibition
- **Long-Term Impact**: Long-term impact on societies
