# Chapter 159: Cartel Violence

## Organised Violence by Drug Cartels

Cartels use systematic violence to maintain control:

**Types of Cartel Violence:**
- **Territorial Violence**: Violence to control territories
- **Competitor Elimination**: Elimination of competing cartels
- **Internal Discipline**: Violent discipline of members
- **State Violence**: Violence against state actors
- **Public Violence**: Public displays of violence
- **Collateral Violence**: Violence affecting civilians

**Violence Methods:**
- **Assassinations**: Targeted assassinations of rivals
- **Massacres**: Massacres of competing groups
- **Torture**: Torture for information and intimidation
- **Beheadings**: Beheadings as public displays
- **Disappearances**: Forced disappearances
- **Bombings**: Bombings of rival territories

**Strategic Violence:**
- **Territorial Control**: Violence to control territories
- **Market Monopoly**: Violence to maintain market monopoly
- **Intimidation**: Violence to intimidate communities
- **Reputation Building**: Violence as reputation building
- **State Response**: Violence in response to state action
- **Recruitment**: Violence to enforce recruitment

**Major Cartels and Violence:**
- **Sinaloa Cartel**: Major violence in Mexico
- **Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG)**: Escalating violence
- **Los Zetas**: Extreme violence and terror tactics
- **Gulf Cartel**: Historic violence in Mexico
- **Colombian Cartels**: Historic violence in Colombia
- **Other Cartels**: Various cartels using violence

**The Cartel Violence Reality:**
- **Systematic**: Systematic use of violence
- **Extreme**: Extreme violence methods
- **Strategic**: Strategic use of violence
- **Devastating**: Devastating impact on communities
- **State Response**: State response often escalates violence
- **Prohibition-Related**: Exacerbated by prohibition
