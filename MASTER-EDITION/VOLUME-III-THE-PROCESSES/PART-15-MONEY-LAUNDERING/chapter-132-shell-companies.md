# Chapter 132: Shell Companies

## Corporate Structures for Hiding Ownership

Shell companies hide the true ownership of assets and money:

**Characteristics of Shell Companies:**
- **No Operations**: No legitimate business operations
- **Nominal Value**: Nominal value only
- **Ownership Hiding**: Hide true beneficial ownership
- **Asset Holding**: Hold assets on behalf of hidden owners
- **Transaction Facilitation**: Facilitate transactions
- **Jurisdiction Shopping**: Established in favourable jurisdictions

**Types of Shell Companies:**
- **International Business Companies (IBCs)**: Offshore companies
- **Limited Liability Companies (LLCs)**: LLCs in favourable jurisdictions
- **Trusts**: Trust structures hiding ownership
- **Foundations**: Foundation structures hiding ownership
- **Partnerships**: Partnership structures hiding ownership
- **Corporate Stacks**: Multiple layers of companies

**Ownership Hiding:**
- **Nominee Directors**: Nominee directors hiding true owners
- **Bearer Shares**: Bearer shares hiding ownership
- **Trust Structures**: Trusts hiding beneficial ownership
- **Foundation Structures**: Foundations hiding ownership
- **Corporate Stacks**: Multiple corporate layers hiding ownership
- **Nominee Shareholders**: Nominee shareholders hiding true owners

**Jurisdictional Advantages:**
- **Secrecy Laws**: Laws protecting corporate secrecy
- **Tax Advantages**: Low or no taxation
- **Regulatory Advantages**: Minimal regulation
- **Political Stability**: Political stability
- **Legal System**: Favourable legal system
- **International Recognition**: International recognition

**The Shell Company Reality:**
- **Essential Tool**: Essential tool for money laundering
- **Legal**: Legitimate corporate structures used for illicit purposes
- **Complex**: Complex corporate structures
- **International**: International scope
- **Effective**: Effective at hiding ownership
- **Regulatory Pressure**: Increasing regulatory pressure on shell companies
