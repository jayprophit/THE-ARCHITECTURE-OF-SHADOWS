# Chapter 152: Dead Drops

## Physical Message and Product Exchange

Dead drops are physical locations for exchanging messages or products:

**Dead Drop Characteristics:**
- **Physical Location**: Physical location for exchange
- **No Direct Contact**: No direct contact between parties
- **Asynchronous Exchange**: Asynchronous exchange
- **Pre-Arranged**: Pre-arranged location and timing
- **Concealment**: Concealed from public view
- **One-Way or Two-Way**: Can be one-way or two-way exchange

**Types of Dead Drops:**
- **Message Dead Drops**: Dead drops for messages only
- **Product Dead Drops**: Dead drops for drug products
- **Money Dead Drops**: Dead drops for money exchange
- **Key Dead Drops**: Dead drops for keys or access codes
- **Digital Dead Drops**: Digital dead drops (USB drives, etc.)
- **Multi-Use**: Dead drops used multiple times

**Location Selection:**
- **Public Places**: Public places with low foot traffic
- **Private Locations**: Private locations with controlled access
- **Temporary Locations**: Temporary locations for single use
- **Permanent Locations**: Permanent locations for repeated use
- **Urban Locations**: Urban dead drops in cities
- **Rural Locations**: Rural dead drops in remote areas

**Security Considerations:**
- **Surveillance**: Risk of surveillance
- **Discovery**: Risk of discovery by third parties
- **Weather**: Weather affecting dead drop
- **Access Control**: Controlling access to dead drop
- **Timing**: Careful timing to avoid detection
- **Cleanup**: Cleanup after use

**The Dead Drop Reality:**
- **Traditional**: Traditional method for illicit exchange
- **Risk**: High risk of discovery
- **Asynchronous**: Asynchronous exchange reduces direct contact risk
- **Skill**: Requires skill and planning
- **Declining**: Declining with digital alternatives
- **Still Used**: Still used in certain contexts
