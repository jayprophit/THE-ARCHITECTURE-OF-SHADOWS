# Chapter 148: Encryption Overview

## Protecting Communications

Encryption protects drug trade communications from surveillance:

**Encryption Basics:**
- **Cryptography**: Science of encoding and decoding information
- **Symmetric Encryption**: Same key for encryption and decryption
- **Asymmetric Encryption**: Public key and private key system
- **End-to-End Encryption**: Encryption that only sender and recipient can decrypt
- **Perfect Forward Secrecy**: Compromised keys don't compromise past communications
- **Zero-Knowledge**: No knowledge of communication content by service providers

**Encryption Standards:**
- **AES**: Advanced Encryption Standard
- **RSA**: Rivest-Shamir-Adleman encryption
- **PGP**: Pretty Good Privacy encryption
- **Signal Protocol**: Signal protocol for encrypted messaging
- **TLS**: Transport Layer Security for internet communication
- **Quantum-Resistant**: Emerging quantum-resistant encryption

**Encryption Tools:**
- **PGP/GPG**: PGP/GPG for email encryption
- **Signal**: Signal for encrypted messaging
- **WhatsApp**: WhatsApp encryption (with concerns)
- **Telegram**: Telegram encryption
- **ProtonMail**: Encrypted email service
- **Tails**: Operating system with encryption tools

**Encryption Challenges:**
- **User Error**: User errors compromising encryption
- **Key Management**: Key management challenges
- **Compromised Devices**: Compromised devices bypass encryption
- **Backdoors**: Potential backdoors in encryption software
- **Quantum Computing**: Future threat from quantum computing
- **Law Enforcement Access**: Law enforcement seeking encryption backdoors

**The Encryption Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential for drug trade security
- **Widely Used**: Widely used across drug trade
- **Effective**: Generally effective against surveillance
- **Not Perfect**: Not perfect, vulnerable to various attacks
- **Cat and Mouse**: Constant cat and mouse with law enforcement
- **Future**: Future challenges from quantum computing
