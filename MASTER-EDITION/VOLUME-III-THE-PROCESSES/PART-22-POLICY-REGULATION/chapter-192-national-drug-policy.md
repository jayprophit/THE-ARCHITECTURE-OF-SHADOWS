# Chapter 192: National Drug Policy

## Country-Level Drug Policy Approaches

National drug policies vary significantly between countries:

**Policy Approaches:**
- **Prohibition**: Strict prohibition and criminalisation
- **Decriminalisation**: Decriminalisation of personal possession
- **Legalisation**: Legal and regulated markets
- **Harm Reduction**: Emphasis on harm reduction
- **Medical Model**: Medical approach to drug use
- **Mixed Approaches**: Combination of approaches

**Prohibitionist Countries:**
- **United States**: Prohibitionist approach with harsh penalties
- **Southeast Asia**: Harsh penalties including death penalty
- **Middle East**: Prohibitionist with severe penalties
- **Other Countries**: Various prohibitionist approaches
- **Enforcement Focus**: Focus on law enforcement
- **Limited Treatment**: Limited treatment emphasis

**Decriminalisation Countries:**
- **Portugal**: Pioneering decriminalisation model
- **Netherlands**: De facto decriminalisation of cannabis
- **Other European Countries**: Various decriminalisation approaches
- **Latin America**: Some decriminalisation efforts
- **Focus on Treatment**: Emphasis on treatment over punishment
- **Reduced Criminal Justice Impact**: Reduced criminal justice system involvement

**Legalisation Countries:**
- **Uruguay**: First country to legalise cannabis
- **Canada**: Legal cannabis market
- **United States**: State-level legalisation in some states
- **Thailand**: Cannabis legalisation
- **Other Countries**: Emerging legalisation efforts
- **Regulated Markets**: Regulated legal markets

**Harm Reduction Countries:**
- **European Countries**: Strong harm reduction emphasis
- **Australia**: Harm reduction approaches
- **Canada**: Harm reduction alongside legalisation
- **Supervised Consumption Sites**: Supervised consumption sites
- **Syringe Exchange**: Syringe exchange programs
- **Health Focus**: Public health focus

**The National Policy Reality:**
- **Variation**: Significant variation between countries
- **Evolution**: Policies evolving over time
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based policies emerging
- **Debate**: Ongoing debate about best approaches
- **International Influence**: International influence on national policies
- **Policy Learning**: Countries learning from each other
