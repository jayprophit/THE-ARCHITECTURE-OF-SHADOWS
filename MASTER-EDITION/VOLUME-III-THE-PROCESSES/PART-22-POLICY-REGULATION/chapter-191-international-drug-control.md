# Chapter 191: International Drug Control

## Global Drug Policy Framework

International drug control provides the framework for global drug policy:

**International Treaties:**
- **1961 Single Convention**: Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs
- **1971 Convention**: Convention on Psychotropic Substances
- **1988 Convention**: Convention Against Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs
- **UN Drug Conventions**: United Nations drug control conventions
- **Regional Conventions**: Regional drug control agreements
- **Bilateral Agreements**: Bilateral drug control agreements

**International Bodies:**
- **UNODC**: United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime
- **CND**: Commission on Narcotic Drugs
- **INCB**: International Narcotics Control Board
- **WHO**: World Health Organization role in drug scheduling
- **UN General Assembly**: UN General Assembly special sessions on drugs
- **Regional Bodies**: Regional drug control bodies

**Drug Scheduling:**
- **UN Schedules**: UN drug scheduling system
- **Schedule I**: Most restrictive schedule
- **Schedule II-IV**: Less restrictive schedules
- **Scheduling Process**: Process for scheduling substances
- **Medical vs. Non-Medical**: Distinction between medical and non-medical use
- **Review Process**: Periodic review of scheduled substances

**International Cooperation:**
- **Technical Assistance**: Technical assistance to countries
- **Capacity Building**: Capacity building for drug control
- **Information Sharing**: Sharing of drug control information
- **Best Practices**: Sharing of best practices
- **Funding**: Funding for drug control programs
- **Evaluation**: Evaluation of drug control efforts

**The International Control Reality:**
- **Prohibition-Based**: Based on prohibition model
- **Controversial**: Controversial effectiveness
- **Reform Pressure**: Pressure for reform
- **Balancing Act**: Balancing control and public health
- **Global Framework**: Global framework for national policies
- **Evolving**: Evolving approach to drug control
