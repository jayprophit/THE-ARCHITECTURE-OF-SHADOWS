# Chapter 109: The Cutting and Dilution Process

## Adulterating Drugs for Profit

Cutting and dilution increases drug volume and profits:

**Cutting Agents:**
- **Inert Fillers**: Lactose, mannitol, inositol
- **Active Cutters**: Paracetamol, caffeine, lidocaine
- **Bulking Agents**: Baking soda, sugars
- **Local Anaesthetics**: Lidocaine, benzocaine
- **Other Drugs**: Mixing with other drugs
- **Unknown Substances**: Sometimes unknown or dangerous substances

**Cutting Methods:**
- **Physical Mixing**: Blending powders together
- **Solution Mixing**: Dissolving and recrystallising
- **Layering**: Creating layers of pure and cut product
- **Spraying**: Liquid cutting agents sprayed onto plant material
- **Micro-dosing**: Distributing potent substances throughout large quantities
- **Industrial Cutting**: Industrial-scale cutting operations

**Quality Control Issues:**
- **Uneven Mixing**: Uneven mixing creates dangerous hotspots
- **Contamination**: Contamination during cutting process
- **Unknown Substances**: Sometimes unknown cutting agents used
- **Variable Potency**: Variable potency across batches
- **No Quality Control**: No quality control in illicit cutting
- **Dangerous Additives**: Sometimes dangerous additives used

**Economic Incentives:**
- **Volume Increase**: Cutting increases volume
- **Profit Maximisation**: Maximises profit margins
- **Market Competition**: Competition drives cutting
- **Consumer Demand**: Consumer demand for lower prices
- **Supply Chain**: Each level may cut further
- **Final Product**: Street-level product often heavily cut

**Health Risks:**
- **Hotspots**: Dangerous concentrations of active drug
- **Toxic Additives**: Toxic cutting agents
- **Allergic Reactions**: Allergic reactions to cutting agents
- **Unknown Effects**: Unknown effects of cutting agents
- **Variable Potency**: Variable potency increases overdose risk
- **Contamination**: Contamination during cutting

**The Cutting Reality:**
- **Standard Practice**: Standard practice in drug trade
- **Profit-Driven**: Driven by profit maximisation
- **Dangerous**: Creates significant health risks
- **Unregulated**: No regulation of cutting practices
- **Consumer Risk**: Consumers bear the risk
- **Quality Unknown**: Quality unknown to consumers
