# Chapter 122: Cutting Agent Detection

## Identifying Adulterants

Cutting agent detection identifies dangerous adulterants:

**Detection Methods:**
- **Reagent Testing**: Some reagents detect cutting agents
- **Laboratory Analysis**: Laboratory analysis identifies cutting agents
- **Spectroscopy**: Spectroscopic analysis identifies substances
- **Chromatography**: Chromatography separates and identifies components
- **Mass Spectrometry**: Mass spectrometry identifies molecular composition
- **Visual Inspection**: Visual inspection for obvious adulteration

**Common Cutting Agents:**
- **Inert Fillers**: Lactose, mannitol, inositol
- **Active Cutters**: Paracetamol, caffeine, lidocaine
- **Bulking Agents**: Baking soda, sugars
- **Local Anaesthetics**: Lidocaine, benzocaine
- **Dangerous Substances**: Fentanyl, levamisole, other dangerous adulterants
- **Unknown Substances**: Sometimes unknown substances

**Health Risks of Cutting Agents:**
- **Toxicity**: Some cutting agents are toxic
- **Allergic Reactions**: Allergic reactions to cutting agents
- **Unknown Effects**: Unknown effects of novel cutting agents
- **Hotspots**: Uneven mixing creates dangerous concentrations
- **Contamination**: Contamination during cutting process
- **Synergistic Effects**: Dangerous interactions with active drug

**Harm Reduction:**
- **Drug Checking**: Drug checking services detect cutting agents
- **Information**: Information about common cutting agents
- **Testing Kits**: Home testing kits for cutting agents
- **Laboratory Services**: Laboratory analysis services
- **Community Knowledge**: Community knowledge about cutting agents
- **Warning Systems**: Warning systems about dangerous adulterants

**The Cutting Agent Reality:**
- **Standard Practice**: Standard practice in drug trade
- **Dangerous**: Creates significant health risks
- **Variable**: Variable cutting agents and quality
- **Detection Challenge**: Challenging to detect all cutting agents
- **Harm Reduction**: Growing harm reduction efforts
- **Consumer Risk**: Consumers bear the risk
