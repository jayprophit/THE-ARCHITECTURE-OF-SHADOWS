# Chapter 123: Dosage Measurement

## Measuring Safe and Effective Doses

Accurate dosage measurement is critical for safety:

**Dosage Units:**
- **Milligrams (mg)**: Common unit for potent substances
- **Micrograms (μg)**: Unit for extremely potent substances (LSD, fentanyl)
- **Grams (g)**: Unit for less potent substances (cannabis)
- **Milliliters (ml)**: Liquid dosage measurement
- **Drops**: Liquid dosage measurement
- **Units**: Some substances measured in units (insulin, etc.)

**Measurement Methods:**
- **Precision Scales**: Precision scales for accurate measurement
- **Volumetric Measurement**: Volumetric measurement for liquids
- **Dropper Bottles**: Dropper bottles for liquid dosing
- **Capsules**: Pre-measured capsules
- **Pre-Dosed Products**: Pre-dosed products (edibles, etc.)
- **Visual Estimation**: Risky visual estimation (not recommended)

**Dosage Guidelines:**
- **Threshold Dose**: Minimum dose to feel effects
- **Common Dose**: Typical recreational dose
- **Strong Dose**: Higher dose for experienced users
- **Heavy Dose**: Very high dose (not recommended)
- **Overdose Dose**: Dose that causes overdose
- **Lethal Dose**: Dose that can cause death

**Substance-Specific Dosages:**
- **MDMA**: 75-150mg typical dose
- **LSD**: 50-200μg typical dose
- **Psilocybin**: 1-3g dried mushrooms typical dose
- **Fentanyl**: 50μg can be lethal
- **Cannabis**: Variable dosage depending on tolerance
- **Cocaine**: Variable dosage depending on purity

**Dosage Risks:**
- **Overdose**: Overdose from too high dose
- **Variable Potency**: Variable potency makes dosing difficult
- **Unknown Substances**: Unknown substances make dosing impossible
- **Tolerance**: Tolerance affects dosage requirements
- **Individual Variation**: Individual variation in sensitivity
- **Polydrug Use**: Combined substances increase risk

**The Dosage Reality:**
- **Critical**: Accurate dosing is critical for safety
- **Challenging**: Challenging in illicit market
- **Variable**: Variable potency makes dosing difficult
- **Risk**: High risk of overdose with inaccurate dosing
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction emphasises accurate dosing
- **Education**: Education about safe dosing essential
