# Chapter 119: Street Slang for Weights

## Drug Trade Terminology

Street slang for drug weights varies by region and substance:

**Common Slang Terms:**
- **Gram**: Often called "g" or "gram"
- **Eighth**: 3.5 grams, called "eighth" or "half-eighth" (1.75g)
- **Quarter**: 7 grams, called "quarter" or "Q"
- **Half Ounce**: 14 grams, called "half" or "half-O"
- **Ounce**: 28 grams, called "ounce" or "O"
- **Key**: Kilogram, called "key" or "kilo"

**Regional Variations:**
- **UK**: "Teener" (1.7g), "Half-gram" (0.5g)
- **US**: "Dime bag" (varies), "Nickel bag" (varies)
- **Australia**: Various regional terms
- **Europe**: Regional variations across countries
- **Urban vs. Rural**: Different slang in urban vs. rural areas

**Substance-Specific Slang:**
- **Cannabis**: "Eighth", "quarter", "ounce", "QP" (quarter pound)
- **Cocaine**: "8-ball" (3.5g), "gram", "ounce"
- **Heroin**: "Bundle" (varies), "gram", "stamp bag"
- **MDMA**: "Point" (0.1g), "gram", "half-gram"
- **Methamphetamine**: Similar to cocaine slang

**Wholesale Slang:**
- **Kilo**: "Key", "brick", "cake"
- **Multi-Kilo**: "Multiple keys", "weight"
- **Pound**: "P", "lb", "pack"
- **Multi-Pound**: "Multiple packs", "heavy weight"

**The Slang Reality:**
- **Regional**: Highly regional variation
- **Evolving**: Constantly evolving slang
- **Code**: Code to avoid detection
- **Cultural**: Cultural significance of slang
- **Confusing**: Confusing for outsiders
- **Essential**: Essential for drug trade communication
