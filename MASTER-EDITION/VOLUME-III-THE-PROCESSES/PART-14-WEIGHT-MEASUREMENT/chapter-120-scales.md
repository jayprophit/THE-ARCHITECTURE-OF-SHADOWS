# Chapter 120: Scales and Measurement Devices

## Tools for Measuring Drugs

Precision scales are essential tools in the drug trade:

**Types of Scales:**
- **Digital Scales**: Digital precision scales
- **Pocket Scales**: Small portable scales
- **Jewelry Scales**: High-precision jewelry scales
- **Industrial Scales**: Large industrial scales
- **Mechanical Scales**: Traditional mechanical scales
- **Analytical Scales**: Laboratory-grade analytical scales

**Precision Requirements:**
- **Gram-Level**: Precision to 0.1g for retail sales
- **Milligram-Level**: Precision to 0.01g or 0.001g for potent substances
- **Kilogram-Level**: Precision to 1g for wholesale
- **Calibration**: Regular calibration required
- **Quality Control**: Quality control requires precision
- **Legal Evidence**: Legal evidence requires accurate measurement

**Scale Features:**
- **Tare Function**: Tare function for container weight
- **Auto-Off**: Auto-off to preserve battery
- **Backlight**: Backlight for visibility
- **Protective Cover**: Protective cover for scale
- **Calibration Weight**: Calibration weight included
- **Battery or AC**: Battery or AC power options

**Legal Issues:**
- **Paraphernalia**: Scales considered drug paraphernalia in some jurisdictions
- **Evidence**: Scales as evidence in drug cases
- **Regulation**: Regulation of scale sales
- **Legitimate Use**: Legitimate uses for precision scales
- **Search Warrants**: Scales included in search warrants
- **Possession**: Possession of scales as evidence of drug dealing

**The Scale Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential tool for drug trade
- **Dual Use**: Legitimate and illicit uses
- **Precision**: Precision essential for safety and business
- **Legal Risk**: Legal risk of possession
- **Quality**: Quality varies significantly
- **Calibration**: Calibration critical for accuracy
