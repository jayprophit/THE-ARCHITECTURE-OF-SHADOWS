# Chapter 118: The Imperial System

## Traditional Drug Measurements

The imperial system persists in some drug markets:

**Imperial Units:**
- **Ounce (oz)**: 28 grams
- **Pound (lb)**: 16 ounces (448 grams)
- **Stone**: 14 pounds (rarely used in drug trade)

**Common Drug Quantities:**
- **Ounce**: Common unit for mid-level distribution
- **Pound**: Common unit for wholesale distribution
- **Key (Kilogram)**: Sometimes called "key" but actually kilogram
- **Half-Pound**: 8 ounces (224 grams)
- **Quarter-Pound**: 4 ounces (112 grams)

**Imperial in Drug Trade:**
- **Historical Continuity**: Historical continuity in some markets
- **Regional Variation**: Regional variation in measurement systems
- **Street Slang**: Street slang using imperial terms
- **Traditional Markets**: Traditional markets using imperial
- **Conversion**: Conversion between metric and imperial
- **Declining Use**: Declining use in favour of metric

**Conversion Challenges:**
- **Precision**: Less precise than metric
- **International**: Not internationally standard
- **Legal**: Legal system uses metric
- **Confusion**: Potential for confusion
- **Error**: Potential for measurement errors
- **Obsolete**: Increasingly obsolete

**The Imperial Reality:**
- **Historical**: Historical measurement system
- **Declining**: Declining use in drug trade
- **Regional**: Regional variation in use
- **Slang**: Street slang using imperial terms
- **Conversion**: Conversion to metric increasingly common
- **Obsolete**: Becoming obsolete in international trade
