# Chapter 175: Asset Forfeiture

## Seizing Drug-Related Assets

Asset forfeiture targets the financial proceeds of the drug trade:

**Types of Forfeiture:**
- **Criminal Forfeiture**: Forfeiture as part of criminal conviction
- **Civil Forfeiture**: Civil forfeiture without criminal conviction
- **Administrative Forfeiture**: Administrative forfeiture of seized assets
- **International Forfeiture**: International asset forfeiture
- **Forfeiture Sharing**: Sharing of forfeited assets between agencies
- **Equitable Sharing**: Equitable sharing with state and local agencies

**Assets Targeted:**
- **Cash**: Cash seizures
- **Real Estate**: Real estate forfeiture
- **Vehicles**: Vehicle forfeiture
- **Bank Accounts**: Bank account forfeiture
- **Businesses**: Business forfeiture
- **Luxury Goods**: Luxury goods forfeiture

**Forfeiture Process:**
- **Seizure**: Initial seizure of assets
- **Notice**: Notice to asset owners
- **Claim Process**: Process for owners to claim assets
- **Forfeiture Proceedings**: Legal proceedings for forfeiture
- **Distribution**: Distribution of forfeited assets
- **Appeals**: Appeals process for forfeiture

**Controversies:**
- **Due Process Concerns**: Due process concerns in civil forfeiture
- **Innocent Owners**: Innocent owners losing assets
- **Profit Motive**: Profit motive for law enforcement
- **Abuse**: Potential for abuse
- **Racial Disparities**: Racial disparities in forfeiture
- **Reform Efforts**: Reform efforts to address abuses

**The Asset Forfeiture Reality:**
- **Profitable**: Profitable for law enforcement agencies
- **Controversial**: Highly controversial practice
- **Due Process Concerns**: Significant due process concerns
- **Effective**: Effective at targeting drug profits
- **Abuse Potential**: High potential for abuse
- **Reform Needed**: Need for reform to protect innocent owners
