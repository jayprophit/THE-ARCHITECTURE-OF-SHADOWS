# Chapter 171: Interdiction

## Intercepting Drug Shipments

Interdiction targets drug shipments in transit:

**Interdiction Methods:**
- **Border Interdiction**: Interdiction at borders
- **Maritime Interdiction**: Maritime interdiction of shipments
- **Air Interdiction**: Air interdiction of shipments
- **Road Interdiction**: Road interdiction of shipments
- **Rail Interdiction**: Rail interdiction of shipments
- **Postal Interdiction**: Interdiction of postal shipments

**Border Interdiction:**
- **Customs**: Customs inspection of shipments
- **X-Ray Scanning**: X-ray scanning of cargo
- **Canine Detection**: Canine detection of drugs
- **Inspection Teams**: Specialised inspection teams
- **Intelligence-Led**: Intelligence-led interdiction
- **Risk-Based**: Risk-based targeting

**Maritime Interdiction:**
- **Naval Patrols**: Naval patrols of trafficking routes
- **Boarding**: Boarding of suspicious vessels
- **Satellite Monitoring**: Satellite monitoring of maritime traffic
- **Cooperation**: International cooperation on maritime interdiction
- **Hot Pursuit**: Hot pursuit across maritime boundaries
- **Asset Seizure**: Seizure of vessels and cargo

**Air Interdiction:**
- **Air Interdiction**: Interdiction of air shipments
- **Cargo Inspection**: Inspection of air cargo
- **Passenger Inspection**: Inspection of passengers
- **Intelligence**: Intelligence on air trafficking
- **Cooperation**: International cooperation on air interdiction
- **Private Aircraft**: Monitoring of private aircraft

**The Interdiction Reality:**
- **Resource Intensive**: Resource intensive operations
- **Adaptive**: Traffickers adapt to interdiction methods
- **International**: Requires international cooperation
- **Limited Impact**: Limited impact on overall drug supply
- **Cat and Mouse**: Constant cat and mouse with traffickers
- **Displacement**: Displacement rather than elimination
