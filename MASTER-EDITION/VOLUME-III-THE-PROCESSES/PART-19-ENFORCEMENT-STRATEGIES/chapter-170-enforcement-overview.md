# Chapter 170: Enforcement Overview

## Drug Enforcement Strategies

Drug enforcement encompasses various strategies and approaches:

**Enforcement Approaches:**
- **Supply Reduction**: Targeting supply side of drug trade
- **Demand Reduction**: Targeting demand for drugs
- **Interdiction**: Interdicting drug shipments
- **Investigation**: Investigating drug organisations
- **Intelligence**: Intelligence gathering on drug trade
- **International Cooperation**: International cooperation on enforcement

**Supply Reduction:**
- **Crop Eradication**: Eradication of drug crops
- **Laboratory Destruction**: Destruction of drug laboratories
- **Trafficking Interdiction**: Interdiction of drug trafficking
- **Arrests**: Arrests of drug traffickers
- **Asset Seizure**: Seizure of drug-related assets
- **Disruption**: Disruption of drug organisations

**Demand Reduction:**
- **Prevention**: Prevention programs
- **Education**: Drug education programs
- **Treatment**: Drug treatment programs
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction programs
- **Prescription Monitoring**: Prescription drug monitoring
- **Public Awareness**: Public awareness campaigns

**Challenges:**
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for enforcement
- **Adaptive Criminals**: Criminals adapt to enforcement
- **International Scope**: International scope complicates enforcement
- **Political Pressure**: Political pressure on enforcement
- **Effectiveness Questions**: Questions about effectiveness
- **Unintended Consequences**: Unintended consequences of enforcement

**The Enforcement Reality:**
- **Resource Intensive**: Resource intensive operations
- **Complex**: Complex set of strategies
- **Controversial**: Controversial effectiveness
- **Adaptive**: Must adapt to criminal adaptation
- **International**: Requires international cooperation
- **Debate**: Ongoing debate about effectiveness
