# Chapter 178: Effectiveness of Enforcement

## Evaluating Drug Enforcement Success

The effectiveness of drug enforcement is widely debated:

**Measures of Effectiveness:**
- **Price**: Drug prices as measure of availability
- **Purity**: Drug purity as measure of availability
- **Seizures**: Seizure statistics
- **Arrests**: Arrest statistics
- **Prevalence**: Drug use prevalence rates
- **Treatment Admissions**: Treatment admission rates

**Evidence on Effectiveness:**
- **Limited Impact**: Limited impact on drug availability
- **Price Stability**: Drug prices relatively stable despite enforcement
- **Purity Stability**: Drug purity relatively stable despite enforcement
- **Displacement**: Displacement rather than elimination
- **Adaptation**: Criminals adapt to enforcement pressure
- **Unintended Consequences**: Significant unintended consequences

**Unintended Consequences:**
- **Violence**: Increased violence from enforcement
- **Health Risks**: Increased health risks from adulterated drugs
- **Prison Overcrowding**: Prison overcrowding from arrests
- **Racial Disparities**: Racial disparities in enforcement
- **Economic Costs**: High economic costs
- **Human Rights**: Human rights violations

**Alternative Approaches:**
- **Legalisation**: Legalisation as alternative to enforcement
- **Decriminalisation**: Decriminalisation as alternative
- **Regulation**: Regulation as alternative
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction approaches
- **Treatment Expansion**: Expansion of treatment
- **Public Health**: Public health approaches

**The Effectiveness Reality:**
- **Limited**: Limited effectiveness in reducing drug use
- **Costly**: Extremely costly approach
- **Unintended Consequences**: Significant unintended consequences
- **Debate**: Ongoing debate about effectiveness
- **Policy Alternatives**: Alternative policy approaches available
- **Need for Evaluation**: Need for evidence-based policy
