# Chapter 186: Prevention Overview

## Drug Prevention Strategies

Drug prevention aims to prevent drug use before it starts:

**Prevention Approaches:**
- **Primary Prevention**: Preventing drug use before it starts
- **Secondary Prevention**: Early intervention for at-risk individuals
- **Tertiary Prevention**: Preventing escalation of drug use
- **Universal Prevention**: Programs targeting entire populations
- **Selective Prevention**: Programs targeting at-risk groups
- **Indicated Prevention**: Programs targeting individuals showing early signs

**Universal Prevention:**
- **School Programs**: School-based prevention programs
- **Community Programs**: Community-wide prevention programs
- **Media Campaigns**: Public awareness campaigns
- **Policy Approaches**: Policy approaches to prevention
- **Environmental Strategies**: Environmental prevention strategies
- **Family Programs**: Family-focused prevention programs

**Selective Prevention:**
- **At-Risk Youth**: Programs for at-risk youth
- **High-Risk Communities**: Programs for high-risk communities
- **Families with History**: Programs for families with addiction history
- **School Dropouts**: Programs for school dropouts
- **Youth in Foster Care**: Programs for youth in foster care
- **Other At-Risk Groups**: Programs for other at-risk populations

**Indicated Prevention:**
- **Early Experimenters**: Programs for early experimenters
- **School Problems**: Programs for youth with school problems
- **Behavioural Issues**: Programs for youth with behavioural issues
- **Mental Health**: Programs for youth with mental health issues
- **Trauma**: Programs for youth with trauma history
- **Early Intervention**: Early intervention for at-risk individuals

**The Prevention Reality:**
- **Varied Approaches**: Multiple prevention approaches
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based programs most effective
- **Early Intervention**: Early intervention most effective
- **Multi-Component**: Multi-component programs most effective
- **Long-Term**: Long-term commitment required
- **Mixed Results**: Mixed results from prevention programs
