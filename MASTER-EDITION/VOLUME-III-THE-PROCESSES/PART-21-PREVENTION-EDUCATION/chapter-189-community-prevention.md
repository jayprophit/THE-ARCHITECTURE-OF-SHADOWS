# Chapter 189: Community-Based Prevention

## Community Approaches to Drug Prevention

Community-based prevention engages entire communities in prevention efforts:

**Program Types:**
- **Community Coalitions**: Community coalitions for prevention
- **Environmental Strategies**: Environmental prevention strategies
- **Media Campaigns**: Community media campaigns
- **Policy Initiatives**: Community policy initiatives
- **Youth Programs**: Community youth programs
- **Community Mobilisation**: Community mobilisation efforts

**Environmental Strategies:**
- **Alcohol Outlet Density**: Reducing alcohol outlet density
- **Minimum Legal Drinking Age**: Enforcing minimum legal drinking age
- **Smoke-Free Laws**: Smoke-free laws for tobacco
- **Advertising Restrictions**: Restrictions on alcohol and tobacco advertising
- **Price Policies**: Tax policies affecting alcohol and tobacco prices
- **Availability Restrictions**: Restrictions on availability

**Community Coalitions:**
- **CADCA**: Community Anti-Drug Coalitions of America
- **Drug-Free Communities**: Drug-Free Communities Support Program
- **Local Coalitions**: Local community coalitions
- **Multi-Sector**: Involvement of multiple community sectors
- **Community Assessment**: Community needs assessment
- **Strategic Planning**: Strategic planning for prevention

**Youth Programs:**
- **After-School Programs**: After-school programs for youth
- **Mentoring Programs**: Mentoring programs for at-risk youth
- **Sports Programs**: Sports and recreation programs
- **Youth Leadership**: Youth leadership programs
- **Summer Programs**: Summer youth programs
- **Community Service**: Community service opportunities

**The Community Prevention Reality:**
- **Comprehensive**: Comprehensive community approaches
- **Multi-Sector**: Involvement of multiple sectors
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based strategies most effective
- **Long-Term**: Long-term community commitment required
- **Community Ownership**: Community ownership of programs
- **Sustainable**: Sustainable community-based approaches
