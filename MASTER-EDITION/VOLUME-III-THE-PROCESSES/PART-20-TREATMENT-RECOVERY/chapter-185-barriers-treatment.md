# Chapter 185: Barriers to Treatment

## Obstacles to Accessing Drug Treatment

Many barriers prevent people from accessing drug treatment:

**Stigma:**
- **Social Stigma**: Social stigma against addiction
- **Self-Stigma**: Internalised stigma preventing treatment seeking
- **Professional Stigma**: Stigma from healthcare providers
- **Family Stigma**: Family stigma about addiction
- **Community Stigma**: Community stigma affecting treatment seeking
- **Workplace Stigma**: Workplace stigma affecting treatment

**Financial Barriers:**
- **Cost of Treatment**: High cost of treatment programs
- **Insurance Coverage**: Limited insurance coverage for addiction treatment
- **Uninsured**: Uninsured individuals unable to afford treatment
- **Underinsured**: Underinsured with insufficient coverage
- **Transportation Costs**: Transportation costs to access treatment
- **Lost Income**: Lost income during treatment

**Systemic Barriers:**
- **Limited Capacity**: Limited treatment capacity
- **Wait Lists**: Long wait lists for treatment
- **Geographic Access**: Limited geographic access to treatment
- **Rural Access**: Limited access in rural areas
- **Fragmented System**: Fragmented treatment system
- **Bureaucracy**: Bureaucratic barriers to accessing treatment

**Personal Barriers:**
- **Denial**: Denial of addiction problem
- **Readiness**: Not ready to stop using
- **Fear**: Fear of withdrawal and treatment
- **Mental Health**: Co-occurring mental health issues
- **Trauma**: Trauma affecting treatment engagement
- **Lack of Support**: Lack of social support for recovery

**The Barriers Reality:**
- **Significant**: Significant barriers to treatment access
- **Multiple**: Multiple overlapping barriers
- **Systemic**: Systemic barriers requiring systemic solutions
- **Individual**: Individual barriers requiring individualised approaches
- **Preventable**: Many barriers are preventable
- **Reform Needed**: Systemic reform needed to improve access
