# Chapter 181: Behavioural Therapy

## Psychological Treatment of Addiction

Behavioural therapy addresses the psychological aspects of addiction:

**Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT):**
- **Thought Patterns**: Identifying and changing unhealthy thought patterns
- **Coping Skills**: Developing coping skills for drug use triggers
- **Relapse Prevention**: Relapse prevention strategies
- **Problem Solving**: Problem-solving skills
- **Skill Building**: Building skills for drug-free life
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base for effectiveness

**Motivational Interviewing:**
- **Ambivalence**: Addressing ambivalence about change
- **Motivation**: Enhancing motivation for change
- **Client-Centered**: Client-centered approach
- **Collaborative**: Collaborative relationship between therapist and client
- **Goal Setting**: Setting and achieving treatment goals
- **Effective**: Effective for enhancing treatment engagement

**Contingency Management:**
- **Incentives**: Providing incentives for drug-free behaviour
- **Voucher Programs**: Voucher-based incentive programs
- **Prize Draws**: Prize draws for drug-free tests
- **Positive Reinforcement**: Positive reinforcement for abstinence
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base for effectiveness
- **Cost-Effective**: Cost-effective approach

**Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT):**
- **Emotional Regulation**: Emotional regulation skills
- **Distress Tolerance**: Distress tolerance skills
- **Interpersonal Effectiveness**: Interpersonal effectiveness skills
- **Mindfulness**: Mindfulness practices
- **Dual Diagnosis**: Effective for dual diagnosis patients
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based approach

**Family Therapy:**
- **Family Dynamics**: Addressing family dynamics
- **Family Involvement**: Involving family in treatment
- **Communication**: Improving family communication
- **Support**: Building family support for recovery
- **Multidimensional Family Therapy**: Multidimensional family therapy approach
- **Effective**: Effective for adolescents and families

**The Behavioural Therapy Reality:**
- **Effective**: Effective treatment approach
- **Varied Approaches**: Multiple effective approaches
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base
- **Individualised**: Individualised to patient needs
- **Combination**: Often combined with other treatments
- **Skilled Providers**: Requires skilled therapists
