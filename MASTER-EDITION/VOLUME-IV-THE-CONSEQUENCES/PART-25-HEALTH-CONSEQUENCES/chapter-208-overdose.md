# Chapter 208: Overdose

## Drug Overdose Crisis

Drug overdose is a leading cause of preventable death:

**Overdose Types:**
- **Opioid Overdose**: Opioid overdose as leading cause
- **Stimulant Overdose**: Cocaine and methamphetamine overdose
- **Depressant Overdose**: Alcohol and benzodiazepine overdose
- **Polydrug Overdose**: Overdose from multiple substances
- **Fentanyl Overdose**: Fentanyl-related overdoses
- **Other Overdoses**: Overdoses from other substances

**Opioid Overdose:**
- **Respiratory Depression**: Respiratory depression as primary mechanism
- **Fentanyl**: Fentanyl's role in overdose crisis
- **Prescription Opioids**: Prescription opioid overdoses
- **Heroin**: Heroin overdoses
- **Naloxone**: Naloxone for overdose reversal
- **Risk Factors**: Risk factors for opioid overdose

**Stimulant Overdose:**
- **Cardiovascular Effects**: Cardiovascular effects of stimulant overdose

- **Hyperthermia**: Hyperthermia from stimulant overdose
- **Seizures**: Seizures from stimulant overdose
- **Stroke**: Stroke risk from stimulant overdose
- **Heart Attack**: Heart attack risk from stimulant overdose

**Overdose Prevention:**
- **Naloxone Distribution**: Naloxone distribution programs
- **Good Samaritan Laws**: Good Samaritan laws protecting overdose responders
- **Supervised Consumption Sites**: Supervised consumption sites
- **Drug Checking**: Drug checking to detect dangerous substances
- **Education**: Overdose prevention education
- **Treatment Access**: Treatment access to prevent overdose

**The Overdose Reality:**
- **Crisis**: Overdose crisis as public health emergency
- **Preventable**: Most overdoses preventable
- **Fentanyl**: Fentanyl driving overdose crisis
- **Policy Response**: Policy response to crisis
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction essential to prevention
