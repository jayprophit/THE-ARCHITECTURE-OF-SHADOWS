# Chapter 216: Nutrition and Malnutrition

## Drug Effects on Nutrition

Drug use significantly impacts nutritional status:

**Malnutrition:**
- **Poor Nutrition**: Poor nutritional status among drug users
- **Weight Loss**: Unintentional weight loss
- **Vitamin Deficiencies**: Vitamin and mineral deficiencies
- **Protein Deficiency**: Protein deficiency
- **Malnutrition Risk**: High risk of malnutrition
- **Health Consequences**: Health consequences of malnutrition

**Appetite Effects:**
- **Stimulants**: Appetite suppression from stimulants
- **Cannabis**: Increased appetite from cannabis
- **Opioids**: Appetite effects from opioids
- **Alcohol**: Poor nutrition from alcohol calories
- **Eating Disorders**: Co-occurring eating disorders
- **Food Insecurity**: Food insecurity among drug users

**Specific Substance Effects:**
- **Alcohol**: Empty calories, malnutrition
- **Stimulants**: Severe appetite suppression
- **Opioids**: Gastrointestinal effects affecting nutrition
- **Cannabis**: Increased appetite, poor food choices
- **Inhalants**: Severe nutritional neglect
- **Multiple Substances**: Combined effects on nutrition

**Gastrointestinal Effects:**
- **Malabsorption**: Malabsorption of nutrients
- **Digestive Problems**: Digestive problems affecting nutrition
- **Nausea and Vomiting**: Nausea and vomiting affecting food intake
- **Diarrhea**: Diarrhea affecting nutrient absorption
- **Gastritis**: Gastritis affecting nutrition
- **Liver Disease**: Liver disease affecting nutrition

**Recovery and Treatment:**
- **Nutritional Assessment**: Nutritional assessment in treatment
- **Nutritional Support**: Nutritional support during recovery
- **Dietary Education**: Education about nutrition
- **Supplementation**: Vitamin and mineral supplementation
- **Recovery of Nutrition**: Nutritional recovery with abstinence
- **Long-Term Health**: Long-term health effects of malnutrition

**The Nutrition Reality:**
- **Significant Impact**: Significant impact on nutritional status
- **Preventable**: Malnutrition preventable with proper care
- **Treatment**: Nutritional support in treatment
- **Recovery**: Nutritional recovery with abstinence
- **Health Consequences**: Long-term health consequences
- **Integrated Care**: Need for nutritional care in treatment
