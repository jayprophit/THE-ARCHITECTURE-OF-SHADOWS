# Chapter 209: Addiction

## The Nature of Addiction

Addiction is a chronic brain disease characterised by compulsive drug seeking and use:

**Definition of Addiction:**
- **Chronic Disease**: Addiction as chronic brain disease
- **Compulsive Use**: Compulsive drug seeking and use
- **Loss of Control**: Loss of control over drug use
- **Negative Consequences**: Continued use despite negative consequences
- **Craving**: Intense craving for drugs
- **Relapse**: High risk of relapse even after abstinence

**Brain Mechanisms:**
- **Reward System**: Dysregulation of brain reward system
- **Dopamine**: Dopamine dysregulation in addiction
- **Prefrontal Cortex**: Impaired decision-making and impulse control
- **Amygdala**: Enhanced stress and emotional reactivity
- **Hippocampus**: Enhanced drug-related memories
- **Neuroadaptation**: Neuroadaptation to chronic drug use

**Risk Factors:**
- **Genetic**: Genetic factors contributing to addiction risk
- **Environmental**: Environmental risk factors
- **Trauma**: Childhood trauma and addiction
- **Mental Health**: Co-occurring mental health disorders
- **Early Use**: Early drug use increases risk
- **Social Factors**: Social factors influencing addiction risk

**Progression of Addiction:**
- **Experimentation**: Initial experimentation with drugs
- **Regular Use**: Regular use pattern develops
- **Problematic Use**: Use becomes problematic
- **Addiction**: Full addiction develops
- **Treatment**: Treatment can interrupt progression
- **Recovery**: Recovery is possible with treatment

**The Addiction Reality:**
- **Disease**: Addiction is a brain disease
- **Chronic**: Chronic condition requiring ongoing management
- **Treatable**: Addiction is treatable
- **Stigma**: Stigma affects treatment seeking
- **Recovery**: Recovery is possible
- **Public Health**: Major public health issue
