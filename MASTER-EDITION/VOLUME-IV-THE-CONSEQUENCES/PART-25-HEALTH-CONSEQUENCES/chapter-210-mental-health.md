# Chapter 210: Mental Health Consequences

## Drug Use and Mental Health

Drug use has significant impacts on mental health:

**Drug-Induced Mental Health Issues:**
- **Depression**: Drug-induced depression
- **Anxiety**: Drug-induced anxiety disorders
- **Psychosis**: Drug-induced psychosis
- **Mania**: Drug-induced mania
- **Panic Attacks**: Drug-induced panic attacks
- **Paranoia**: Drug-induced paranoia

**Co-Occurring Disorders:**
- **Dual Diagnosis**: Co-occurring substance use and mental health disorders
- **Self-Medication**: Self-medication hypothesis
- **Shared Risk Factors**: Shared risk factors for addiction and mental illness
- **Complex Interaction**: Complex interaction between substance use and mental health
- **Treatment Challenges**: Treatment challenges for dual diagnosis
- **Integrated Treatment**: Need for integrated treatment

**Specific Substances and Mental Health:**
- **Stimulants**: Stimulants and psychosis, anxiety
- **Depressants**: Depressants and depression
- **Hallucinogens**: Hallucinogens and persistent psychosis
- **Cannabis**: Cannabis and psychosis risk
- **Alcohol**: Alcohol and depression, anxiety
- **Opioids**: Opioids and depression

**Suicide Risk:**
- **Increased Risk**: Increased suicide risk among drug users
- **Acute Intoxication**: Suicide risk during acute intoxication
- **Withdrawal**: Suicide risk during withdrawal
- **Depression**: Depression contributing to suicide risk
- **Impulsivity**: Drug-related impulsivity
- **Treatment**: Treatment reduces suicide risk

**The Mental Health Reality:**
- **Complex**: Complex relationship between drugs and mental health
- **Bidirectional**: Bidirectional relationship
- **Co-Occurring**: High rates of co-occurring disorders
- **Treatable**: Both conditions treatable
- **Integrated Care**: Need for integrated care
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting both conditions
