# Chapter 212: Infectious Disease

## Drug Use and Infectious Diseases

Drug use, particularly injection drug use, drives infectious disease transmission:

**HIV/AIDS:**
- **Transmission**: HIV transmission through injection drug use
- **Needle Sharing**: Needle sharing as primary transmission route
- **Global Impact**: Global impact of HIV among drug users
- **Prevention**: Prevention strategies for HIV
- **Treatment**: HIV treatment among drug users
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting HIV-positive drug users

**Hepatitis C:**
- **Transmission**: Hepatitis C transmission through injection drug use
- **Prevalence**: High prevalence among injection drug users
- **Chronic Infection**: Chronic hepatitis C infection
- **Liver Disease**: Progression to liver disease
- **Treatment**: New hepatitis C treatments
- **Elimination**: Hepatitis C elimination efforts

**Hepatitis B:**
- **Transmission**: Hepatitis B transmission through drug use
- **Vaccination**: Vaccination for hepatitis B
- **Chronic Infection**: Chronic hepatitis B infection
- **Liver Disease**: Progression to liver disease
- **Treatment**: Hepatitis B treatment
- **Prevention**: Prevention strategies for hepatitis B

**Other Infections:**
- **Bacterial Infections**: Bacterial infections from injection
- **Endocarditis**: Endocarditis among injection drug users
- **Skin Infections**: Skin and soft tissue infections
- **Sepsis**: Sepsis from injection drug use
- **Tuberculosis**: Tuberculosis among drug users
- **Sexually Transmitted Infections**: STIs among drug users

**Prevention Strategies:**
- **Syringe Exchange**: Syringe exchange programs
- **Needle Exchange**: Needle exchange programs
- **Vaccination**: Vaccination for hepatitis B
- **Education**: Education about safe injection
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction approaches
- **Treatment Access**: Treatment access for infected individuals

**The Infectious Disease Reality:**
- **Significant**: Significant burden of infectious disease
- **Preventable**: Most infections preventable
- **Harm Reduction**: Harm reduction essential to prevention
- **Treatment**: Treatment available for many infections
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting prevention and treatment
- **Public Health**: Major public health concern
