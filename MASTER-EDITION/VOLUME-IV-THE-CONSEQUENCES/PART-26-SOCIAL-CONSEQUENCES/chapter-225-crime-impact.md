# Chapter 225: Crime Impact

## The Relationship Between Drugs and Crime

The relationship between drugs and crime is complex:

**Types of Drug-Related Crime:**
- **Drug Possession**: Possession of illegal drugs
- **Drug Trafficking**: Drug trafficking offenses
- **Drug-Driven Crime**: Crime committed to obtain drugs
- **System-Related Crime**: Crime resulting from drug market dynamics
- **Violent Crime**: Drug-related violent crime
- **Property Crime**: Drug-related property crime

**Drug-Driven Crime:**
- **Acquisitive Crime**: Crime to fund drug purchases
- **Theft**: Theft to obtain money for drugs
- **Robbery**: Robbery to obtain money for drugs
- **Burglary**: Burglary to obtain money for drugs
- **Prostitution**: Prostitution to fund drug use
- **Fraud**: Fraud to obtain money for drugs

**System-Related Crime:**
- **Market Violence**: Violence from drug market competition
- **Territorial Violence**: Violence over drug territories
- **Enforcement Violence**: Violence from enforcement actions
- **Corruption**: Corruption related to drug trade
- **Money Laundering**: Money laundering crimes
- **Organised Crime**: Organised crime involvement

**Drug Trafficking:**
- **Distribution**: Drug distribution offenses
- **Importation**: Drug importation offenses
- **Manufacturing**: Drug manufacturing offenses
- **International Trafficking**: International drug trafficking
- **Large-Scale Operations**: Large-scale trafficking operations
- **Organised Networks**: Organised criminal networks

**The Crime Impact Reality:**
- **Complex**: Complex relationship between drugs and crime
- **Significant**: Significant impact on crime rates
- **Systemic**: Systemic issues driving drug-related crime
- **Policy-Dependent**: Crime rates affected by drug policy
- **Preventable**: Some drug-related crime preventable
- **Reform Potential**: Policy reform can reduce drug-related crime
