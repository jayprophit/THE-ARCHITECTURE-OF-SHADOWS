# Chapter 230: Social Cohesion

## The Effect of Drug Issues on Social Cohesion

Drug-related issues can erode social cohesion in communities:

**Trust and Social Capital:**
- **Trust Decline**: Decline in interpersonal trust
- **Social Capital**: Loss of social capital
- **Community Bonds**: Weakening of community bonds
- **Neighborhood Relations**: Deterioration of neighborhood relations
- **Institutional Trust**: Decline in trust in institutions
- **Social Networks**: Disruption of social networks

**Community Fragmentation:**
- **Divisions**: Divisions within communities
- **Fear**: Fear among community members
- **Isolation**: Social isolation of affected individuals
- **Segregation**: Segregation of drug-affected areas
- **Gentrification**: Gentrification displacing affected communities
- **Community Displacement**: Displacement of community members

**Collective Efficacy:**
- **Reduced Collective Efficacy**: Reduced ability of communities to address problems
- **Disempowerment**: Disempowerment of communities
- **Lack of Cooperation**: Lack of community cooperation
- **Individualism**: Increased individualism over collective action
- **Apathy**: Community apathy
- **Hopelessness**: Hopelessness about community improvement

**Community Response:**
- **Community Organising**: Community organising to address drug issues
- **Neighborhood Watch**: Neighborhood watch programs
- **Community Activism**: Activism around drug policy
- **Mutual Aid**: Mutual aid among affected community members
- **Community-Based Solutions**: Community-based solutions
- **Resilience**: Community resilience in face of challenges

**Rebuilding Social Cohesion:**
- **Community Engagement**: Engaging communities in solutions
- **Participatory Approaches**: Participatory approaches to drug policy
- **Community Investment**: Investment in affected communities
- **Social Programs**: Social programs to rebuild cohesion
- **Community Events**: Community events to rebuild connections
- **Leadership Development**: Development of community leadership

**The Social Cohesion Reality:**
- **Erosion**: Erosion of social cohesion in affected communities
- **Recoverable**: Social cohesion can be rebuilt
- **Community-Led**: Community-led solutions most effective
- **Investment Needed**: Investment in community development
- **Resilience**: Community resilience exists
- **Essential**: Social cohesion essential for healthy communities
