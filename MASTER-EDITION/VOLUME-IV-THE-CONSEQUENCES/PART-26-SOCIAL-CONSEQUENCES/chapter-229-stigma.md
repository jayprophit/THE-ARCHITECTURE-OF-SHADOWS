# Chapter 229: Stigma

## The Stigma of Drug Use and Addiction

Stigma is a major barrier to addressing drug-related issues:

**Types of Stigma:**
- **Public Stigma**: Public attitudes toward drug users
- **Self-Stigma**: Internalised stigma among drug users
- **Structural Stigma**: Institutional discrimination against drug users
- **Healthcare Stigma**: Stigma in healthcare settings
- **Family Stigma**: Stigma within families
- **Workplace Stigma**: Stigma in employment

**Consequences of Stigma:**
- **Treatment Avoidance**: Avoiding treatment due to stigma
- **Social Isolation**: Social isolation of drug users
- **Employment Discrimination**: Employment discrimination
- **Housing Discrimination**: Housing discrimination
- **Healthcare Barriers**: Barriers to healthcare access
- **Family Rejection**: Family rejection of drug users

**Stigma in Healthcare:**
- **Provider Attitudes**: Negative attitudes of healthcare providers
- **Poor Treatment**: Poor treatment of drug users in healthcare
- **Pain Management**: Inadequate pain management for drug users
- **Mental Health**: Stigma affecting mental health treatment
- **Emergency Care**: Stigma in emergency care
- **Treatment Access**: Barriers to addiction treatment

**Stigma in Criminal Justice:**
- **Criminalisation**: Criminalisation contributing to stigma
- **Media Portrayal**: Media portrayal of drug users
- **Public Perception**: Public perception of drug users as criminals
- **Legal Consequences**: Legal consequences contributing to stigma
- **Employment Impact**: Criminal record contributing to stigma
- **Social Exclusion**: Social exclusion from criminal justice involvement

**Stigma Reduction:**
- **Education**: Education to reduce stigma
- **Contact**: Contact with people in recovery
- **Language**: Language changes to reduce stigma
- **Media Campaigns**: Media campaigns to reduce stigma
- **Policy Reform**: Policy reform to reduce structural stigma
- **Advocacy**: Advocacy by people in recovery

**The Stigma Reality:**
- **Significant Barrier**: Significant barrier to treatment and recovery
- **Widespread**: Widespread stigma against drug users
- **Harmful**: Harmful consequences of stigma
- **Preventable**: Stigma is preventable through education and policy
- **Reform Needed**: Need for stigma reduction efforts
- **Recovery**: Recovery possible despite stigma
