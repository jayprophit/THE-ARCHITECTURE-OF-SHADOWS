# Chapter 277: Global Trade Overview

## The International Drug Trade

The international drug trade is a global enterprise with complex networks:

**Global Scale:**
- **Value**: Hundreds of billions in annual revenue
- **Geographic Reach**: Global reach across all continents
- **Commodities**: Multiple drug commodities traded globally
- **Networks**: Complex global networks
- **Routes**: Global trafficking routes
- **Markets**: Global consumer markets

**Producer Regions:**
- **Andean Region**: Coca production in Andean countries
- **Golden Triangle**: Heroin production in Southeast Asia
- **Golden Crescent**: Heroin production in Central Asia
- **Africa**: Emerging production regions
- **Middle East**: Cannabis and synthetic production
- **Other Regions**: Various production regions

**Transit Regions:**
- **Central America**: Transit routes through Central America
- **Caribbean**: Caribbean transit routes
- **West Africa**: West African transit hub
- **Balkans**: Balkan transit routes
- **Southeast Asia**: Southeast Asian transit
- **Other Transit Regions**: Various transit regions

**Consumer Markets:**
- **North America**: Major consumer market
- **Europe**: Major consumer market
- **Asia**: Growing consumer markets
- **Oceania**: Consumer markets in Australia/New Zealand
- **Africa**: Emerging consumer markets
- **Global Distribution**: Global distribution of consumption

**Trade Dynamics:**
- **Supply Chain**: Global supply chain
- **Price Structure**: Price structure along supply chain
- **Market Forces**: Market forces affecting trade
- **Adaptation**: Adaptation to enforcement pressure
- **Innovation**: Innovation in trade methods
- **Resilience**: Resilience of global trade

**The Global Trade Reality:**
- **Massive**: Massive global enterprise
- **Complex**: Complex global networks
- **Resilient**: Resilient to enforcement
- **Adaptive**: Constantly adapting
- **Profitable**: Extremely profitable
- **Global**: Truly global in scope
