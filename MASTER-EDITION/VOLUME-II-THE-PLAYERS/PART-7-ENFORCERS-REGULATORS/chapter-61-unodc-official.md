# Chapter 61: The UNODC Official

## United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime

The UNODC is the UN agency for drug control:

**Roles and Responsibilities:**
- **Research**: Conducting research on global drug trends
- **Policy Advice**: Providing policy advice to member states
- **Technical Assistance**: Providing technical assistance to countries
- **Treaty Implementation**: Implementing UN drug conventions
- **Data Collection**: Collecting and analysing global drug data
- **International Cooperation**: Facilitating international cooperation

**UNODC Publications:**
- **World Drug Report**: Annual comprehensive report on global drug situation
- **Regional Reports**: Regional drug situation reports
- **Research Publications**: Research on specific drug issues
- **Guidelines**: Guidelines for drug policy and treatment
- **Training Materials**: Training materials for law enforcement

**Challenges:**
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for global coverage
- **Political Constraints**: Political constraints on recommendations
- **Data Quality**: Variable quality of data from member countries
- **Implementation**: Limited ability to enforce recommendations
- **Balancing Act**: Balancing different national interests
- **Evolving Threats**: Rapidly evolving drug threats

**The UNODC Official's Perspective:**
- **Global Perspective**: Global perspective on drug issues
- **Evidence-Based**: Commitment to evidence-based policy
- **Diplomacy**: Balancing science with diplomacy
- **Professional Challenge**: Professional challenge of international coordination
- **Impact**: Desire to have positive global impact
