# Chapter 59: The DEA Agent (US)

## US Drug Enforcement Administration

The DEA is the US federal agency for drug enforcement:

**Roles and Responsibilities:**
- **Investigation**: Investigating drug trafficking organizations
- **Intelligence**: Gathering intelligence on drug markets
- **Enforcement**: Enforcing federal drug laws
- **International Operations**: International drug enforcement operations
- **Diversion Control**: Regulating legal drug distribution
- **Training**: Training law enforcement partners

**DEA Operations:**
- **One Pill Can Kill**: Campaign against counterfeit pills containing fentanyl
- **International Cooperation**: Working with international agencies
- **Major Investigations**: Major investigations into cartels and trafficking networks
- **Asset Forfeiture**: Seizing assets from drug traffickers
- **Special Operations**: Special operations against major targets

**Challenges:**
- **Scope**: Enormous scope of US drug market
- **Violence**: Risk of violence from armed traffickers
- **International Complexity**: Complexity of international drug trafficking
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for comprehensive coverage
- **Political Pressure**: Political pressure for results
- **Public Perception**: Mixed public perception of drug enforcement

**The DEA Agent's Perspective:**
- **Public Service**: Motivated by public service
- **Professional Challenge**: Professional challenge of complex investigations
- **Danger**: Constant danger from armed traffickers
- **International Context**: Working in international context
- **Moral Dilemmas**: Moral dilemmas about drug policy effectiveness
