# Chapter 74: The "Front Business" Owner

## Legitimate Businesses Covering Illicit Activity

Front businesses provide cover for drug money:

**Types of Front Businesses:**
- **Cash Businesses**: Restaurants, bars, retail stores
- **Service Businesses**: Car washes, laundromats, nail salons
- **Import/Export**: Import/export companies
- **Construction**: Construction companies
- **Entertainment**: Nightclubs, casinos
- **Professional Services**: Legal, accounting, consulting firms

**Money Laundering Methods:**
- **Cash Injection**: Injecting drug cash into legitimate business revenue
- **Overstating Revenue**: Overstating legitimate business revenue
- **Fake Invoices**: Creating fake invoices to move money
- **Shell Companies**: Using shell companies to move money
- **Commingling**: Commingling legitimate and illicit funds
- **Tax Evasion**: Evading taxes on both legitimate and illicit income

**Detection Challenges:**
- **Legitimate Business**: Legitimate business makes detection difficult
- **Complex Structures**: Complex corporate structures obscure ownership
- **Professional Services**: Professional services create appearance of legitimacy
- **Geographic Spread**: Businesses spread across multiple jurisdictions
- **Industry Knowledge**: Requires industry knowledge to detect fraud
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for comprehensive investigation

**The Front Business Owner's Perspective:**
- **Legitimate Business**: Often has legitimate business operations
- **Additional Income**: Drug money as additional income
- **Risk Management**: Managing risk of detection
- **Business Skills**: Utilising business skills for money laundering
- **Legal Cover**: Legitimate business provides legal cover
