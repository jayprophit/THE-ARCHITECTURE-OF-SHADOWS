# Chapter 77: The Addict

## The Primary Victim

Addicts are the primary victims of the drug trade:

**Physical Health Consequences:**
- **Overdose Risk**: Constant risk of fatal overdose
- **Organ Damage**: Liver, kidney, heart, brain damage
- **Infectious Disease**: HIV, hepatitis from injection drug use
- **Malnutrition**: Poor nutrition from drug use
- **Chronic Health Problems**: Chronic pain, respiratory problems
- **Premature Death**: Significantly reduced life expectancy

**Mental Health Consequences:**
- **Depression**: High rates of depression
- **Anxiety**: Chronic anxiety and paranoia
- **Psychosis**: Drug-induced psychosis
- **Cognitive Impairment**: Memory and cognitive problems
- **Suicide**: High suicide rates
- **Trauma**: Cumulative trauma from drug use

**Social Consequences:**
- **Relationship Loss**: Loss of family and friends
- **Social Isolation**: Social isolation and loneliness
- **Stigma**: Severe social stigma
- **Employment Loss**: Loss of employment
- **Homelessness**: High risk of homelessness
- **Criminalisation**: Criminalisation of addiction

**Economic Consequences:**
- **Financial Ruin**: Depletion of financial resources
- **Debt**: Accumulation of debt
- **Poverty**: Slide into poverty
- **Employment Barriers**: Barriers to employment
- **Healthcare Costs**: High healthcare costs
- **Legal Costs**: Legal fees and fines

**The Addict's Reality:**
- **Trapped**: Feeling trapped by addiction
- **Stigma**: Constant social stigma
- **Isolation**: Social isolation
- **Despair**: Feelings of hopelessness
- **Cycle**: Caught in cycle of addiction
- **Recovery Hope**: Hope for recovery
