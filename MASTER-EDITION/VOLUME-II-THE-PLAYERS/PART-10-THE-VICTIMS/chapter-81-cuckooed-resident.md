# Chapter 81: The Cuckooed Resident

## Victims of County Lines Exploitation

Cuckooed residents are vulnerable people whose homes are taken over by drug dealers:

**Who Is Targeted:**
- **Vulnerable Adults**: People with vulnerabilities
- **Addicts**: People with drug addiction
- **Mental Health Issues**: People with mental health problems
- **Socially Isolated**: People without strong social networks
- **Elderly**: Elderly people living alone
- **Disabled**: People with disabilities

**How Cuckooing Works:**
- **Initial Approach**: Dealers approach with offers or threats
- **Gradual Takeover**: Gradual takeover of the home
- **Drug Dealing**: Home used for drug dealing
- **Storage**: Home used for drug storage
- **Accommodation**: Dealers stay in the home
- **Control**: Dealers control the resident

**Impact on Victims:**
- **Loss of Control**: Loss of control over their home
- **Fear**: Fear of dealers and violence
- **Intimidation**: Intimidation and threats
- **Violence**: Physical violence and abuse
- **Exploitation**: Exploitation of vulnerabilities
- **Isolation**: Increased social isolation

**Living Conditions:**
- **Overcrowding**: Overcrowded living conditions
- **Drug Activity**: Constant drug activity in the home
- **Violence**: Violence in the home
- **Fear**: Constant fear
- **Degradation**: Degradation of living conditions
- **Health Impact**: Impact on physical and mental health

**The Cuckooed Resident's Reality:**
- **Trapped**: Feeling trapped in their own home
- **Fear**: Constant fear of dealers
- **Helplessness**: Feeling helpless to stop takeover
- **Shame**: Shame about situation
- **Isolation**: Social isolation
- **Hope**: Hope for intervention and rescue
