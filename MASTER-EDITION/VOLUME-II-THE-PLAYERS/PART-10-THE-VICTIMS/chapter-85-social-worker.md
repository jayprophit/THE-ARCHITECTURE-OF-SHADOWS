# Chapter 85: The Social Worker

## Supporting Drug-Affected Families

Social workers support families affected by drug issues:

**Roles and Responsibilities:**
- **Case Management**: Managing cases of drug-affected families
- **Child Protection**: Protecting children in drug-affected families
- **Support Services**: Providing support services
- **Resource Connection**: Connecting families to resources
- **Crisis Intervention**: Responding to crises
- **Advocacy**: Advocating for clients

**Challenges:**
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for support services
- **Burnout**: High rates of burnout
- **Vicarious Trauma**: Vicarious trauma from clients' experiences
- **Systemic Issues**: Frustration with systemic issues
- **Moral Dilemmas**: Moral dilemmas about child protection
- **Emotional Toll**: Emotional toll of working with trauma

**Child Protection:**
- **Assessment**: Assessing risk to children
- **Intervention**: Intervening in at-risk situations
- **Foster Care**: Arranging foster care when necessary
- **Family Preservation**: Supporting family preservation when possible
- **Court Proceedings**: Involvement in court proceedings
- **Long-Term Support**: Long-term support for children

**The Social Worker's Perspective:**
- **Compassion**: Compassion for clients
- **Frustration**: Frustration with limited resources
- **Burnout**: High rates of burnout
- **Systemic Issues**: Awareness of systemic issues
- **Advocacy**: Advocacy for better policies
- **Hope**: Hope for better outcomes
