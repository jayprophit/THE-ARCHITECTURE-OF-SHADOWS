# Chapter 89: The Corrupt Politician

## Political Corruption in Drug Policy

Corrupt politicians enable the drug trade through political corruption:

**Forms of Political Corruption:**
- **Campaign Contributions**: Drug money funding political campaigns
- **Bribery**: Direct bribery for political favors
- **Policy Influence**: Influencing drug policy for corrupt purposes
- **Protection**: Providing political protection to drug traffickers
- **Regulatory Capture**: Regulatory agencies captured by drug interests
- **International Corruption**: Corruption across international borders

**Mechanisms of Influence:**
- **Lobbying**: Corrupt lobbying practices
- **Campaign Finance**: Drug money in campaign finance
- **Political Donations**: Political donations from drug interests
- **Shell Companies**: Shell companies hiding political donations
- **Family Connections**: Family connections to drug trade
- **Business Interests**: Business interests in drug trade

**Impact on Policy:**
- **Prohibition Maintenance**: Maintaining prohibition to protect profits
- **Selective Enforcement**: Selective enforcement to protect interests
- **Regulatory Capture**: Regulatory agencies protecting drug interests
- **Policy Blocking**: Blocking reform efforts
- **International Pressure**: International pressure to maintain prohibition
- **Legal Frameworks**: Legal frameworks that enable corruption

**Detection Challenges:**
- **Legal Campaign Finance**: Legal campaign finance hides corruption
- **Complex Networks**: Complex networks hide connections
- **International Scope**: International scope complicates investigation
- **Political Protection**: Political protection from investigation
- **Legal Immunity**: Legal immunity for certain political actions
- **Resource Constraints**: Limited resources for investigation

**The Corrupt Politician's Reality:**
- **Power**: Political power enables corruption
- **Rationalisation**: Rationalisation of corrupt behaviour
- **Risk**: Risk of exposure and prosecution
- **Public Trust**: Betrayal of public trust
- **Systemic Impact**: Systemic impact on governance
- **Accountability**: Need for accountability
