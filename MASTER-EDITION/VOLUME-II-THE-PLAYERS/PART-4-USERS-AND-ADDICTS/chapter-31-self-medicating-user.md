# Chapter 31: The "Self-Medicating" User

## Trauma, Mental Health, and Addiction

Many drug users are self-medicating underlying mental health issues:

**Common Co-occurring Disorders:**
- **Depression**: Self-medication with stimulants or depressants
- **Anxiety**: Use of alcohol, benzodiazepines, or cannabis
- **PTSD**: Self-medication to manage trauma symptoms
- **Bipolar Disorder**: Attempts to manage mood swings
- **ADHD**: Self-medication with stimulants
- **Personality Disorders**: Difficulty regulating emotions

**Trauma and Drug Use:**
- **Childhood Trauma**: Strong predictor of later substance use
- **Physical Abuse**: Higher rates of drug use
- **Sexual Abuse**: Particularly strong association with substance use
- **Emotional Neglect**: Difficulty regulating emotions
- **Complex PTSD**: Multiple traumatic experiences

## Depression, Anxiety, PTSD

**Depression:**
- **Self-Medication Patterns**: Alcohol for sedation, stimulants for energy
- **Temporary Relief**: Drugs provide brief respite from symptoms
- **Worsening**: Long-term drug use often worsens depression
- **Treatment Barrier**: Drug use complicates depression treatment

**Anxiety:**
- **Self-Medication**: Alcohol, benzodiazepines, cannabis for anxiety relief
- **Paradoxical Effect**: Some drugs increase anxiety over time
- **Withdrawal Anxiety**: Drug withdrawal mimics anxiety disorders
- **Vicious Cycle**: Anxiety leads to use, use leads to more anxiety

**PTSD:**
- **Symptom Management**: Drugs used to manage flashbacks, nightmares, hypervigilance
- **Numbing**: Opioids and depressants used to numb emotional pain
- **Avoidance**: Drugs used to avoid trauma memories
- **Complex Treatment**: Requires integrated trauma and addiction treatment

**The Self-Medication Cycle:**
1. Underlying mental health issue causes distress
2. Drugs provide temporary relief
3. Tolerance develops, requiring more drugs
4. Drug use creates additional problems
5. Mental health worsens
6. Increased drug use to cope
7. Cycle continues and intensifies

**Treatment Implications:**
- **Integrated Treatment**: Must address both addiction and mental health
- **Trauma-Informed Care**: Understanding trauma's role in addiction
- **Dual Diagnosis**: Specialised treatment for co-occurring disorders
- **Medication-Assisted Treatment**: Sometimes appropriate for both conditions
- **Therapy**: Cognitive-behavioural, EMDR, trauma-focused therapies
