# Chapter 29: The Recreational User

## Social Use and Experimentation

Recreational drug use is characterised by controlled, social use:

**Patterns of Use:**
- **Weekend Use**: Limited to weekends or social occasions
- **Social Context**: Use primarily in social settings
- **Controlled Consumption**: Moderate amounts, not daily use
- **Functional**: Maintains work, family, and social responsibilities

**Motivations:**
- **Social Bonding**: Facilitates social interaction
- **Celebration**: Marks special occasions
- **Curiosity**: Experimentation and novelty-seeking
- **Relaxation**: Stress relief and recreation
- **Enhancement**: Enhancing social experiences

**Risk Factors:**
- **Progression**: Can transition to problematic use
- **Legal Risks**: Criminalisation of possession
- **Health Risks**: Even occasional use carries risks
- **Quality Control**: Variable drug quality increases risks
- **Context**: Different contexts produce different risks

## Trends and Patterns

Recreational use patterns vary by substance and demographic:

**Cannabis:**
- Most commonly used illicit drug globally
- Often considered "soft" drug with lower perceived risk
- Socially acceptable in many circles
- Increasing legalisation and decriminalisation

**MDMA/Party Drugs:**
- Associated with nightlife, festivals, raves
- Periodic rather than daily use
- Strong social context
- Risks include dehydration, adulteration

**Cocaine:**
- Associated with nightlife and professional circles
- Perceived as status drug in some contexts
- Higher addiction potential than cannabis
- Significant health risks even with occasional use

**Psychedelics:**
- Often used for exploration rather than recreation
- Less frequent use due to intensity
- Growing interest in therapeutic potential
- Generally lower addiction potential
