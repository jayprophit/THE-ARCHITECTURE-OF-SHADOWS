# Chapter 32: The Rich User

## The Ultra-Wealthy and Their Consumption Habits

Wealthy drug users have different patterns and access:

**Access to High-Quality Substances:**
- **Purity**: Access to higher-purity drugs
- **Variety**: Access to rare and novel substances
- **Safety**: Less exposure to adulterated street drugs
- **Prescription Access**: Easier access to prescription medications

**Consumption Patterns:**
- **Discretion**: Use often private and hidden
- **Professional Settings**: Use in business and social contexts
- **High-End Venues**: Exclusive clubs, private parties
- **Travel**: Drug use during international travel
- **Prescription Masking**: Use of legitimate prescriptions to obtain drugs

**Common Substances:**
- **Cocaine**: Status drug in wealthy circles
- **Prescription Drugs**: Opioids, benzodiazepines, stimulants
- **Novel Substances**: Access to new designer drugs
- **Alcohol**: Expensive wines, spirits
- **Cannabis**: High-quality strains, concentrates

## The "VIP" Treatment

Wealthy users receive different treatment:

**Legal System:**
- **Better Legal Representation**: High-quality lawyers
- **Diversion Programs**: More likely to receive treatment instead of jail
- **Privacy**: Court records often sealed or expunged
- **Bail**: Easily able to post bail
- **Sentencing**: Often receive lighter sentences

**Healthcare:**
- **Private Treatment**: Exclusive rehabilitation centres
- **Privacy**: Confidential treatment
- **Quality**: Higher-quality treatment programs
- **Aftercare**: Extensive follow-up and support
- **Cost**: Treatment costs are not a barrier

**Social Consequences:**
- **Less Stigma**: Drug use often overlooked or accepted
- **Enabling**: Friends and family may enable use
- **Isolation**: Secret use prevents honest relationships
- **Financial Protection**: Wealth buffers financial consequences
- **Career Impact**: May have more career protection

**The Wealth Paradox:**
- **Resources for Recovery**: Have resources for treatment but may not use them
- **Enabling Environment**: Wealth can enable continued use
- **Less External Pressure**: Fewer consequences forcing change
- **Denial**: Success can mask addiction severity
- **Isolation**: Wealth can create social isolation
