# Chapter 49: The Edible Producer

## The Kitchen Industry

Edible producers create food and drink products containing drugs:

**Products:**
- **Cannabis Edibles**: Brownies, cookies, gummies, chocolates
- **Cannabis Beverages**: Sodas, teas, infused drinks
- **THC/CBD Products**: Various THC and CBD-infused products
- **Dosed Products**: Precisely dosed products
- **Variety**: Wide variety of edible products

**Production:**
- **Kitchen-Based**: Often produced in home kitchens
- **Commercial Production**: Increasing commercial production in legal markets
- **Dosing**: Precise dosing is critical for safety
- **Quality Control**: Variable quality control
- **Packaging**: Professional packaging in legal markets

## Legal and Illegal Markets

**Legal Markets:**
- **Licensed Production**: Licensed facilities in legal jurisdictions
- **Testing**: Required testing for potency and contaminants
- **Regulation**: Strict regulations on production and labelling
- **Taxation**: Subject to taxation
- **Quality Standards**: Must meet quality standards

**Illegal Markets:**
- **Unregulated**: No regulation or oversight
- **Variable Quality**: Quality varies significantly
- **Dosing Issues**: Inconsistent dosing can be dangerous
- **Packaging**: Often unprofessional packaging
- **Risk**: Higher risk of contamination and inconsistent dosing

**Safety Concerns:**
- **Overconsumption**: Edibles can cause overconsumption due to delayed onset
- **Dosing**: Accurate dosing is critical
- **Labelling**: Clear labelling is essential
- **Child Safety**: Child-resistant packaging required in legal markets
- **Contamination**: Risk of contamination in unregulated production

**The Edible Producer's Role:**
- **Growing Market**: Increasing demand for edible products
- **Skill Required**: Requires culinary and technical skills
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Legal markets require regulatory compliance
- **Innovation**: Constant innovation in product development
- **Public Health Impact**: Quality edibles are safer than unregulated products
