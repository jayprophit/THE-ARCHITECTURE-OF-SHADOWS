# Chapter 53: Coca Cultivation Methods

**Traditional Cultivation:**
- **Small-Scale**: Traditional small-scale cultivation
- **Mountainous Terrain**: Cultivated on steep mountain slopes
- **Manual Labour**: Intensive manual labour
- **Traditional Methods**: Methods passed down through generations
- **Sustainable**: Traditional methods often more sustainable
- **Cultural Significance**: Deep cultural significance in Andean cultures

**Cultivation Process:**
- **Seedling Stage**: Nurseries for seedlings
- **Transplanting**: Transplanting to fields
- **Maintenance**: Weeding, pruning, pest control
- **Harvesting**: Manual harvesting of leaves
- **Drying**: Sun-drying harvested leaves
- **Processing**: Initial processing for market

**Environmental Factors:**
- **Altitude**: Optimal altitude 500-2,000 metres
- **Climate**: Tropical, consistent rainfall, moderate temperatures
- **Soil**: Well-drained soil
- **Water**: Adequate water supply
- **Sunlight**: Adequate sunlight
- **Pests and Diseases**: Management of pests and diseases

**Traditional vs. Industrial:**
- **Traditional**: Small-scale, sustainable, cultural
- **Industrial**: Large-scale, intensive, commercial
- **Environmental Impact**: Industrial cultivation has greater environmental impact
- **Quality**: Traditional methods often produce higher quality
- **Sustainability**: Traditional methods more sustainable
- **Cultural Preservation**: Traditional methods preserve cultural practices

**Coca Leaf Processing:**
- **Fresh Leaves**: Used fresh for traditional consumption
- **Dried Leaves**: Sun-dried for storage and transport
- **Cocaine Extraction**: Chemical extraction of cocaine
- **Traditional Products**: Traditional coca products (tea, flour)
- **Byproducts**: Legal byproducts from coca leaves
