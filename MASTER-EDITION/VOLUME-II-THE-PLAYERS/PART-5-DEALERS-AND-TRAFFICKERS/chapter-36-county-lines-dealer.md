# Chapter 36: The County Lines Dealer

## UK-Specific Model

County lines is a UK-specific drug trafficking model:

**Definition:**
- **County Lines**: Drug supply networks from urban areas to smaller towns and rural areas
- **Phone Lines**: Dedicated mobile phone lines for taking orders
- **Expansion**: Urban gangs expanding into new territories
- **Exploitation**: Often exploits vulnerable people as runners

**How It Works:**
1. Urban gang establishes phone line for a specific county
2. Customers call dedicated number to place orders
3. Gang dispatches runners to deliver drugs
4. Runers often stay in cuckooed accommodation
5. Profits flow back to urban gang leaders

**Geographic Spread:**
- **Origin**: Major cities (London, Birmingham, Manchester, Liverpool)
- **Destination**: Smaller towns and rural areas across UK
- **Expansion**: Rapid expansion since 2010s
- **Scope**: Affects most of England and Wales

## Cuckooing and Territorial Control

**Cuckooing:**
- **Definition**: Taking over someone's home for drug dealing
- **Targets**: Vulnerable people (addicts, elderly, disabled)
- **Methods**: Coercion, violence, deception
- **Duration**: Can be temporary or permanent
- **Impact**: Victims lose control of their homes

**Territorial Control:**
- **Monopolies**: Gangs establish monopolies in specific areas
- **Violence**: Used to maintain control and deter competition
- **Intimidation**: Local communities intimidated into silence
- **Branding**: Gang names and graffiti mark territory
- **Expansion**: Constant expansion into new territories

**Exploitation of Runners:**
- **Young People**: Often recruit children and teenagers
- **Vulnerable Adults**: Exploit addicts, homeless, mentally ill
- **Coercion**: Use violence and threats to control runners
- **Debt Bondage**: Create debts that cannot be repaid
- **Grooming**: Initially friendly, then exploitative

**Impact on Communities:**
- **Violence**: Increased violence in previously peaceful areas
- **Drug Availability**: Increased availability of hard drugs
- **Fear**: Communities living in fear
- **Children**: Children recruited and exploited
- **Vulnerable People**: Most vulnerable targeted for exploitation

**Law Enforcement Response:**
- **County Lines Programme**: UK government programme to combat county lines
- **National County Lines Coordination Centre**: Coordinated response
- **Disruption**: Focus on disrupting phone lines and networks
- **Safeguarding**: Focus on protecting exploited children
- **Arrests**: Thousands of arrests annually

**The Human Cost:**
- **Children**: Children exploited, traumatised, criminalised
- **Vulnerable Adults**: Most vulnerable targeted and abused
- **Communities**: Communities disrupted and frightened
- **Families**: Families devastated by exploitation
- **Deaths**: Deaths from violence and overdose
