# Chapter 240: Evaluating Legalisation

## Assessing the Outcomes of Legalisation

Evaluating legalisation outcomes is essential for evidence-based policy:

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- **Use Patterns**: Changes in drug use patterns
- **Youth Use**: Impact on youth drug use
- **Public Health**: Public health outcomes
- **Crime**: Changes in crime rates
- **Economic Impact**: Economic impacts of legalisation
- **Social Impact**: Social impacts on communities

**Cannabis Legalisation Outcomes:**
- **Use Rates**: Changes in cannabis use rates
- **Youth Use**: Impact on youth cannabis use
- **Traffic Safety**: Impact on traffic safety and impaired driving
- **Public Health**: Public health outcomes
- **Crime**: Changes in drug-related crime
- **Economic Impact**: Economic impacts including tax revenue

**Methodological Challenges:**
- **Causality**: Difficulty establishing causality
- **Time Lags**: Time lags between policy and outcomes
- **Confounding Factors**: Confounding factors affecting outcomes
- **Data Quality**: Data quality issues
- **Baseline Comparisons**: Challenges in baseline comparisons
- **International Comparisons**: Challenges in international comparisons
