# Chapter 252: Treatment Expansion

## Expanding Access to Drug Treatment

Expanding access to treatment is a key alternative to enforcement:

**Treatment Access Barriers:**
- **Cost**: High cost of treatment
- **Insurance**: Limited insurance coverage
- **Availability**: Limited availability of treatment slots
- **Geographic**: Geographic barriers to access
- **Stigma**: Stigma preventing treatment seeking
- **Wait Lists**: Long wait lists for treatment

**Expansion Strategies:**
- **Insurance Coverage**: Mandating insurance coverage for addiction treatment
- **Public Funding**: Increased public funding for treatment
- **Workforce Development**: Expanding treatment workforce
- **Telehealth**: Telehealth expansion for treatment access
- **Community-Based**: Community-based treatment programs
- **Integrated Care**: Integration with primary healthcare

**Medication-Assisted Treatment Expansion:**
- **MAT Access**: Expanding access to MAT
- **Primary Care**: MAT in primary care settings
- **Pharmacy Access**: Pharmacy-based MAT
- **Buprenorphine Expansion**: Expanding buprenorphine access
- **Methadone Expansion**: Expanding methadone access
- **Naltrexone Expansion**: Expanding naltrexone access

**Specialized Treatment:**
- **Adolescent Treatment**: Specialized treatment for adolescents
- **Women's Treatment**: Gender-responsive treatment for women
- **Cultural Competence**: Culturally competent treatment
- **Dual Diagnosis**: Treatment for co-occurring disorders
- **LGBTQ Treatment**: LGBTQ-affirming treatment
- **Veterans Treatment**: Specialized treatment for veterans

**Funding Models:**
- **Public Funding**: Public funding for treatment
- **Insurance Mandates**: Insurance coverage mandates
- **Medicaid Expansion**: Medicaid coverage for addiction treatment
- **Grants**: Grant funding for treatment programs
- **Private Funding**: Private funding for treatment
- **Cost-Effectiveness**: Cost-effectiveness of treatment investment

**The Treatment Expansion Reality:**
- **Essential**: Essential for addressing addiction
- **Underfunded**: Historically underfunded
- **Expanding**: Expanding access through policy changes
- **Evidence-Based**: Evidence-based treatment approaches
- **Cost-Effective**: Cost-effective compared to enforcement
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving intervention
