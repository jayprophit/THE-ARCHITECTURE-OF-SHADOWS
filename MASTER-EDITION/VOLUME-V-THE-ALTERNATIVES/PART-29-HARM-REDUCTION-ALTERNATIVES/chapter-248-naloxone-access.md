# Chapter 248: Naloxone Access

## Overdose Reversal Medication Access

Naloxone access is critical for preventing overdose deaths:

**Naloxone Overview:**
- **Mechanism**: Opioid receptor antagonist that reverses overdose
- **Rapid Action**: Rapid reversal of respiratory depression
- **Temporary Effect**: Temporary effect requiring follow-up care
- **Multiple Formulations**: Intranasal, intramuscular, and auto-injector formulations
- **Safety**: Safe with minimal side effects
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving medication

**Access Models:**
- **Pharmacy Access**: Over-the-counter pharmacy access
- **Standing Orders**: Standing orders for distribution
- **Community Programs**: Community distribution programs
- **Syringe Exchange**: Distribution through syringe exchange programs
- **Take-Home Naloxone**: Take-home naloxone for drug users and families
- **First Responder Carry**: First responders carrying naloxone

**Distribution Programs:**
- **Opioid Overdose Prevention Programs**: Community overdose prevention programs
- **Peer Distribution**: Distribution through peer networks
- **Family Distribution**: Distribution to family members of drug users
- **Outreach Programs**: Outreach distribution to active drug users
- **Training Programs**: Training on overdose recognition and response
- **Follow-Up**: Follow-up after naloxone distribution

**Legal Framework:**
- **Good Samaritan Laws**: Laws protecting overdose responders
- **Prescription Status**: Prescription vs. over-the-counter status
- **Liability Protection**: Liability protection for prescribers and distributors
- **Standing Order Authority**: Authority for standing orders
- **Pharmacy Regulations**: Pharmacy regulations for naloxone
- **Insurance Coverage**: Insurance coverage for naloxone

**Challenges:**
- **Stigma**: Stigma affecting naloxone carrying
- **Cost**: Cost of naloxone formulations
- **Access Barriers**: Barriers to access in some areas
- **Training**: Need for training on administration
- **Follow-Up Care**: Need for follow-up after administration
- **Sustainability**: Sustainability of distribution programs

**The Naloxone Access Reality:**
- **Life-Saving**: Life-saving intervention
- **Effective**: Highly effective at preventing overdose deaths
- **Expanding**: Expanding access globally
- **Evidence-Based**: Strong evidence base
- **Essential**: Essential component of overdose prevention
- **Need for Expansion**: Need for continued expansion of access
