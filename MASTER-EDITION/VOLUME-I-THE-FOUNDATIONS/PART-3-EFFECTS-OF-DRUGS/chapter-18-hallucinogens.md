# Chapter 18: Hallucinogens (Psychedelics)

## Effects, Risks, and Addiction Potential

Hallucinogens are substances that profoundly alter perception, thoughts, and feelings by affecting serotonin receptors in the brain.

**Common Hallucinogens:**
- **LSD (Acid)**: Synthetic, extremely potent, long duration (8-12 hours)
- **Magic Mushrooms (Psilocybin)**: Natural, shorter duration (4-6 hours)
- **DMT**: Short but intense experience (15-30 minutes)
- **Ayahuasca**: DMT-containing brew, longer duration (4-6 hours)

**Effects:**
- **Visual**: Hallucinations, colour enhancement, pattern perception
- **Cognitive**: Altered thinking, ego dissolution, mystical experiences
- **Emotional**: Intense emotions, ranging from euphoria to terror
- **Sensory**: Synesthesia (blending of senses), altered time perception

**Risks:**
- **Psychological**: Bad trips, anxiety, paranoia, psychosis in vulnerable individuals
- **Accidents**: Impaired judgment during intoxication
- **HPPD**: Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder (rare)
- **Flashbacks**: Re-experiencing drug effects without use (rare)

**Addiction Potential:**
- **Low**: Classic psychedelics are not physically addictive
- **Tolerance**: Rapid development, but resets quickly
- **Psychological**: Some users develop psychological dependence
- **No Withdrawal**: Physical withdrawal symptoms are absent

## The Renaissance in Psychedelic Research

After decades of prohibition, psychedelic research is experiencing a renaissance:
- **Clinical Trials**: Ongoing research for depression, PTSD, addiction
- **Regulatory Changes**: Some jurisdictions decriminalising or legalising
- **Therapeutic Potential**: Promising results for treatment-resistant conditions
- **Institutional Acceptance**: Growing acceptance in medical and scientific communities
- **Investment**: Significant commercial investment in psychedelic therapies
