# Chapter 24: How Drugs Affect the Body's Frequency and Senses

## Brain Wave Frequencies (Delta, Theta, Alpha, Beta, Gamma)

The brain operates at different frequencies corresponding to different states of consciousness:

**Brain Wave States:**
- **Delta (0.5-4 Hz)**: Deep sleep, unconscious
- **Theta (4-8 Hz)**: Deep relaxation, meditation, creativity
- **Alpha (8-13 Hz)**: Relaxed awareness, calm
- **Beta (13-30 Hz)**: Active thinking, focus, alertness
- **Gamma (30-100 Hz)**: Peak performance, heightened perception

**Drug Effects on Brain Waves:**
- **Stimulants**: Increase beta and gamma activity (heightened alertness)
- **Depressants**: Increase delta and theta activity (sedation)
- **Psychedelics**: Alter normal patterns, increase theta and gamma
- **Meditation**: Increases theta and alpha (similar to some drug states)

## How Drugs Alter Brain Wave Patterns

Different substances affect brain wave activity in characteristic ways:
- **Cocaine/Meth**: Increased beta activity, reduced alpha (hyper-arousal)
- **Alcohol/Benzodiazepines**: Increased theta, reduced beta (sedation)
- **LSD/Psilocybin**: Complex pattern changes, increased gamma (altered consciousness)
- **Ketamine**: Theta increase, disruption of normal rhythms (dissociation)
- **Cannabis**: Increased alpha, reduced beta (relaxed state)

## The Placebo Effect and Frequency

The placebo effect demonstrates the mind's power over the body:
- **Expectation**: Belief about drug effects influences actual experience
- **Brain Changes**: Placebo responses measurable in brain activity
- **Neurotransmitters**: Placebo can trigger endogenous opioid release
- **Frequency Entrainment**: Mind can influence body's energetic state
- **Implications**: Suggests consciousness affects physical reality

## How Drugs Affect the Senses

Drugs profoundly alter sensory perception:

**Visual Changes:**
- **Hallucinations**: Seeing things that aren't there
- **Enhanced Colours**: More vivid, saturated colours
- **Pattern Recognition**: Seeing patterns in random stimuli
- **Visual Distortions**: Changes in size, shape, movement
- **Synesthesia**: Blending of senses (e.g., seeing sounds)

**Auditory Changes:**
- **Enhanced Hearing**: Increased sensitivity to sound
- **Auditory Hallucinations**: Hearing voices or sounds
- **Music Appreciation**: Enhanced emotional response to music
- **Sound Distortion**: Changes in pitch, volume, quality

**Tactile Changes:**
- **Numbness**: Reduced sensation (opioids, dissociatives)
- **Enhanced Touch**: Increased sensitivity (MDMA, psychedelics)
- **Tactile Hallucinations**: Feeling things that aren't there
- **Temperature Changes**: Feeling hot or cold

**Olfactory and Gustatory Changes:**
- **Enhanced Smell/Taste**: Increased sensitivity
- **Distorted Smell/Taste**: Changes in perception
- **Hallucinated Smells/Tastes**: Sensing non-existent odours/flavours

**Proprioceptive Changes:**
- **Body Image Distortion**: Feeling larger, smaller, or disconnected from body
- **Loss of Coordination**: Impaired movement
- **Dissociation**: Feeling detached from body

## The Connection Between Frequency and Consciousness

The relationship between brain frequency and conscious experience:
- **Normal Consciousness**: Primarily beta waves during waking hours
- **Altered States**: Drugs can shift brain wave patterns
- **Meditative States**: Similar to some drug-induced states
- **Spiritual Experiences**: Often associated with theta/gamma activity
- **Therapeutic Potential**: Understanding frequency may help treat conditions
