# Chapter 22: Drug Quality, Shelf Life, and Mixing

## Shelf Life of Drugs

Different drugs have different shelf lives and storage requirements:

**Factors Affecting Shelf Life:**
- **Moisture**: Humidity degrades many substances
- **Light**: UV light breaks down compounds
- **Temperature**: Heat accelerates degradation
- **Air**: Oxidation reduces potency
- **Container**: Proper sealing extends shelf life

**Approximate Shelf Lives:**
- **Cannabis**: 1-2 years (properly stored)
- **Cocaine**: Indefinite (stable powder)
- **Heroin**: Several years (stable powder)
- **MDMA**: 2-3 years (degrades with heat/moisture)
- **LSD**: Indefinite (if kept cool, dark, dry)
- **Mushrooms**: 6-12 months (dried)

## Natural vs. Unnatural States

The form of drugs affects their properties:
- **Plant Material**: Whole plant, dried, or extracted
- **Powders**: Pure or cut substances
- **Crystals**: More potent, concentrated form
- **Liquids**: Solutions or suspensions
- **Pills/Tablets**: Standardised doses, often with binders

## Enhancing and Altering Drugs

Drugs are often modified from their natural state:
- **Extraction**: Isolating active compounds
- **Synthesis**: Creating artificial versions
- **Purification**: Removing impurities
- **Conversion**: Converting one form to another (e.g., morphine to heroin)
- **Cutting**: Diluting with other substances

## Mixing and Blending Practices

Common mixing practices in the drug trade:
- **Cutting**: Adding inert substances to increase volume
- **Adulteration**: Adding active substances to enhance or alter effects
- **Polysubstance Use**: Users combining multiple drugs
- **Unintentional Mixing**: Contamination during production or distribution

## Dangers of Adulteration

Adulteration poses significant risks:
- **Unknown Substances**: Users don't know what they're consuming
- **Variable Potency**: Inconsistent strength leads to overdose
- **Toxic Contaminants**: Dangerous cutting agents or byproducts
- **Allergic Reactions**: Unexpected responses to adulterants
- **Drug Interactions**: Unintended effects from combined substances

## How to Identify Quality

Methods for assessing drug quality:
- **Visual Inspection**: Colour, texture, appearance
- **Smell**: Characteristic odours
- **Taste**: (Not recommended - dangerous)
- **Drug Testing Kits**: Reagent tests for substance identification
- **Laboratory Analysis**: Most accurate, requires specialised equipment
- **Harm Reduction Services**: Some provide drug checking services
