# Chapter 19: Dissociatives

## Effects, Risks, and Addiction Potential

Dissociatives distort perceptions of sight and sound and produce feelings of detachment from the environment and self.

**Common Dissociatives:**
- **Ketamine (Special K)**: Anaesthetic used medically, recreationally for dissociative effects
- **PCP (Angel Dust)**: Originally developed as anaesthetic, now primarily recreational
- **Nitrous Oxide (Laughing Gas)**: Dental anaesthetic, recreational inhalant
- **DXM (Dextromethorphan)**: Cough suppressant, recreational at high doses

**Effects:**
- **Perceptual**: Distorted sensory perception, feeling of detachment from body
- **Cognitive**: Altered thinking, confusion, memory impairment
- **Physical**: Numbness, loss of coordination, slurred speech
- **Psychological**: Out-of-body experiences, dream-like states

**Risks:**
- **Physical**: Respiratory depression (especially with nitrous oxide)
- **Neurological**: Cognitive impairment with chronic use
- **Psychological**: Anxiety, panic, psychosis
- **Accidents**: Impaired coordination increases injury risk
- **Ketamine-Specific**: Bladder damage with chronic use (ketamine cystitis)

**Addiction Potential:**
- **Variable**: Ketamine has moderate addiction potential
- **Psychological**: Psychological dependence can develop
- **Tolerance**: Develops with regular use
- **Withdrawal**: Psychological symptoms (craving, anxiety)
