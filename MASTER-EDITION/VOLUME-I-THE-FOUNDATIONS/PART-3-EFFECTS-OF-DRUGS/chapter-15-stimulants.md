# Chapter 15: Stimulants (Uppers)

## Effects, Risks, and Addiction Potential

Stimulants increase alertness, attention, and energy by enhancing the effects of neurotransmitters dopamine and norepinephrine in the brain.

**Common Stimulants:**
- **Cocaine**: Rapid onset, intense euphoria, short duration
- **Methamphetamine**: Long-lasting, potent, neurotoxic
- **MDMA**: Empathogenic effects, serotonin release
- **Amphetamines**: Medical and recreational use (Adderall, speed)

**Effects:**
- **Physical**: Increased heart rate, blood pressure, body temperature, dilated pupils
- **Psychological**: Euphoria, confidence, energy, reduced appetite, insomnia
- **Cognitive**: Increased focus, alertness, talkativeness

**Risks:**
- **Cardiovascular**: Heart attack, stroke, arrhythmia
- **Neurological**: Seizures, cognitive impairment, psychosis
- **Psychiatric**: Anxiety, paranoia, aggression, addiction
- **Overdose**: Hyperthermia, cardiovascular collapse, death

**Addiction Potential:**
- **High**: Particularly for cocaine and methamphetamine
- **Mechanism**: Powerful dopamine release in reward pathways
- **Withdrawal**: Depression, fatigue, increased appetite, craving
- **Tolerance**: Rapid development, requiring higher doses for same effect
