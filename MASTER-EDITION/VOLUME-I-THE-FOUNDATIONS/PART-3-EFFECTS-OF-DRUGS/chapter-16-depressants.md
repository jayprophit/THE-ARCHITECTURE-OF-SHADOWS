# Chapter 16: Depressants (Downers)

## Effects, Risks, and Addiction Potential

Depressants slow down brain function by enhancing the effects of GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid), the primary inhibitory neurotransmitter.

**Common Depressants:**
- **Alcohol**: Most widely used depressant globally
- **Benzodiazepines**: Prescription medications (Valium, Xanax)
- **GHB**: Club drug and anaesthetic agent
- **Barbiturates**: Less common now, historically significant

**Effects:**
- **Physical**: Slowed breathing, reduced heart rate, muscle relaxation, slurred speech
- **Psychological**: Sedation, euphoria, reduced anxiety, disinhibition
- **Cognitive**: Impaired judgment, memory problems, confusion

**Risks:**
- **Respiratory Depression**: Potentially fatal, especially when combined with other depressants
- **Accidents**: Impaired coordination and judgment increase accident risk
- **Memory Loss**: Blackouts, especially with alcohol and benzodiazepines
- **Withdrawal**: Can be life-threatening (seizures, delirium tremens)

**Addiction Potential:**
- **High**: Particularly for benzodiazepines and alcohol
- **Mechanism**: GABA system adaptation leads to dependence
- **Withdrawal**: Medical detoxification often required
- **Tolerance**: Develops rapidly, requiring higher doses
