# Chapter 20: Synthetic Cannabinoids

## Effects, Risks, and Addiction Potential

Synthetic cannabinoids are laboratory-created chemicals designed to mimic THC, the active compound in cannabis.

**Common Synthetic Cannabinoids:**
- **Spice/K2**: Brand names for synthetic cannabinoid mixtures
- **JWH-018**: Early synthetic cannabinoid, now controlled
- **AB-FUBINACA**: Potent synthetic cannabinoid
- **5F-ADB**: Extremely potent synthetic cannabinoid
- **Hundreds of Variants**: Constantly evolving to avoid legal restrictions

**Effects:**
- **Similar to Cannabis**: Euphoria, relaxation, altered perception
- **More Intense**: Often more potent and unpredictable than natural cannabis
- **Variable**: Different compounds produce different effects
- **Unpredictable**: Users often don't know exactly what they're consuming

**Risks:**
- **Severe Health Effects**: Seizures, kidney damage, heart problems
- **Psychiatric**: Severe anxiety, psychosis, hallucinations
- **Overdose**: More dangerous than natural cannabis
- **Unknown Long-Term Effects**: Limited research on many compounds
- **Contamination**: Often contaminated with other substances

**Addiction Potential:**
- **Moderate to High**: Can be psychologically addictive
- **Withdrawal**: Irritability, anxiety, insomnia, craving
- **Tolerance**: Develops rapidly
