# Chapter 12: The Chemistry of Cutting Agents

## Bulking Agents (Baking Soda, Lactose, Paracetamol)

Cutting agents are substances used to dilute drugs:
- **Purpose**: Increase volume, stretch supply, increase profits
- **Common Agents**:
  - **Inert Fillers**: Lactose, mannitol, inositol
  - **Active Cutters**: Paracetamol, caffeine, lidocaine
  - **Chemical Modifiers**: Baking soda (for crack conversion)
- **Selection Criteria**: Similar appearance, similar melting point, low cost
- **Safety Concerns**: Unknown interactions, allergic reactions, toxicity

## Dilution Methods

Various techniques for cutting drugs:
- **Physical Mixing**: Blending powders together
- **Solution Mixing**: Dissolving and recrystallising
- **Layering**: Creating layers of pure and cut product
- **Spraying**: Liquid cutting agents sprayed onto plant material
- **Micro-dosing**: Distributing potent substances throughout large quantities

## The Risk of Hotspots

Uneven mixing creates dangerous concentrations:
- **Definition**: Areas of unexpectedly high drug concentration
- **Causes**: Inadequate mixing, settling during transport
- **Consequences**: Overdose deaths from "hot" doses
- **Prevention**: Proper mixing techniques, quality control
- **Detection**: Variable potency in different parts of the same batch
