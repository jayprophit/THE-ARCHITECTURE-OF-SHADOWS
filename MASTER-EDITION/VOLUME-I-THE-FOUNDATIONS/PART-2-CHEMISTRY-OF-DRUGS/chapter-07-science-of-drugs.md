# Chapter 7: The Science of Drugs

## How Drugs Work on the Brain

Drugs affect the brain by interfering with neurotransmitter systems:
- **Neurotransmitters**: Chemical messengers that transmit signals between neurons
- **Receptors**: Proteins on neurons that respond to specific neurotransmitters
- **Reuptake**: Process by which neurotransmitters are recycled
- **Mechanisms**: Drugs can mimic neurotransmitters, block reuptake, or alter receptor sensitivity

## Addiction and the Reward System

The brain's reward system plays a central role in addiction:
- **Dopamine**: Key neurotransmitter in reward and pleasure
- **Mesolimbic Pathway**: Brain circuit activated by rewarding stimuli
- **Tolerance**: Brain adapts to repeated drug exposure, requiring higher doses
- **Withdrawal**: Physical and psychological symptoms when drug use stops
- **Craving**: Intense desire for drug triggered by cues or stress

## Tolerance and Withdrawal

**Tolerance Development:**
- **Cellular Adaptation**: Neurons adjust to constant drug presence
- **Downregulation**: Reduced receptor sensitivity
- **Metabolic Tolerance**: Faster drug metabolism
- **Behavioural Tolerance**: Learned adaptation to drug effects

**Withdrawal Symptoms:**
- **Physical**: Tremors, sweating, nausea, pain, seizures
- **Psychological**: Anxiety, depression, irritability, insomnia
- **Duration**: Varies by drug, from days to weeks
- **Severity**: Ranges from uncomfortable to life-threatening
