# Chapter 9: Synthetic Drugs (Man-Made)

## Amphetamines (Speed)

Amphetamines are synthetic stimulants:
- **Chemical Structure**: Phenethylamine class
- **Medical Uses**: ADHD treatment, narcolepsy
- **Recreational Use**: "Speed" or "whizz"
- **Effects**: Increased energy, alertness, confidence
- **Risks**: Cardiovascular strain, psychosis, addiction

## Methamphetamine (Crystal Meth)

Methamphetamine is a more potent amphetamine derivative:
- **Production**: Simple synthesis from pseudoephedrine
- **Forms**: Crystal meth, powder, pills
- **Effects**: Intense euphoria, energy, libido
- **Duration**: 8-12 hours
- **Risks**: Severe dental problems ("meth mouth"), skin sores, neurotoxicity, extreme addiction

## MDMA (Ecstasy, Molly)

MDMA is a synthetic empathogen:
- **Chemical Name**: 3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine
- **Forms**: Pills (ecstasy), powder/crystal (molly)
- **Effects**: Euphoria, emotional openness, increased sociability
- **Therapeutic Potential**: PTSD treatment, couples therapy
- **Risks**: Dehydration, hyperthermia, serotonin depletion, adulteration

## Fentanyl and Its Analogues

Fentanyl is a synthetic opioid of extraordinary potency:
- **Potency**: 50-100 times stronger than heroin
- **Medical Uses**: Surgical anaesthesia, chronic pain
- **Illicit Use**: Often mixed with heroin or sold as fake prescription pills
- **Risks**: Extreme overdose risk, respiratory depression, rapid death
- **Analogues**: Carfentanil (elephant tranquiliser), acetylfentanyl, furanylfentanyl

## Nitazenes (Emerging Synthetic Opioids)

Nitazenes are a new class of synthetic opioids:
- **Development**: Designed to circumvent drug laws
- **Potency**: Similar to or greater than fentanyl
- **Variants**: Isotonitazene, metonitazene, etonitazene
- **Risks**: High overdose potential, unknown long-term effects
- **Legal Status**: Rapidly being scheduled internationally

## Orphines (New Generation Synthetic Opioids)

Orphines are the latest synthetic opioid threat:
- **Chemical Class**: Benzimidazol-2-ones
- **Examples**: Cychlorphine, DFNZ
- **Potency**: Variable, some extremely potent
- **Detection**: New, challenging to identify in standard drug screens
- **Emergence**: Following China's 2025 nitazene ban

## Other Synthetics (Bath Salts, Mephedrone)

- **Bath Salts**: Cathinones like MDPV, mephedrone
- **Effects**: Stimulant and empathogenic properties
- **Risks**: Paranoia, aggression, cardiovascular problems
- **Legal Status**: Many are now controlled substances
