# Chapter 13: The Geography and Cultivation of Drug Plants

## Coca: Cultivation and Identification

**Erythroxylum coca** cultivation:
- **Geographic Range**: Andes Mountains (Colombia, Peru, Bolivia)
- **Altitude**: 500-2,000 metres above sea level
- **Climate**: Tropical, consistent rainfall, moderate temperatures
- **Identification**: Glossy green leaves, small white flowers, red berries
- **Cultivation Cycle**: 12-18 months to maturity, multiple harvests per year
- **Yield**: Approximately 1-2 kg of dried leaves per 100 square metres

## Opium Poppy: Cultivation and Identification

**Papaver somniferum** cultivation:
- **Geographic Range**: Afghanistan, Myanmar, Mexico, Golden Triangle
- **Climate**: Temperate to subtropical, well-drained soil
- **Identification**: Large flowers (white, pink, purple), distinctive seed pod
- **Harvest**: Latex collected from seed pods when scored
- **Yield**: 5-15 kg opium per hectare
- **Processing**: Opium refined to morphine, then heroin

## Cannabis: Cultivation and Identification

**Cannabis sativa/indica** cultivation:
- **Geographic Range**: Global, both indoor and outdoor
- **Climate**: Varied, from tropical to temperate
- **Identification**: Distinctive leaf shape (5-7 leaflets), resinous buds
- **Indoor Cultivation**: Controlled environment, hydroponics common
- **Outdoor Cultivation**: Seasonal, dependent on climate
- **Yield**: 100-500 grams per plant (varies by strain and method)

## Psilocybin Mushrooms: Habitat and Identification

**Psilocybe** species:
- **Geographic Range**: Global, diverse habitats
- **Common Species**: Psilocybe cubensis, Psilocybe semilanceata
- **Habitat**: Damp environments, grasslands, forests, dung
- **Identification**: Brown caps, purple-brown spore prints, bruising blue
- **Season**: Autumn in temperate regions, year-round in tropics
- **Cultivation**: Possible but requires sterile conditions

## Khat: Cultivation and Identification

**Catha edulis** cultivation:
- **Geographic Range**: Ethiopia, Kenya, Yemen, Somalia
- **Climate**: High altitude, arid to semi-arid
- **Identification**: Evergreen shrub, small leaves, white flowers
- **Harvest**: Fresh leaves chewed for stimulant effect
- **Potency**: Declines rapidly after harvest (within 48 hours)

## Kava: Cultivation and Identification

**Piper methysticum** cultivation:
- **Geographic Range**: Pacific Islands (Fiji, Vanuatu, Samoa, Tonga)
- **Climate**: Tropical, high rainfall, well-drained soil
- **Identification**: Shrub, heart-shaped leaves, knotted root
- **Harvest**: Roots harvested at 3-5 years
- **Preparation**: Ground and mixed with water to make traditional drink

## The "Terroir" Concept — Environmental Factors Influencing Drug Quality

Similar to wine, drug plants exhibit terroir:
- **Soil Composition**: Mineral content affects alkaloid production
- **Climate**: Temperature, rainfall, humidity influence potency
- **Altitude**: Affects cannabinoid and alkaloid profiles
- **Genetics**: Strain varieties produce different effects
- **Cultivation Methods**: Organic vs. synthetic nutrients affect final product
- **Regional Reputation**: Certain regions known for superior quality (e.g., Colombian cocaine, Afghan heroin)
