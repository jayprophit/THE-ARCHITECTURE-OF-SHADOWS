# Chapter 3: The Global Scale of the Drug Trade

## Statistics and Trends (UNODC World Drug Report 2026)

According to the UNODC World Drug Report 2026:
- Approximately 331 million people used drugs globally in 2024
- This represents 6.2% of the global population aged 15-64
- Cannabis remains the most commonly used drug worldwide
- Opioids continue to cause the most harm
- New psychoactive substances (NPS) pose emerging threats

## Regional Differences in Production and Consumption

**Production Regions:**
- **Cocaine**: Colombia, Peru, Bolivia
- **Heroin**: Afghanistan, Myanmar, Mexico
- **Cannabis**: Global cultivation, major producers include Morocco, Afghanistan, Mexico
- **Synthetic Drugs**: China, Southeast Asia, Europe, North America

**Consumption Patterns:**
- **North America**: High opioid and stimulant use
- **Europe**: Cocaine and MDMA prevalence
- **Asia**: Diverse patterns from traditional opium use to synthetic drugs
- **Africa**: Emerging markets for various substances
- **Oceania**: High methamphetamine use in some regions

## The Evolution of the Drug Trade Over Time

The drug trade has evolved from localised traditional use to a globalised industrial operation:
- **Pre-20th Century**: Regional trade, plant-based substances
- **Early 20th Century**: Beginning of international regulation
- **Mid-20th Century**: Rise of organised trafficking networks
- **Late 20th Century**: Globalisation and synthetic drugs
- **21st Century**: Digital markets, AI-designed substances, and complex supply chains
