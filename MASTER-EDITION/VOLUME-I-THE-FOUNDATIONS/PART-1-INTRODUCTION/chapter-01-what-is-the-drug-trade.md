# Chapter 1: What Is the Drug Trade?

## Definitions and Scope

The drug trade encompasses the global production, distribution, and sale of illegal psychoactive substances. It operates as a shadow economy parallel to legal markets, with its own supply chains, pricing structures, and enforcement mechanisms.

## The Scale of the Global Drug Trade

The global drug trade is a multi-billion dollar industry affecting every continent. According to UNODC estimates, approximately 331 million people used drugs in 2024, representing 6.2% of the global population aged 15-64.

## The Drug Trade as a Shadow Economy

Operating outside legal frameworks, the drug trade functions as a parallel economic system with its own rules, currencies, and power structures. It intersects with legitimate economies through money laundering, corruption, and the exploitation of legal businesses.

## Key Terminology and Concepts

Understanding the drug trade requires familiarity with specific terminology:
- **Supply Chain**: The network from production to consumption
- **Trafficking**: International transportation of illegal drugs
- **Distribution**: Local sale and delivery systems
- **Cutting**: Diluting drugs with other substances
- **Street Level**: Direct sales to end users
- **Wholesale**: Bulk sales between dealers
