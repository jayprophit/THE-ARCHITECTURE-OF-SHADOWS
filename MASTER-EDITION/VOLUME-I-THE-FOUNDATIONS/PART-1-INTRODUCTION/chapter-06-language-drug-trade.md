# Chapter 6: The Language of the Drug Trade

## Slang and Terminology (A-Z)

The drug trade has developed its own extensive vocabulary:

**Common Terms:**
- **Blow**: Cocaine
- **Smack/H**: Heroin
- **Weed/Pot/Marijuana**: Cannabis
- **Molly/E**: MDMA/Ecstasy
- **Crystal/Ice**: Methamphetamine
- **Special K**: Ketamine
- **Acid**: LSD
- **Tabs**: LSD doses
- **Bags/Wraps**: Street-level drug packages
- **8-Ball**: 3.5 grams (eighth of an ounce)
- **Ounce/Zip**: 28 grams
- **Kilo/Key**: 1,000 grams
- **Cooking**: Manufacturing synthetic drugs
- **Cutting**: Diluting drugs with other substances
- **Hotspot**: Concentrated area of high-purity drug in a mixture

## How Language Evolves in the Trade

Drug terminology constantly evolves:
- **Law Enforcement Adaptation**: New terms emerge to avoid detection
- **Regional Variation**: Different slang develops in different areas
- **Cultural Influence**: Music and media spread new terminology
- **Internet Influence**: Dark web and social media create new terms
- **Generational Changes**: Each generation develops its own drug vocabulary

## The Importance of Linguistic Codes

The specialized language of the drug trade serves several functions:
- **Secrecy**: Codes prevent outsiders from understanding conversations
- **Identity**: Language signals membership in drug subcultures
- **Safety**: Euphemisms reduce legal risk in communications
- **Business**: Standardised terms facilitate transactions
- **Law Enforcement**: Police must learn these codes to investigate effectively
