# Chapter 254: Media Overview

## How Media Portrays Drugs and Drug Policy

Media representation significantly shapes public perception of drugs:

**Media Types:**
- **News Media**: News coverage of drug issues
- **Entertainment Media**: Movies and television depicting drugs
- **Social Media**: Social media content about drugs
- **Advertising**: Advertising related to drugs
- **Documentary**: Documentary coverage of drug issues
- **Online Media**: Online media and blogs

**News Media Coverage:**
- **Sensationalism**: Sensationalist coverage of drug issues
- **Crime Focus**: Focus on drug-related crime
- **Moral Panic**: Moral panic framing of drug issues
- **Individual Stories**: Individual stories of addiction and recovery
- **Policy Coverage**: Coverage of drug policy debates
- **Expert Sources**: Use of expert sources in coverage

**Entertainment Media:**
- **Glamorisation**: Glamorisation of drug use
- **Dramatisation**: Dramatic portrayal of drug addiction
- **Stereotypes**: Stereotypical portrayals of drug users
- **Consequences**: Depiction of drug consequences
- **Recovery Stories**: Stories of recovery and redemption
- **Cultural Impact**: Cultural impact of entertainment portrayals

**Social Media:**
- **User-Generated Content**: User-generated content about drugs
- **Influencers**: Influencers discussing drug use
- **Misinformation**: Misinformation about drugs on social media
- **Community Building**: Online communities around drug use
- **Peer Influence**: Peer influence through social media
- **Drug Marketing**: Drug marketing through social media

**The Media Reality:**
- **Powerful**: Powerful influence on public perception
- **Varied**: Varied portrayals across media types
- **Sensationalist**: Often sensationalist coverage
- **Stereotypical**: Stereotypical portrayals common
- **Evolving**: Evolving media representations
- **Responsibility**: Media responsibility in accurate portrayal
